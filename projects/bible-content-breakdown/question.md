# What Is the Bible Actually About?

**Hypothesis:** Churches teach the Bible is ~50% sexual morality, ~25% generosity/tithing, and ~25% "be a good person." The actual text — measured verse by verse — is overwhelmingly genealogy, geography, census data, construction specs, and historical narrative, with sexual behavior rules comprising less than 1% of the total content.

**Method:** Categorize every verse in the Bible (NET translation) by its primary function — what the verse is *doing*, not what sermons interpret it to mean. Additionally, identify story boundaries and tag each story with weighted moral themes from a fixed list.

**Two layers of analysis:**
1. **Verse-level function** — What is this verse doing? (12 categories, one per verse)
2. **Story-level morals** — What is this story teaching? (15 themes, 1-3 weighted tags per story)

**Graph:** Interactive pie chart (verse functions) + stacked bar chart by book (moral themes shifting from OT to NT). Filterable by book, testament, book group.

**Bible version:** NET (New English Translation). Free JSON API, verse-level markup, permissive license. The NRSVue is the academic gold standard but has no public API and restrictive licensing.
