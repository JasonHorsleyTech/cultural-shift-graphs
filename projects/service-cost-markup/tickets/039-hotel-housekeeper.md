# Hotel Housekeeper — Lodging

The lowest-paid fundamental worker against one of the higher-priced services. A hotel room in a major city costs $200-400/night. The housekeeper who cleans it makes $12-16/hour and spends maybe 30 minutes per room. That's $6-8 of labor on a $300 room. 2-3% ratio.

**Fundamental worker:** Hotel housekeeper / room attendant
**Customer cost proxy:** Average daily room rate (STR/CoStar tracks this industry data going back decades). Or average hotel spending per night from consumer expenditure surveys.

**Interesting angles:**
- Hotels cut housekeeping during COVID (no daily cleaning). Room rates didn't drop. The "savings" from eliminating the fundamental worker went straight to profit.
- Luxury hotel rates have exploded. Has any of that reached the housekeeper? (Almost certainly not.)
- Airbnb created a new competitor where the "housekeeper" is the homeowner or a cleaning service. Different cost structure entirely.
- Resort fees — pure overhead extraction added to the bill that has nothing to do with the service worker.
