# Result: Employer-Sponsored Health Insurance Coverage Rate, 1960–2024

## Source

  - AHRQ Medical Expenditure Panel Survey (MEPS), Statistical Brief #543, for private-sector employee enrollment rates 2008–2021.
- **Date accessed:** 2026-04-30

## Methodology
- **Definition:** The percentage of the total U.S. population (all ages) covered by employment-based health insurance for some or all of the calendar year, as reported by the Census Bureau's CPS ASEC.
- **Universe (main table):** Total civilian noninstitutionalized population. Includes workers, dependents, retirees, and children covered through an employed family member's plan.
- **Pre-1987 estimates:** Based on academic analyses of earlier CPS data and BLS surveys; different methodology from the published CPS ASEC series. Low confidence; included for historical context only.
- **Known methodology changes:**
  - **~1994:** CPS ASEC added more specific health insurance questions, resulting in an apparent jump in measured coverage from 1993 to 1994 (~57.1% → 60.9%). This is largely a measurement artifact, not a real coverage increase.
  - **1999:** Addition of a verification question in the CPS ASEC further improved measurement. The 1999 estimate with verification is 63.3%; without it, 62.8%.
  - **2014 (for 2013 data):** Major redesign of the CPS ASEC health insurance questions. Estimates for 2013 onward are based on the new questionnaire and are not directly comparable to the 1999–2012 series. The Census Bureau considers this a significant break.
  - **2018:** Improvements to CPS ASEC processing system.

## Gaps and Caveats

### Missing years
- **2007 and 2008 (total population):** Could not confirm exact percentages from primary Census CPS ASEC source. The values lie between 2006 (59.7%) and 2009 (56.1%); the recession that began December 2007 caused significant ESI erosion. Contextual evidence suggests ~59% for 2007 and ~57–58% for 2008 but these are not confirmed.
- **2013 (total population):** This is a methodology break year. The CPS ASEC questionnaire was redesigned for calendar year 2013 data. The Census Bureau explicitly warns against comparing pre-2013 and post-2013 estimates. The redesigned estimates for 2013 were not readily available in published summaries; the under-65 rate was approximately 56%.
- **1960–1986:** No consistent annual survey data. Pre-1987 estimates are derived from academic analyses and BLS bulletins with varying methodologies.

### Methodology breaks
- The three methodology breaks (1994, 1999, 2014) make long-run comparisons challenging. The 1993→1994 jump of 3.8 percentage points is almost entirely a measurement artifact. The 1999 verification question produced a smaller but noticeable effect. The 2014 redesign is the most significant break for modern analysis.
- When using this data for the "share of the pie" graph, the most reliable continuous stretch is **1999–2012** (verification question era) or **2013–2024** (redesigned questionnaire era). Bridging across the 2014 break requires acknowledging the discontinuity.

### Key trends
- ESI coverage peaked around **2000 at 63.6%** of total population (or ~69% of non-elderly) and has been in secular decline since.
- The decline accelerated during the 2001 and 2007–2009 recessions.
- The ACA (2014) did NOT reverse the ESI decline — it expanded coverage through Medicaid and the individual marketplaces, not through employer plans.
- By 2024, ESI covers about **53.8%** of total population, a roughly 10 percentage point decline from the 2000 peak.
- Among private-sector employees specifically, enrollment rates fell from ~54% in 2008 to ~48% by 2021 (MEPS data), reflecting both fewer employers offering coverage and more employees declining available coverage due to cost.

### Interaction with Ticket 018
- Ticket 018 covers employer health insurance **cost** per covered worker. This ticket covers the **coverage rate**. Together they tell the full compensation story: costs per worker have risen dramatically while the share of workers actually covered has declined — a double squeeze.

### Confidence assessment
- **1987–2005:** High confidence (direct Census CPS ASEC published series, consistent methodology within the 1994–1998 and 1999–2005 sub-periods).
- **2006, 2009–2012:** High confidence (confirmed from individual Census P60 reports and press releases).
- **2014–2024:** High confidence (post-redesign CPS ASEC series, confirmed from individual P60 reports).
- **2007–2008:** Low confidence (could not confirm from primary sources; marked as missing).
- **2013:** Low confidence (methodology break year; marked as missing).
- **Pre-1987:** Low confidence (varying methodologies, patchy data, approximate estimates).
- **1993→1994 transition:** The apparent jump is a measurement artifact; medium confidence in individual values on either side.
