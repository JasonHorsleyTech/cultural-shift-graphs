# Ticket 022: Seat Belts → Crash Survival

## Objective

Research the timeline from scientific/engineering evidence that seat belts reduce crash fatalities to binding regulation mandating seat belt use.

## What to research

1. **Early evidence date** — Crash safety research in the 1950s established the effectiveness of seat belts. Hugh DeHaven's crash survivability research (1940s) was foundational. Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory crash testing. When did controlled studies or engineering data first demonstrate that seat belts save lives?

2. **Consensus date** — When did a major body formally recommend mandatory seat belt use? SAE standards? Medical association endorsements? Government reports?

3. **First OECD regulation** — Two types of seat belt regulation: (a) vehicle equipment mandates (cars must HAVE seat belts) and (b) usage mandates (people must WEAR seat belts). Australia's state of Victoria was the first jurisdiction to mandate seat belt WEARING (1970). Which OECD country had the first national usage mandate?

4. **US regulation** — Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard 208 (1968) required seat belts in new cars (equipment mandate). New York was the first state to require seat belt USE (1984). Federal law requires states to have seat belt laws but doesn't set a national mandate. Document both equipment and usage timelines.

5. **Industry opposition** — The auto industry resisted BOTH equipment and usage mandates initially. Lee Iacocca at Ford resisted even installing seat belts. Later the industry shifted — it was cheaper to add belts than to redesign crash structures. Moderate opposition, shorter duration than tobacco/lead.

6. **50% exposure reduction** — US seat belt use rate rose from ~14% (1983, before state laws) to ~90% (2020s). NHTSA data tracks usage rates annually.

## Where to look

1. NHTSA regulatory history for FMVSS 208
2. Hugh DeHaven's research (Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory, 1940s-1950s)
3. Victoria, Australia — Road Safety Act 1970 (first seat belt wearing law)
4. Ralph Nader, "Unsafe at Any Speed" (1965) — catalyzed auto safety regulation
5. NHTSA seat belt usage rate data

## What "good data" looks like

- Clear distinction between equipment mandates (cars must have seat belts) and USAGE mandates (people must wear them). The usage mandate is the "action" metric — that's what changes behavior.
- Australia 1970 as first OECD usage mandate. Confirm this.
- The 14% → 90% usage rate transformation as dramatic exposure reduction data
- Industry opposition was SHORT-LIVED compared to tobacco — note the contrast

## Known pitfalls

- "Regulation" for seat belts has TWO distinct milestones: installation requirement (US 1968) and wearing requirement (US states 1984+, Australia 1970). Use the WEARING mandate as the regulation date — installation without usage doesn't reduce fatalities.
- Category: behavioral-mandate (mandates individual behavior).
- The auto safety movement (Nader, federal hearings) was a political/advocacy catalyst, similar to MADD for drunk driving. Note in judgment calls.
