# Defensive Cultural Legislation Timeline

Countries passing laws to protect their culture from American influence is itself the strongest evidence that the pressure exists. Track every significant piece of legislation worldwide that was enacted specifically to resist American/Anglo cultural dominance — language laws, content quotas, tech regulations, trade restrictions on cultural goods.

**Metric:** Cumulative count of defensive cultural legislation enacted globally, by decade. Timeline of major legislative actions by country.

**Data proxy:** UNESCO Convention on Cultural Diversity (2005) ratification data, national cultural policy databases, WTO "cultural exception" negotiation records, EU audiovisual directives, individual country regulatory archives.

**Interesting angles:**
- Compile a comprehensive timeline:
  - UK Cinematograph Films Act (1927) — screen quotas
  - France: screen quotas (1946), Toubon Law (1994), digital services tax (2019)
  - Canada: CRTC Canadian content rules (1971), magazine protection (1999)
  - South Korea: screen quotas (various years), reduced under US pressure (2006)
  - EU: Television Without Frontiers (1989), AVMS Directive (2018), Digital Services Act (2022)
  - China: Great Firewall (1997+), film import quotas, tech company restrictions
  - Russia: data localization laws, LinkedIn ban (2016), social media restrictions
  - India: various internet regulation proposals
- The trend line itself is the story: is the count of defensive laws increasing over time (suggesting growing pressure) or decreasing (suggesting capitulation)?
- UNESCO Convention on the Protection and Promotion of the Diversity of Cultural Expressions (2005): 151 parties. The US refused to ratify.
