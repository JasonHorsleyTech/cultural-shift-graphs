# Hollywood Market Share in European Cinema

Europe had thriving national film industries — French, German, Italian, British, Scandinavian — that were systematically overwhelmed by Hollywood. The European case is especially interesting because these were wealthy countries with strong cultural traditions and, in many cases, active government protection of domestic cinema. Hollywood won anyway.

**Metric:** US-produced films' share of cinema admissions/box office in major European markets (France, Germany, UK, Italy, Spain), per decade.

**Data proxy:** European Audiovisual Observatory (LUMIERE database), national film institutes (CNC for France, FFA for Germany, BFI for UK), UNESCO film statistics.

**Interesting angles:**
- UK: Closest cultural/linguistic cousin. Track whether US share is higher there than on the continent.
- France: Most aggressive protector of domestic cinema (screen quotas since 1946, CNC subsidies). Does protection actually work? Compare French domestic market share vs. Germany's (less protected).
- Italy: Had a golden age (1940s-60s neorealism, spaghetti westerns). Track the collapse of Italian cinema's domestic share.
- Post-EU expansion: Did Eastern European markets immediately fill with Hollywood after the Iron Curtain fell?
- The Blum-Byrnes Agreement (1946): France literally negotiated Hollywood import quotas as part of post-WW2 debt restructuring. The US used financial leverage to force open the French film market.
