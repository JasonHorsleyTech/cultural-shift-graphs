# Geographic Survival Lottery

A heat map of the US showing estimated survival probability by county for a given collapse scenario.

## Why it's interesting

The apocalyptic-population-dropoff project revealed that national average survival curves mask enormous geographic variation. Manhattan (70,000 people/sq mi, zero food production) vs. rural Iowa (15 people/sq mi, surrounded by cropland) are different planets in a grid-down scenario. Survival probability varies by orders of magnitude based on location.

This is the graph that actually answers "should I move to Montana?"

## Possible factors per county
- Population density
- Arable land per capita
- Water access (wells vs. municipal)
- Distance from military installations
- Climate (growing season length, winter severity)
- Existing agricultural output
- Gun ownership rates (proxy for self-defense capacity and/or violence risk)
