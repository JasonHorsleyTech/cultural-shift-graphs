# McDonald's and American Fast Food Global Expansion

McDonald's has 40,000+ restaurants in 100+ countries. The "McDonald's Index" (Big Mac Index) is literally used by The Economist as a purchasing power indicator. American fast food chains (McDonald's, KFC, Burger King, Subway, Starbucks, Pizza Hut) have reshaped eating habits worldwide.

**Metric:** Cumulative number of countries with McDonald's presence, by year. Secondary: total international McDonald's locations over time. Tertiary: other major US chains (KFC, Starbucks) for comparison.

**Data proxy:** McDonald's Corporation annual reports, franchise disclosure documents, business press coverage of market entries, QSR Magazine international data.

**Interesting angles:**
- First international: Canada (1967), then rapid expansion. Track year of entry for each major market.
- Japan (1971): became McDonald's largest international market. Japanese McDonald's adapted the menu but the brand is unmistakably American.
- Soviet Union (1990): the Pushkin Square McDonald's opening was one of the most symbolic moments of the Cold War's end. 30,000 people lined up.
- "McDonaldization" (George Ritzer, 1993): an academic coined a term for American commercial-cultural homogenization. The concept itself entered academic discourse.
- Anti-McDonald's movements: José Bové (France) demolished a McDonald's under construction (1999). Track protests and resistance as evidence of cultural pressure.
- Starbucks expansion timeline as a second data series — coffee culture transformation globally.
- Track KFC in China specifically — it arrived before McDonald's and has more locations there. American fast food through a different brand.
