# American Tech Platform Market Share Globally

Google, Facebook/Meta, YouTube, Instagram, WhatsApp, Amazon, Apple, Microsoft, Twitter/X — American tech platforms mediate daily life for billions of people worldwide. In most countries, the top search engine, social network, video platform, messaging app, and smartphone OS are all American. Local competitors only survive where governments actively blocked the American ones (China's Great Firewall, Russia's VKontakte).

**Metric:** Market share of American tech platforms (Google search, Facebook/Instagram, YouTube, iPhone/Android, Amazon) in major non-US markets, by year (2005-present).

**Data proxy:** StatCounter (search engine share by country), DataReportal (social media usage by country), Statista, company annual reports, App Annie/data.ai (app rankings by country).

**Interesting angles:**
- Google search: >90% market share in most countries. Exceptions: China (Baidu, because Google was blocked), Russia (Yandex, ~60%), South Korea (Naver, ~60%). The exceptions prove the rule.
- Facebook: dominant social network in most of the world. Exceptions: China (WeChat), Russia (VKontakte), Japan (LINE). But even in exception countries, Instagram (Meta-owned) is often top 3.
- YouTube: no meaningful local competitor in most markets worldwide.
- Android (Google) + iOS (Apple): together control ~99% of global smartphone OS. Every phone on Earth runs American software.
- Amazon: dominant e-commerce in US, UK, Germany, but not everywhere (Mercado Libre in Latin America, Alibaba in China). Track where American e-commerce wins vs. doesn't.
- The "splinternet" question: is American tech dominance fracturing (China, Russia, EU regulation) or consolidating?
