# Ticket 018: Smoking Cessation — QALY Evidence and Cost

## Objective

Research the cost-effectiveness of smoking cessation interventions for Bob's profile. This wasn't in the original question.md list but is critical: 25-30% of adults in the bottom income quartile smoke (vs. ~10% in the top quartile). For the ~30% of Bobs who smoke, quitting may be the single most impactful intervention.

## What to research

### QALY Evidence
1. **Cost-effectiveness of smoking cessation** — this is one of the most studied CEA topics in medicine. Find the canonical estimates.
2. **QALY gain per year from quitting** — varies enormously by age and pack-years. What's the estimate for a 35-45 year old with 10-20 pack-years?
3. **By method:**
   - Nicotine replacement therapy (NRT): patch, gum, lozenge
   - Varenicline (Chantix) — most effective single-agent
   - Brief counseling (5-10 min from a doctor)
   - Quitline (free phone counseling) + NRT
   - Combination: NRT + counseling
4. **Long-term mortality reduction** — the QALY gains from smoking cessation compound over decades

### Cost Analysis
1. **NRT costs:**
   - Nicotine patches: $30-60/month for 8-12 weeks
   - Gum/lozenges: $40-60/month
   - Many state quitlines provide FREE NRT
2. **Varenicline (generic):**
   - Generic chantix: $50-150/month for 12-week course
   - With insurance: copay
3. **Counseling:**
   - State quitline: FREE (1-800-QUIT-NOW)
   - Brief physician counseling: included in checkup
4. **Total cost for one quit attempt**
5. **Success rates per attempt** (15-25% for NRT + counseling, lower for cold turkey)
6. **Expected cost per successful quit** (accounting for multiple attempts)

### Access and Barriers
1. **State quitlines** — free, phone-based, available everywhere. Very low barrier.
2. **NRT availability** — OTC, no prescription needed for patches/gum
3. **Nicotine addiction difficulty** — high relapse rate, multiple attempts typical
4. **Stress as trigger** — Bob's life is more stressful; stress is a major relapse trigger

## Where to look

1. **Tufts CEA Registry** — "smoking cessation" (extensive literature)
2. **USPSTF** — smoking cessation recommendation with supporting CEA
3. **CDC** — smoking prevalence by income, quitline data
4. **Cochrane Reviews** — "NRT for smoking cessation," "Varenicline for smoking cessation"
5. **Key studies:**
   - Cromwell et al. — AHRQ smoking cessation cost-effectiveness
   - Various $/QALY estimates: smoking cessation is typically $1,000-5,000/QALY (extremely cost-effective)
6. **1-800-QUIT-NOW / smokefree.gov** — free resource details

## Output format

Use the full intervention result format from plan.md. Note in the summary that this only applies to the ~30% of Bobs who smoke — for non-smoking Bobs, this intervention doesn't exist.

## What "good data" looks like

- QALY estimates that are annualized (not lifetime total)
- Cost per successful quit, not per quit attempt (big difference)
- Evidence quality should be Tier 1 — this is one of the best-studied interventions in medicine
- Include the "it's free" pathway (quitline + free NRT)
