# English-Language Share of National Music Charts

What percentage of top-charting songs in non-English-speaking countries are in English? This measures how much American/Anglo music dominates the actual listening habits of populations worldwide. France literally passed a law (Toubon Law, 1994) requiring radio stations to play at least 40% French-language music — because without it, English would dominate.

**Metric:** Percentage of top 10/20/40 chart positions occupied by English-language songs in non-English-speaking countries, per decade.

**Data proxy:** National music charts (France: SNEP, Germany: GfK, Japan: Oricon, Brazil: ABPD, South Korea: Gaon/Circle), Billboard international chart data, IFPI global music reports.

**Interesting angles:**
- Pick 5-6 representative countries across regions: France, Germany, Japan, Brazil, South Korea, Sweden.
- France with the Toubon Law (1994): compare English-language chart share before and after. Did legislation actually work?
- Sweden/Netherlands: high English proficiency countries — do they have higher English music chart shares?
- South Korea: K-pop's rise reversed the trend locally AND globally. When did English-language share start declining in Korean charts?
- Japan: J-pop maintained domestic dominance longer than most countries. Track the trajectory.
- Latin America: reggaeton and Latin pop have created a counter-wave. Is English-language share declining in Spanish-speaking markets?
- Streaming era (2010s+): has Spotify/Apple Music homogenized or diversified listening habits?
