# Exotic Dancer / Stripper — Adult Entertainment

One of the few professions where the worker is classified as an independent contractor, pays the VENUE to work (stage fees of $50-200/night), and earns primarily from direct tips. The power dynamic is inverted compared to most industries — the venue extracts money FROM the worker.

**Fundamental worker:** Exotic dancer / stripper
**Customer cost proxy:** Total customer spending per visit (cover charge + drinks + tips + VIP room fees). Or average revenue per dancer per shift if available.

**Interesting angles:**
- The dancer pays house fees, tip-outs to DJs and bouncers, and is classified as a 1099 contractor (no benefits, no minimum wage guarantee). Multiple lawsuits have challenged this classification.
- The customer's dollar splits: cover charge → venue. Drinks → venue. Tips → dancer (minus tip-outs). VIP room → split. The dancer might see 30-50% of total customer spend despite being the entire product.
- Online platforms (OnlyFans) disrupted this model — the performer keeps 80% and controls the platform. Has OnlyFans changed strip club economics?
