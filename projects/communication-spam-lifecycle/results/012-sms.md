# Result: SMS / Text Messages

## Summary

SMS is the clearest illustration of the cost-as-spam-barrier thesis in the entire dataset. From 2000 to roughly 2008, per-message fees of $0.10-0.20 made mass unsolicited texting economically nonviable -- the same dynamic that protected telegrams a century earlier. During this window, spam texts were essentially nonexistent. The shift began around 2008-2010 as unlimited texting plans became standard for consumers, and accelerated dramatically when mass-texting platforms (Twilio, EZTexting, etc.) dropped the cost of sending bulk SMS to fractions of a cent. By 2018, political campaigns discovered peer-to-peer texting loopholes and sent hundreds of millions of unsolicited texts. By 2022, RoboKiller estimated 225.7 billion spam texts were sent to Americans -- a 157% increase over 2021's 87.8 billion.

Despite the spam surge, SMS has not yet reached the >50% spam crossover that email hit. With total US carrier messaging volume holding steady around 2-2.2 trillion messages per year, spam texts represent roughly 10-11% of total traffic as of 2022-2025. This is partly because SMS still carries enormous volumes of legitimate person-to-person and business notification traffic, and partly because carrier-level filtering (introduced seriously only around 2021-2023) and 10DLC registration requirements have provided some friction against spam growth. The FCC adopted its first rules specifically targeting scam texts in 2023, and Apple's adoption of RCS in iOS 18 (2024) is beginning to shift the messaging ecosystem.

The pricing history is remarkable on its own: carriers charged consumers $0.10-0.20 per text for a message that cost approximately $0.0003-0.0005 in infrastructure -- a markup of roughly 20,000-60,000%. This was one of the most profitable products in telecom history. They were accidentally building a spam barrier as a side effect of price gouging. When unlimited plans destroyed the per-message price signal, they also destroyed the spam barrier.

### The Accidental Spam Barrier

The pricing story deserves its own section. Carriers charged consumers $0.10-0.20 per text for a message that cost approximately **$0.0003-0.0005** in infrastructure -- a markup of **20,000-60,000%**. This was one of the most profitable products in telecom history. They were accidentally building a spam barrier as a side effect of price gouging. When unlimited plans destroyed the per-message price signal around 2008-2010, they also destroyed the spam barrier.

### Regulations

| Year | Law | What it did |
|------|-----|-------------|
| 1991 | TCPA | Originally targeted telemarketing calls; later reinterpreted to cover texts. Requires prior express consent. Penalties $500-$1,500 per violation. |
| 2003 | CAN-SPAM Act | Primarily email, but provisions interpreted to cover commercial texts to wireless devices. |
| 2019 | TRACED Act | Expanded FCC authority to $10,000 penalties per illegal text. Extended statute of limitations from 1 to 4 years. |
| 2023 | FCC First Scam Text Rules | First rules *specifically* targeting scam texts. Carriers must block texts from Do-Not-Originate numbers and invalid/unallocated numbers. |
| 2024 | FCC Enhanced Text Blocking | Carriers must block specific numbers on FCC notification. Extended Do-Not-Call protections to texts. 1:1 consent rules effective Jan 2025. |

### Defenses

| Year | Defense | How it worked |
|------|---------|---------------|
| 2010 | Short code verification | Carriers required businesses to register and vet 5-6 digit short codes for commercial messaging. |
| 2018 | iOS "Report Junk" | Apple added spam reporting and filtering for texts from unknown senders, routing suspected junk to a separate folder. |
| 2021 | 10DLC registration | Businesses must register 10-digit long codes for A2P messaging. Unregistered senders throttled or blocked. Brand trust scores determine throughput. |
| 2021 | STIR/SHAKEN | Caller ID authentication framework extended to messaging. Carriers must certify in Robocall Mitigation Database. |
| 2022 | Carrier-level spam filtering | ML-based SMS filtering similar to email spam filters. Messages scanned for keywords, patterns, and sender behavior before delivery. |
| 2024 | RCS on iPhone | Apple added RCS in iOS 18 with built-in sender verification and verified business badges. 1B RCS messages daily in the US by May 2025. |

### Timeline Highlights

- **2000-2008 -- The price wall holds.** At $0.10-0.20 per text (with carriers charging for both sending *and* receiving), mass unsolicited texting was economically impossible. Spam was literally zero. Carriers raised prices from $0.10 to $0.20 even as volume tripled -- peak price gouging that doubled as perfect spam defense.
- **2008-2010 -- Unlimited plans destroy the barrier.** Per-message cost to consumers drops to effectively $0. Volume hits 2 trillion texts/year. The spam barrier evaporates, but it takes spammers a few years to notice.
- **2018 -- Political texting exploits the loophole.** Democratic campaigns sent 350M texts via peer-to-peer platforms (Hustle, GetThru) -- 6x over 2016-2017. P2P texting was technically "person-initiated," exempt from TCPA autodialer restrictions. The RNC allocated $3M extra for P2P texts in the final two weeks before midterms. This normalized unsolicited texting at scale.
- **2020 -- Spam texts overtake spam calls.** COVID drove a surge in scam texts (vaccine schemes, government impersonation). Political texting hit ~3 billion for the election cycle. Average user now getting 14.7 spam texts per month, up from 6.3 in 2015.
- **2022 -- The explosion: 225.7 billion spam texts.** A 157% increase over 2021's 87.8 billion. Financial losses from SMS fraud estimated at $20.6 billion. The FCC scrambled to develop its first text-specific spam rules.
- **2025 -- Holding at ~11%.** Carrier filtering, 10DLC enforcement, and FCC rules have stabilized spam around 10-11% of total traffic. Still far below email's historic 90%+ peak -- but the fight is ongoing and AI-generated scam texts are getting harder to catch.

## Confidence Level

**Overall: MEDIUM-HIGH**

- **Message volumes**: HIGH confidence. CTIA publishes official carrier-reported data annually. The trajectory (12B in 2000 → 1T in 2008 → 2.3T peak in 2011 → ~2T+ stable since) is well-documented.
- **Spam volumes and percentages**: MEDIUM confidence. RoboKiller and Truecaller are the primary sources, but their methodologies involve extrapolation from their user base. The 225.7B spam text figure for 2022 is widely cited but not independently verified by carriers or FCC. Pre-2020 spam percentages are estimates based on per-user spam rates extrapolated to the population.
- **User cost per message**: HIGH confidence. Carrier pricing is well-documented. The $0.10-0.20 per-text era and transition to unlimited plans (~2008-2010) are widely reported.
- **Infrastructure cost**: MEDIUM confidence. The $0.0003-0.0005 figure is consistent across multiple analyses but actual carrier costs are proprietary. The order of magnitude (fractions of a cent) is not disputed.
- **User adoption shares**: MEDIUM confidence. Pew Research provides excellent data points for certain years, but "primary communication channel" requires interpretation and estimation between survey years.
- **Spam crossover**: HIGH confidence that it has NOT occurred yet. Even at ~225B spam texts, total carrier messaging volume of ~2T+ means spam is ~10-11% of traffic -- well below 50%.
