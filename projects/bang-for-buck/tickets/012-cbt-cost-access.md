# Ticket 012: CBT — Cost and Access Barriers for Bob

## Objective

Determine the realistic annual cost of CBT for Bob, including all realistic access scenarios from cheapest to most common.

## What to research

1. **In-person CBT costs**
   - Private practice therapist: per-session rate without insurance ($100-250)
   - Standard course: 12-20 sessions
   - Sliding scale availability and typical rates for $30k income
   - Community mental health center rates

2. **Lower-cost alternatives**
   - Group CBT programs — per-session and total course cost
   - University training clinics (supervised graduate students) — $10-30/session
   - FQHC / community health center behavioral health — sliding scale
   - Telehealth CBT platforms (BetterHelp, etc.) — $60-100/week

3. **Digital / self-guided CBT**
   - App-based CBT (Woebot, MoodGYM) — free to $15/month
   - Computerized CBT (cCBT) — do the QALY estimates from ticket 011 apply?
   - Bibliotherapy / workbook-based CBT ($15-25 one-time)

4. **Access barriers**
   - Therapist shortage in Bob's areas — waitlist times for low-cost options
   - Transportation to appointments (12-20 trips)
   - Time off work for appointments (if hourly/shift work)
   - Cultural stigma, health literacy, emotional readiness
   - Internet/device access for telehealth

5. **Time commitment**
   - Session time + travel: per appointment and total
   - Homework between sessions: hours per week
   - Total hours for a full course
   - Annualized (including maintenance sessions if recommended)

6. **Total annual cost scenarios**
   - Best case: Digital CBT or university clinic
   - Mid case: Sliding scale community provider + telehealth
   - Expensive case: Private practice without insurance
   - Amortization: if a 16-session course costs $X and benefits last 3 years, annual cost = $X/3

## Where to look

1. **Psychology Today** — therapist directory with rate filters
2. **Open Path Collective** — low-cost therapy network rates
3. **SAMHSA** — behavioral health treatment locator, access barriers
4. **BetterHelp / Talkspace** — published pricing
5. **NIMH** — psychotherapy access statistics
6. **HRSA** — FQHC behavioral health services

## Output format

Use the intervention result format from plan.md, focusing on cost and access. Reference ticket 011 for QALY data. Produce the complete "Data for Graph" table.

## What "good data" looks like

- Multiple cost scenarios, with the "realistic for Bob" scenario clearly identified
- Amortized annual cost accounting for lasting benefits (3-year amortization per plan.md)
- Honest assessment of whether Bob can actually GET CBT, not just what it costs in theory
