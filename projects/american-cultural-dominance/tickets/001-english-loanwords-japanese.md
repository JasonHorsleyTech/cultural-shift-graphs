# English Loanwords in Japanese

One of the deepest data points in the project. Japanese absorbed waves of foreign loanwords — Dutch and Portuguese dominated pre-1850s, then English exploded after the Meiji Restoration (1868). Modern Japanese is saturated with katakana English (コンピューター, テレビ, etc.). The adoption rate per decade tells the story of American cultural pressure before Hollywood even existed.

**Metric:** Number of new English-origin loanwords entering standard Japanese dictionaries per decade.

**Data proxy:** Japanese linguistic corpora and loanword dictionaries. The 国語辞典 (Kokugo Jiten) series and academic studies on gairaigo (外来語) adoption rates are the primary sources.

**Interesting angles:**
- Pre-Meiji loanwords were mostly Dutch (science, medicine) and Portuguese (religion, trade). English overtook both post-1868.
- Post-WW2 American occupation created a massive spike — thousands of English words entered daily Japanese.
- Technology waves (TV, computing, internet, smartphones) each brought fresh floods.
- Japan has an official government committee (国語審議会) that periodically attempts to limit English loanword use. These policy responses are data points themselves.
- Track the shift: pre-1945 loanwords were often British English; post-1945 overwhelmingly American English.
