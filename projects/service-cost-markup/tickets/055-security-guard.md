# Security Guard — Private Security

The security guard is often the lowest-paid person at the most expensive building. A guard making $15/hour is billed out by the security company at $25-35/hour. The spread is the security company's profit + insurance + management.

**Fundamental worker:** Security guard (unarmed, general post)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average billing rate per guard-hour charged to the client (the business hiring the security company).

**Interesting angles:**
- The private security industry is larger than public policing in terms of total personnel. There are roughly 3x as many private security guards as police officers.
- G4S, Allied Universal, Securitas — a few companies dominate and the guard turnover rate is enormous (100-300%/year). The guards are disposable; the contracts are valuable.
- Armed vs. unarmed: armed guards bill at 2-3x but the guard's pay premium is much smaller. The liability insurance premium goes to the company.
- Technology (cameras, access control) was supposed to reduce the need for guards. Has it? Or has it just changed what guards do?
