# Dumb Solutions

Problems with obvious, known, implementable solutions that sound too dumb or upsetting to suggest seriously. The graph quantifies wasted human energy/money spent on NOT doing the obvious thing.

## The pattern

- Solution is known and implementable today
- Doesn't require changing human behavior
- Doesn't create perverse government incentives
- Not politically charged (liberals AND conservatives equally flabbergasted)
- Reason it hasn't been done: someone profits from the broken system, or the solution sounds too stupid to champion

## Examples so far

**Plastic recycling** — The solution: put it in a hole. Plastic is inert, doesn't smell, doesn't leach from lined landfills. Recycling plastic was invented by plastic manufacturers to make people feel better. The material degrades when reshaped and virgin plastic is essentially free (ethane byproduct of oil extraction). Quantify: entire plastics recycling industry labor, municipal recycling program costs, sorting facility costs, shipping-to-China costs.

**Gerrymandering** — The solution: make it a math problem. Algorithmic redistricting (shortest splitline, compactness) has been solved for decades. The people who draw maps are the people who benefit from drawing maps. Quantify: estimated 5% of local politics time spent on redistricting, multiply by salaries.

**Drug addiction (opioids)** — The solution: give them slower, more boring opioids forever. Methadone/suboxone maintenance therapy has 60-70% success vs sub-20% for abstinence-based rehab. Methadone is cheap. Rehab centers charge $30k-$80k for 28-day programs that don't work. Quantify: entire abstinence-based rehab industry spend vs. what maintenance therapy would cost for the same population.

**Daylight saving time** — The solution: stop. Causes measurable spikes in car accidents, heart attacks, workplace injuries. Every poll shows public agrees. Nobody spends political capital on it. Quantify: annual cost of the health/accident spike plus productivity loss from the transition.

**Pennies** — The solution: stop making them. Cost 2.7 cents to make, worth 1 cent. US Mint loses ~$200M/year. Canada, Australia, NZ eliminated theirs with zero problems. Jason's theory: the real reason is that eliminating denominations is a visible reminder of currency deflation, and governments don't want that conversation. Quantify: direct minting losses plus transaction time costs (counting/handling pennies).

**Politician pay / corruption** — The solution: make lobbying illegal, pay politicians 3-4x more. Singapore pays ministers $1M+, has lowest corruption rates on earth. Current low pay selects for fame-seekers or people not smart enough to run companies. The $900K bribe that passes a billion-dollar bad law (ethanol mandate) is the cost model. Quantify: politician salary increase cost vs. estimated cost of lobbied legislation (ethanol subsidies, etc.).

**Student loans** — The solution: stop guaranteeing unlimited loans to 18-year-olds. College prices tripled because money was unlimited. Stop the loans, colleges lower prices overnight. University lobby keeps it going. Quantify: total student debt outstanding + tuition inflation above baseline.

**Robocalls** — The solution: hard cap on unique outbound numbers per month (~300). Telecoms got paid per call regardless of spam. STIR/SHAKEN finally mandated in 2021 and it's working. Could've been done 20 years earlier. Quantify: estimated productivity loss from spam calls over the decades it went unsolved.

## Graph concept

Two bars per problem. Left bar: cost of the dumb solution. Right bar: what we actually spend instead. The gap is the stupidity tax. Visual should be devastating — tiny left bars next to enormous right bars, repeating.
