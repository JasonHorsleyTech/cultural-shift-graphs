# K-12 Public School Teacher — Primary/Secondary Education

Interesting comparison to the college professor. Public schools are funded by property taxes, not tuition. The "customer" pays indirectly. Does that change the ratio dynamics?

**Fundamental worker:** Public school teacher (K-12, average across all grades)
**Customer cost proxy:** Total per-pupil spending (NCES tracks this back to the 1960s at least). This includes everything — teacher salary, administration, buildings, buses, lunch programs.

**Interesting angles:**
- Teacher salaries have been famously stagnant in real terms. But has per-pupil spending gone up? If so, the ratio is collapsing — more money in the system but not reaching teachers.
- Special education mandates, administrative requirements (No Child Left Behind, etc.) — did specific policy changes create inflection points?
- Compare across states if possible — states with strong unions vs. right-to-work states.
