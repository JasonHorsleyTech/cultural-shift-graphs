# Plumber — Plumbing Services

A plumber shows up at your house with a van full of tools and fixes your pipes. The service call might be $200-500. How much of that is the plumber's pay?

**Fundamental worker:** Plumber (journeyman / master)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average cost of a plumbing service call, or average annual household spending on plumbing services.

**Interesting angles:**
- Plumbing has a strong union tradition and apprenticeship system. Has that protected the ratio compared to non-union trades?
- The "emergency premium" — a plumber at 2 AM costs 2-3x a daytime call. Does the plumber see the premium, or does the dispatch service capture it?
- Trades in general are supposed to be the "honest" industries where the worker keeps a fair share. Is that actually true, or is it a myth?
