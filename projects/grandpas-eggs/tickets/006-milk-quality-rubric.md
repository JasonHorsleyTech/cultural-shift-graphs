# Milk — Quality Rubric

## What to research

Define what a "gallon of milk" meant in ~1955 and what the quality-equivalent product is today. Build a multi-attribute quality rubric.

## Research tasks

1. **1955 baseline:** What was the standard milk product? Whole milk, delivered or store-bought? Pasteurized but not ultra-pasteurized? From grass-fed cows on smaller farms? How was it produced, processed, and distributed?

2. **Quality attributes to score (minimum 5):**
   - Fat content and processing (whole vs. skim, homogenization)
   - Cow diet (pasture/grass-fed vs. grain-fed vs. mixed)
   - Hormone use (rBST/rBGH — introduced 1994)
   - Antibiotic use in dairy herds
   - Pasteurization method (standard vs. ultra-pasteurized vs. raw)
   - Freshness/supply chain (days from farm to shelf)
   - Herd size / farming system

3. **Modern quality-equivalent:** Is it organic whole milk? Grass-fed whole milk? A specific brand like Organic Valley or Strauss? Raw milk (if legally available)?

4. **Divergence timeline:** When did milk quality tiers emerge? Key dates: homogenization becoming standard (1950s-60s), the shift from whole to reduced-fat as default (1980s), rBST introduction (1994), organic milk entering mainstream retail (late 1990s-2000s).

## Data source hierarchy

1. USDA historical dairy production reports
2. Academic studies on dairy farming practices by era
3. FDA/USDA regulations on pasteurization, hormones, antibiotics
4. Industry sources (National Dairy Council, dairy cooperatives)

## Output format

Follow the **Type 2: Quality rubric** format from plan.md.

## What "good data" looks like

- Honest about the fact that 1955 milk had genuine food safety disadvantages (shorter shelf life, bacterial risk)
- Distinguishes between changes that were quality degradation (hormones, confinement) and changes that were improvement (food safety, consistency)
- Addresses the whole-milk-to-skim shift as a separate story from quality degradation
