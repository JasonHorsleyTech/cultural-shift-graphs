# Surgeon — Healthcare

The highest-skill, highest-stakes medical worker. Interesting because surgeons are among the highest-paid workers in any industry, yet the total cost of surgery has still managed to outpace their compensation.

**Fundamental worker:** Surgeon (general surgery or a common specialty like orthopedic)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average cost of a common surgical procedure (appendectomy, knee replacement, C-section — pick one that has consistent data going back decades). Or use total surgical spending per capita if that's findable.

**Interesting angles:**
- Surgeon compensation is high in absolute terms but what percentage of a $50,000 knee replacement actually goes to the surgeon? It might be shockingly small.
- Operating room time costs ~$30-60/minute in overhead. How much of that is the surgeon vs. the room?
- Has the ratio changed with the rise of surgical centers vs. hospitals?
