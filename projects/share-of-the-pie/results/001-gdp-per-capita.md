# Result: GDP Per Capita (Nominal), 1947–2024

## Source
- **Primary source:** FRED series A939RC0A052NBEA — "Gross domestic product per capita," Bureau of Economic Analysis, NIPA Table 7.1 Line 1. https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/A939RC0A052NBEA
- **Cross-validation:** World Bank API (NY.GDP.PCAP.CD) for 1960–2024; values agree within 0.5% for most years, diverging ~1–2% in recent years due to population denominator differences.
- **Date accessed:** 2026-04-28

## Methodology
- **Definition:** Nominal (current-dollar) gross domestic product divided by midyear resident population. This is BEA's official per capita GDP series.
- **Universe:** Total US resident population (includes armed forces stationed in the US, excludes those abroad in early vintages; current methodology includes all residents).
- **GDP concept:** Market value of all final goods and services produced within the US in a given year, before adjusting for inflation or depreciation.
- **Known methodology changes:** BEA conducts comprehensive revisions roughly every 5 years (most recent: 2023 comprehensive update). These can revise historical values slightly. The data below reflects the latest available vintage as of April 2026.
- **Note on ticket source ID:** The ticket cited FRED series A792RC0A052NBEA, which is actually "Personal income per capita," not GDP per capita. The correct series for nominal GDP per capita is **A939RC0A052NBEA**.

## Gaps and Caveats
- **No missing data.** All 78 years (1947–2024) have values from the BEA series.
- **2024 is preliminary.** The BEA 2024 annual figure reflects the advance/second estimate and will be revised in subsequent annual and comprehensive updates. FRED also shows a 2025 value ($89,962), which is even more preliminary and excluded from this table.
- **Nominal dips are real.** GDP per capita declined in nominal terms in 1949, 1954, 1958, 2009, and 2020 — all correspond to NBER recession years. This is expected and correct.
- **Values are rounded to whole dollars** as published by FRED/BEA.
- **Revision vintage:** Data reflects BEA's latest revision as of April 2026. Historical values may differ slightly from older publications or other sources using earlier revision vintages.
- **Confidence: High** across the full 1947–2024 range. GDP per capita is one of the most authoritative and consistently measured macroeconomic statistics. The BEA series has no methodology breaks within this time span.
