# Ticket 001: Bob's Physical Health Baseline

## Objective

Establish the physical health profile of an American at the 25th percentile of household income (~$30,000). This is a reference document — all intervention tickets will use it to assess population match and relevance.

## What to research

For adults in the bottom quartile (or closest available bracket) of US household income, find:

1. **BMI distribution** — obesity prevalence (BMI ≥30), severe obesity (BMI ≥40), overweight (BMI 25-30)
2. **Chronic conditions** — prevalence of type 2 diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, chronic pain, arthritis
3. **Physical activity levels** — % meeting CDC guidelines (150 min/wk moderate activity), % completely sedentary
4. **Diet patterns** — fast food frequency, fruit/vegetable intake, cooking at home frequency
5. **Mobility** — any mobility limitations, difficulty with daily activities
6. **Smoking status** — current smoker %, former smoker %
7. **Healthcare utilization** — frequency of doctor visits, preventive care uptake, ER vs primary care usage

## Where to look

1. **CDC BRFSS** (Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System) — sliceable by income bracket, most metrics available
2. **NHANES** (National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey) — objective measurements (BMI, blood pressure, lab work) by income
3. **CDC MMWR reports** — published analyses of health disparities by income
4. **Kaiser Family Foundation** — health status and access data by income

## Output format

Use the baseline profile format from plan.md. Key statistics table with metric, value, and source citation for each finding.

## What "good data" looks like

- Statistics specifically for the bottom income quartile (or <$25k / $25-35k brackets)
- Recent data (2018-2024 preferred, 2015+ acceptable)
- National-level data (not state-specific)
- Actual prevalence numbers, not just "higher than average"
