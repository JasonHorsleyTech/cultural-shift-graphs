# College Professor — Higher Education

The poster child for this entire project. A professor reads from a book and draws on a whiteboard. The inputs are cheap. The output costs $40,000/year. Where does the money go?

**Fundamental worker:** Full-time college professor (all ranks — track full professor, associate, and assistant separately if data allows)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average tuition + fees per student per year (NCES has this going back decades). Use published tuition, not net-after-aid — we want the sticker price because that's what the institution charges.

**Interesting angles:**
- The adjunctification of higher education: ~75% of college teaching is now done by adjuncts making $3,000-5,000 per course. The "professor" line might be misleading if the actual teaching is done by contingent labor. Consider tracking both tenure-track and adjunct.
- Administrative bloat: administrator-to-student ratios have roughly doubled since 1990 while professor-to-student ratios have barely changed.
- AAUP has salary data going back to the 1970s. NCES has tuition data going back further.
- This should be one of the most dramatic collapses on the graph.
