# Primary Care Physician — Healthcare

The doctor you go see when you're sick. The most fundamental healthcare worker.

**Fundamental worker:** Primary care physician / general practitioner
**Customer cost proxy:** This is the hard one. Individual doctor visit copays are meaningless because insurance obscures the real cost. Consider total national health expenditure per capita (CMS tracks this back to the 1960s) as the denominator. Or find average total cost per primary care visit (including what insurance pays). Pick whichever gives you the longest, most consistent time series.

**Interesting angles:**
- Pre-insurance era (before ~1940s) vs. post — did the ratio collapse when third-party payment entered?
- The rise of administrative staff in medicine: in 1970 there were roughly equal doctors and administrators. Now administrators outnumber doctors ~10:1.
- How does the GP's share compare across decades as healthcare spending exploded?
