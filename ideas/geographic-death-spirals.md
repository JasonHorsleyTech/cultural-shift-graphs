# Geographic death spirals

When a company town or single-industry region loses its industry, what's the cascade? Map property values, population, school enrollment, crime, addiction metrics for places like Flint MI, outport Newfoundland, coal country West Virginia.

## Why it's interesting
The displaced-professions project tracks what happens to *people* when a profession dies. This tracks what happens to *places*. The data is different (Census, Zillow, FBI UCR, CDC WONDER) and the story is different (community collapse vs. individual displacement). Could be a powerful companion graph.

## Where it came from
Spin-off from displaced-professions exploration (Pass 2). The Newfoundland cod moratorium case showed 30-50% population loss in outport communities — the geographic dimension was as dramatic as the employment dimension.
