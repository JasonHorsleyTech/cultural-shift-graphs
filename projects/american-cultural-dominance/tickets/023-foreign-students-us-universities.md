# Foreign Students at American Universities

When the world's ambitious young people choose to study in the US, they absorb American culture, language, professional norms, and social expectations — then bring them home. The foreign student pipeline is one of the most powerful long-term Americanization vectors. Many of these students become leaders in their home countries.

**Metric:** Total number of international students enrolled at US universities, by decade (1940s-present). Secondary: top sending countries over time.

**Data proxy:** Institute of International Education (IIE) "Open Doors" reports (published annually since 1954, with historical data back to 1948), OECD education statistics, UNESCO student mobility data.

**Interesting angles:**
- Post-WW2: GI Bill era coincided with US universities becoming the world's most prestigious. Marshall Plan scholars came to the US.
- Cold War: US actively recruited foreign students as a soft power strategy (Fulbright program, 1946).
- 1950s-80s: steady growth, primarily from Asia and Europe.
- 1990s-2000s: explosive growth from China and India. By 2020, Chinese and Indian students comprised ~50% of all international enrollments.
- Post-9/11 dip: visa restrictions briefly reduced enrollment. Recovery came quickly.
- US share of global international students: has it declined as UK, Australia, Canada compete? Track US share vs. total global mobility.
- Return rate: what percentage of foreign-educated students return home vs. stay in the US? Both outcomes spread American culture.
