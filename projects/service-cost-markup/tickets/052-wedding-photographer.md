# Wedding Photographer — Wedding Industry

The wedding industry is infamous for price inflation. Tell a vendor it's for a wedding and the price doubles. A photographer who charges $500 for a corporate headshot session charges $3,000-8,000 for a wedding. The work is different (longer day, higher stakes, more editing), but is it 6-16x different?

**Fundamental worker:** Wedding photographer
**Customer cost proxy:** Average cost of wedding photography. The Knot and WeddingWire publish annual surveys. Or use total average wedding cost and isolate the photography line item.

**Interesting angles:**
- The "wedding tax" — how much of the photographer's premium over non-wedding work is the photographer capturing vs. how much is going to venues, coordinators, and platforms (The Knot charges vendors for leads)?
- Digital photography eliminated film and processing costs. The equipment got cheaper. Did wedding photography get cheaper? (No.)
- Social media pressure has pushed couples toward more elaborate photography (engagement shoots, drone shots, same-day edits). Has scope creep increased the photographer's real hourly rate or just their total hours?
- Compare to a portrait photographer or commercial photographer doing similar skilled work without the "wedding" label.
