# Ticket 004: Pre-Industrial US Agricultural Carrying Capacity

## What to research

What population could the continental US support with pre-industrial agriculture — and what does that number actually depend on?

## Specific data needed

1. **Historical US population and food production data:**
   - 1800, 1850, 1900, 1920, 1940 — population, farmland acreage, crop yields per acre, caloric output
   - Was the US at carrying capacity at any of these points, or still expanding?
   - Food imports vs. exports at each period

2. **What "pre-industrial" actually means:**
   - Pre-synthetic fertilizer (Haber-Bosch, ~1913): What were yields without it?
   - Pre-mechanization: Yields with draft animals vs. hand labor only
   - Pre-hybrid seeds: Heritage/open-pollinated yields vs. modern hybrids
   - Pre-irrigation (electric pumps): Rainfed-only arable acreage

3. **Draft animal requirements:**
   - How many acres can one horse/mule work?
   - Current US draft animal population (~300K) vs. 1900 (~21M)
   - Time to rebuild draft animal herds from current stock

4. **Arable land:**
   - Total US arable land (currently ~900M acres in farmland)
   - How much is arable without irrigation? Without fertilizer?
   - How much has been degraded by modern monoculture (topsoil loss)?

5. **Caloric yield per acre by crop:**
   - Potatoes, corn, wheat, rice, beans — calories per acre with and without modern inputs
   - Best crops for hand-labor subsistence farming

## Where to look

1. **USDA Historical Statistics** — agricultural census data back to 1840
2. **Smil, Vaclav — "Enriching the Earth" (2001)** — Haber-Bosch impact on carrying capacity
3. **FAO historical yield data**
4. **Pimentel, David** — energy and agriculture studies
5. **USDA NRCS** — soil survey, arable land classification
6. **Historical demography** — US Census historical population data

## Result format

Key Data Points table with entries for:
- Carrying capacity estimates at different technology levels (hand labor only, draft animals, pre-fertilizer mechanized)
- Historical population-to-farmland ratios
- Caloric yields per acre by crop and technology level

## What good data looks like

- Multiple independent estimates that converge (or diverge with explanation)
- Clear distinction between "could theoretically feed" and "historically did feed"
- The range should be wide: 30-150M depending on assumptions. If it's narrower, explain what constrains it
- Accounts for the Haber-Bosch discontinuity — synthetic fertilizer roughly doubled yields. Without it, carrying capacity drops dramatically
