# Concert Musician / Performer — Live Music

The Ticketmaster story. Live concert revenue has exploded since the early 2000s as recorded music revenue collapsed (streaming pays fractions of a cent). But how much of your $150 concert ticket reaches the performer?

**Fundamental worker:** Musician / performing artist
**Customer cost proxy:** Average concert ticket price (Pollstar has industry data going back to the 1990s; earlier data exists from trade publications).

**Interesting angles:**
- Ticketmaster/Live Nation merged in 2010, creating a vertical monopoly (venue + ticketing + promotion). Service fees now add 30-50% on top of face value. None of that goes to the artist.
- "Dynamic pricing" — Ticketmaster's surge pricing for popular shows. The price goes up but the artist's guarantee doesn't change. Pure middleman capture.
- Compare arena acts vs. club/bar bands. A bar band might keep 100% of a $10 cover. A stadium act keeps maybe 15% of a $200 ticket after venue, promoter, management, agents, and fees.
- Streaming killed album revenue but boosted touring as the primary income source. Did this shift improve or worsen the live performance ratio?
