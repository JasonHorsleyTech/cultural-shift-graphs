# AP/UPI News Wire Global Dominance

The Associated Press (founded 1846) and United Press International became the backbone of global news reporting. When a newspaper in Kenya or Thailand publishes a world news story, chances are it originated from an American wire service. This means American framing, American editorial judgment, and American news values shape how the world understands events.

**Metric:** AP/UPI share of international news content in foreign newspapers/broadcasts, by decade (1900-present). Secondary: number of countries served by AP vs. Reuters vs. AFP vs. TASS.

**Data proxy:** UNESCO "Many Voices, One World" report (MacBride Commission, 1980), academic studies on international news flows, AP corporate history, Reuters/AFP comparative studies.

**Interesting angles:**
- Early 20th century: Reuters (British) dominated global news. AP was primarily domestic. Track when AP went global.
- Post-WW2: US wire services expanded massively. UPI at its peak served 7,000+ clients in 100+ countries.
- "New World Information and Communication Order" (NWICO) debate at UNESCO (1970s-80s): developing countries formally protested Western (primarily American) dominance of global news flows. The US withdrew from UNESCO partly over this debate (1984).
- UPI's decline (1980s-2000s): went bankrupt, sold to various owners. But AP remained dominant.
- The "Big Four" wire services (AP, Reuters, AFP, TASS/ITAR-TASS): track their relative global reach over time. Two of four are Anglo-American.
- Digital era: has social media and internet news reduced wire service dominance, or do AP/Reuters still set the agenda that everyone else follows?
