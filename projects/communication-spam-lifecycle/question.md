# Communication Spam Lifecycle

**Core question:** How quickly does each communication system go from "useful" to "worthless" once spam and advertising take hold — and is the cycle accelerating?

## Why it's interesting

Every communication medium in history follows the same arc: invention, adoption, advertisers arrive, signal-to-noise collapses, the system dies as communication, people migrate to the next thing. The pattern repeats faster each time. Telegraph took decades to get spammy. Email maybe 15 years. Facebook maybe 5. The graph should make this acceleration viscerally obvious.

The deeper thesis: when the cost to send a message drops to zero, the system's survival depends on someone paying for infrastructure — and that someone is always advertisers, who are literally purchasing the right to destroy the communication system. The business model is the disease.

## The punchline

The final data point is face-to-face communication. Every other line on the graph is a mountain that rises and falls. Face-to-face is a mostly flat line near the bottom with periodic historical spikes (religious movements, political purges, MLM). The question is whether the right edge — people performing their personal brand at you in meatspace, acting as unwitting mouthpieces for movements — is curving up permanently. It's the most speculative line, and that's what makes it land.

## Usability thresholds (Jason's framework)

- 50% junk = still usable
- 75% junk = somewhat unusable
- 90%+ junk = dead as a communication system

## Data approach

One research ticket per communication system. Each ticket collects 14 dimensions across the system's lifecycle: spam percentage, message volume, adoption rates, three layers of cost (user-pays, user-time, infrastructure), platform ARPU, barrier to reach, reach per message, gatekeeping, time to spam saturation, regulatory response, and defense mechanisms.

## Graph concepts

- **Primary:** Staggered mountain chart — X = year, Y = spam %, one line per system. The mountains march left to right through history, each one rising and falling. Face-to-face is the flat line with the ominous uptick.
- **Secondary:** Cost per message vs. time-to-spam-death. Scatter plot showing the inverse correlation.
- **Tertiary:** The shrinking window. How many years each system lasted before hitting 50% spam. The bars get shorter over time.

## Focus

US-centric (most data sources are US, Jason's frame of reference). Inflation-adjusted to 2026 USD for all cost figures.
