# Ticket 008: Cycling / Swimming (Alternative Cardio) — QALY Evidence and Cost

## Objective

Research the cost-effectiveness of cycling or swimming as cardio alternatives to walking. These have different cost and access profiles that may be interesting comparison points on the graph.

## What to research

### QALY Evidence
1. **Cycling** — all-cause mortality reduction, QALY estimates. Cycling-specific meta-analyses.
2. **Swimming** — mortality reduction, QALY estimates. Swimming-specific evidence.
3. **Comparison to walking** — are QALY gains per hour comparable to walking, or is there a meaningful difference?
4. **Any cost-effectiveness analyses** for cycling or swimming programs

### Cost Analysis
**Cycling:**
- Used bicycle ($50-150), new entry-level ($200-400)
- Helmet ($20-40)
- Maintenance (~$50-100/year)
- Total annual cost: estimate range

**Swimming:**
- Pool membership / community pool ($50-150/month, or $2-5/session at public pool)
- Swimwear ($20-40)
- Annual cost: estimate range

### Access and Barriers
- **Cycling safety** — bike lanes in low-income areas, traffic danger, theft risk
- **Swimming access** — public pool proximity in low-income neighborhoods
- **Seasonal limitations** — both are more weather-dependent than walking in many regions

## Where to look

1. **Tufts CEA Registry** — "cycling" or "swimming" cost-effectiveness
2. **Meta-analyses:** Oja et al. (2011) — health benefits of cycling; Chase et al. (2008) — swimming and health
3. **PubMed** — cycling/swimming QALY or cost-effectiveness
4. **Local government recreation departments** — public pool pricing
5. **BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey** — recreation spending patterns

## Output format

Use the full intervention result format from plan.md. If the evidence is strong enough, produce separate data points for cycling and swimming. If evidence is too thin for one, report that and focus on the other.

## What "good data" looks like

- Cycling likely has stronger evidence than swimming — prioritize accordingly
- QALY estimates comparable in methodology to walking (ticket 004) for fair comparison
- Cost data should reflect the cheapest viable entry point for Bob
