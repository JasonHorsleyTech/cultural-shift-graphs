# Ticket 038: AMOC Collapse — Effects on US Climate and Agriculture

## What to research

The Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC, including the Gulf Stream) may be approaching a tipping point. If it collapses, it would dramatically alter climate in the US and Europe. What would that look like?

## Specific data needed

1. **AMOC collapse probability and timeline:**
   - Ditlevsen & Ditlevsen (2023) — estimated AMOC collapse between 2025-2095 (central estimate ~2057)
   - IPCC AR6 assessment: "medium confidence" AMOC will not collapse before 2100 (but this was before Ditlevsen)
   - Is there scientific consensus? (No — it's hotly debated)
   - Current AMOC measurements: is it already weakening?

2. **Climate effects on the US:**
   - Eastern US cooling: how much? (Some models show 5-10°C cooling in the northeast)
   - But simultaneously, global warming continues — so it's regional cooling within global warming
   - Precipitation changes: drier or wetter for different US regions?
   - Growing season changes for Northeast, Midwest, Southeast

3. **Agricultural impact:**
   - Northeast US: shorter growing season, colder winters
   - Midwest: potentially beneficial (less extreme heat) or harmful (precipitation changes)?
   - Net US food production change: unclear and model-dependent
   - European agriculture would be devastated (they depend on Gulf Stream warmth more than the US)

4. **Sea level effects:**
   - AMOC collapse causes sea level rise along US East Coast (1-2 feet additional beyond thermal expansion)
   - This is RAPID — years, not centuries
   - East Coast cities: additional flooding pressure

5. **Cascading effects:**
   - AMOC collapse could shift tropical rain belts
   - Amazon die-back risk increases
   - Sahel desertification
   - These global effects reduce global food supply, which affects the US through trade disruption

6. **US mortality estimate:**
   - AMOC collapse alone is probably NOT a civilization-ending event for the US
   - But combined with other climate pressures (already warming, already stressed agriculture)
   - Estimated excess mortality per year from agricultural disruption + weather extremes

## Where to look

1. **Ditlevsen & Ditlevsen (2023) — Nature Communications** — AMOC collapse timeline
2. **Caesar et al. (2021) — Nature Geoscience** — AMOC weakening evidence
3. **Jackson et al. (2015)** — climate impact of AMOC shutdown
4. **Liu et al. (2017)** — AMOC collapse modeling
5. **IPCC AR6 — WG1 Chapter 9** — ocean circulation assessment
6. **Orihuela-Pinto et al. (2022)** — AMOC collapse global effects

## Result format

Key Data Points:
- `amoc_collapse_probability_by_2100`
- `us_temperature_change_c_northeast`
- `us_growing_season_change_days`
- `east_coast_sea_level_rise_additional_m`
- `us_agricultural_production_change_pct`

## What good data looks like

- Notes that AMOC collapse is the most debated tipping point — no consensus
- Distinguishes between "AMOC weakening" (already happening) and "AMOC collapse" (abrupt shutdown)
- US effects are less severe than European effects (the US doesn't depend on the Gulf Stream as much as Europe does)
- This feeds into the overall climate cascade scenario but may not be civilizational on its own for the US
