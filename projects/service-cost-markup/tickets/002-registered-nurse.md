# Registered Nurse — Healthcare

Nurses do most of the actual patient care. When you're in a hospital, you see the nurse 10x for every 1x you see the doctor. Yet they're paid a fraction of what physicians earn.

**Fundamental worker:** Registered nurse (RN)
**Customer cost proxy:** Same denominator as the physician ticket — total healthcare expenditure per capita, or average hospital stay cost. Keep it consistent with ticket 001 so the lines are directly comparable.

**Interesting angles:**
- Nursing shortage cycles — do wages spike during shortages while patient costs keep climbing regardless?
- The ratio of nurse compensation to total hospital revenue per patient-day
- Travel nursing explosion post-COVID — did that change the ratio at all, or did hospitals just eat it?
