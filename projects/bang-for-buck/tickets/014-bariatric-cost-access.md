# Ticket 014: Bariatric Surgery — Cost, Insurance, and Access for Bob

## Objective

Determine the realistic cost and access path for bariatric surgery for someone at the 25th percentile of household income.

## What to research

1. **Surgical costs**
   - Gastric bypass: total facility + surgeon + anesthesia cost ($20,000-35,000)
   - Sleeve gastrectomy: total cost ($15,000-25,000)
   - Cash-pay / medical tourism options (if relevant)

2. **Insurance pathways**
   - What % of Bob's bracket has insurance that covers bariatric surgery?
   - Medicaid coverage by state (varies widely)
   - Employer insurance coverage requirements
   - Typical copay/coinsurance for covered surgery at Bob's income

3. **Pre-surgical requirements (gatekeeping costs)**
   - 3-6 month supervised weight management program (required by most insurers)
   - Cost of monthly dietitian/physician visits during this period
   - Psychological evaluation ($200-400)
   - Total pre-surgical cost and time investment

4. **Post-surgical ongoing costs**
   - Vitamin/mineral supplementation (lifelong): $30-60/month
   - Follow-up visits: frequency and cost for first 2 years, then annually
   - Lab work: frequency and cost
   - Potential revision surgery: cost and likelihood
   - Dietary supplements and protein: additional food cost
   - Annual ongoing total

5. **Total cost calculation**
   - One-time: surgery + pre-surgical
   - Ongoing: vitamins + follow-ups + labs
   - Amortized over 3 years (per plan.md): formula
   - Realistic cost with Medicaid vs. without insurance

6. **Access barriers**
   - Geographic: surgical centers in rural/low-income areas
   - BMI threshold: must be BMI ≥40 or ≥35 with comorbidities
   - Wait time from decision to surgery (6-12 months typical with insurance)
   - Recovery: 2-4 weeks off work — can Bob afford unpaid leave?

7. **Time commitment**
   - Pre-surgical: monthly appointments for 3-6 months
   - Surgery + recovery: 2-4 weeks
   - Post-surgical: follow-up schedule
   - Annualized hours (front-loaded in year 1, minimal ongoing)

## Where to look

1. **FAIR Health** — surgical cost data by procedure and region
2. **Bariatric Surgery Source** — cost guides and insurance requirements
3. **KFF** — Medicaid bariatric coverage by state
4. **ASMBS** (American Society for Metabolic and Bariatric Surgery) — access data
5. **CMS** — Medicare/Medicaid coverage decisions for bariatric surgery

## Output format

Use the intervention result format from plan.md, focusing on cost and access. Reference ticket 013 for QALY. Produce complete "Data for Graph" table.

## What "good data" looks like

- Separate cost scenarios: with Medicaid, with employer insurance, uninsured
- The "realistic for Bob" number should account for the MOST LIKELY insurance scenario at his income
- Include ongoing costs — the surgery isn't a one-time expense
- Amortized annual cost that's honest about the total lifetime cost
