# Dentist — Dental Care

Dental is interesting because it has LESS insurance distortion than medical. Most dental insurance has low caps ($1,000-2,000/year), so patients see more of the real price. Theory: the ratio should be healthier here than in medicine.

**Fundamental worker:** Dentist (general)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average annual dental spending per patient, or average cost of a common procedure (cleaning, filling, extraction) over time.

**Interesting angles:**
- Dental insurance caps haven't changed much since the 1970s (still ~$1,500) while dental costs have risen. Has this kept the dentist's ratio more honest?
- Corporate dentistry (Aspen Dental, etc.) vs. solo practice — is the ratio different?
- Dental school debt has exploded — does the dentist's gross ratio look healthy but net ratio (after debt service) look worse?
