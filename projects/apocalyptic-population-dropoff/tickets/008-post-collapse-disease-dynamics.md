# Ticket 008: Post-Collapse Disease Dynamics

## What to research

When urban water treatment fails, sewage systems back up, and medical care disappears, what diseases emerge and how fast do they kill? This is the "second wave" of mortality that hits after the immediate medical cliff but before starvation fully kicks in.

## Specific data needed

1. **Waterborne diseases:**
   - Cholera, typhoid, dysentery — onset timeline after water treatment failure
   - Historical mortality rates without treatment
   - How many US water systems depend on continuous electric power?
   - Timeline: how many days after grid failure until water becomes unsafe?

2. **Crowding/refugee diseases:**
   - When urban populations flee to rural areas, what disease dynamics emerge?
   - Historical: refugee camp mortality rates (UNHCR data)
   - Measles, respiratory infections, skin infections in crowded unsanitary conditions

3. **Untreated infections:**
   - Minor injuries (cuts, broken bones) that become fatal without antibiotics
   - Dental infections — a leading cause of pre-antibiotic death
   - Appendicitis, peritonitis, sepsis mortality without surgery

4. **Decomposition and sanitation:**
   - Timeline for disease risk from unburied dead in urban areas
   - Historical: disease spikes following mass casualty events
   - Rat/vermin population explosion when sanitation fails

5. **Pre-industrial disease mortality as baseline:**
   - Leading causes of death in 1850-1900 US (before antibiotics, before water treatment)
   - Crude death rate in pre-industrial societies vs. modern (modern: ~8/1000/year; pre-industrial: ~30-40/1000/year)
   - How quickly does mortality revert to pre-industrial rates after infrastructure collapse?

## Where to look

1. **WHO / CDC** — waterborne disease outbreak data
2. **UNHCR / MSF (Doctors Without Borders)** — refugee camp disease/mortality data
3. **Historical epidemiology** — pre-antibiotic mortality rates by disease
4. **US water utility data** — power dependency of water treatment systems
5. **McNeill, William — "Plagues and Peoples"**
6. **Cipolla, Carlo — "Before the Industrial Revolution"** — pre-industrial mortality data

## Result format

Key Data Points table with parameters:
- Disease-specific mortality rates (untreated) and onset timelines
- Estimated excess deaths from disease by time period (month 1, month 3, month 6, month 12)
- Pre-industrial baseline mortality rate for calibration

## What good data looks like

- Ties disease outbreaks to specific infrastructure failures (water → cholera at day X; sanitation → typhoid at day Y)
- Uses refugee camp mortality as the primary modern calibration (closest analogue to post-collapse conditions)
- Provides a time-phased estimate: disease deaths in month 1 vs. month 3 vs. month 6
- Distinguishes between endemic disease (ongoing elevated mortality) and epidemic spikes (cholera outbreak kills X% in a region over 2 weeks)
