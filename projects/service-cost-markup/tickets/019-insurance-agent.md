# Insurance Agent — Insurance

You pay a premium. Some fraction of that premium goes to claims (the thing you're actually buying). Some fraction goes to the agent who sold you the policy. Some fraction goes to... everything else.

**Fundamental worker:** Insurance agent / broker (property & casualty, or life — pick whichever has better longitudinal data)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average annual insurance premium (auto, home, or life).

**Interesting angles:**
- The "loss ratio" (claims paid / premiums collected) is public data for insurers. The inverse tells you how much is overhead + profit. But what fraction of the overhead is the agent?
- Direct-to-consumer insurance (GEICO, Progressive online) vs. agent-based (State Farm, Allstate) — has cutting out the agent reduced the customer's cost, or did the savings go to shareholder profit?
- The agent's share has been declining for decades as direct sales grow. But premiums haven't dropped proportionally.
