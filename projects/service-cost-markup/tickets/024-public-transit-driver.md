# Public Transit Bus Driver — Municipal Transit

Public transit is subsidized, so the "customer cost" has two components: the fare the rider pays AND the taxpayer subsidy. Most transit agencies recover only 20-40% of operating costs from fares. The rest is taxes.

**Fundamental worker:** Municipal bus driver
**Customer cost proxy:** Total operating cost per passenger trip (this is a standard transit metric — the National Transit Database tracks it). Includes both fare revenue and subsidy. This is the true cost to society per ride.

**Interesting angles:**
- Fares have been relatively flat or even declining in real terms (some cities have made transit free). But operating costs per trip have risen. Is the driver getting a bigger share, or is overhead growing?
- Transit agencies are famously bureaucratic. Administrative overhead is a known issue. How does the driver's share compare to the administrative share?
- Compare to the taxi/rideshare driver — similar job, very different cost structures.
