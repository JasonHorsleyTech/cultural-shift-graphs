# Personal Trainer — Fitness

A personal trainer at a big-box gym (LA Fitness, Equinox) earns $15-30/hour of the $60-100/hour the gym charges clients. The gym provides the space and equipment — but the client could meet the trainer in a park with a kettlebell and get the same workout.

**Fundamental worker:** Personal trainer / fitness instructor
**Customer cost proxy:** Average cost of a personal training session. Or gym membership + training package cost.

**Interesting angles:**
- Gym-employed vs. independent: independent trainers keep 100% but need to find clients. Gym trainers have a captive audience but give up 50-70% of the session fee.
- Online coaching / remote training: the trainer's overhead dropped to nearly zero. Did their ratio improve, or did platforms (Peloton, apps) capture the savings?
- Equinox charges $200+/session in NYC. Planet Fitness charges $10/month for no training at all. The fitness industry has stratified — what does the ratio look like at each end?
