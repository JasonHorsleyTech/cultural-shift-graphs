# American Casual Fashion Adoption Globally

Blue jeans, sneakers, t-shirts, baseball caps — American casual dress has become the global default. Before American cultural influence, most cultures had distinct everyday clothing traditions. The adoption of American casual fashion is a visible, physical measure of cultural homogenization.

**Metric:** Composite indicator of American casual fashion adoption: global jeans sales/production, sneaker market growth, survey data on daily clothing choices by country.

**Data proxy:** Textile industry reports, Levi Strauss corporate history and international sales data, global athletic footwear market reports (Nike, Adidas annual reports), fashion industry surveys.

**Interesting angles:**
- Blue jeans: Invented in the US (1873, Levi Strauss). Became a symbol of American rebellion in the 1950s (James Dean). Were literally black-market items behind the Iron Curtain — Soviet youth paid months' wages for a pair of Levi's.
- Post-WW2: American GIs introduced casual American dress to Europe and Asia. T-shirts went from underwear to outerwear.
- 1980s-90s: Nike and American sneaker culture went global. The Air Jordan phenomenon.
- Japanese "Americana" fashion movement: Japanese brands now produce jeans, workwear, and Americana clothing at higher quality than American originals. A case of cultural adoption becoming cultural mastery.
- Business dress: "business casual" is an American invention that has displaced formal dress codes globally. Track when major non-US markets adopted it.
- Track global denim production volume over time as a rough proxy for jeans adoption.
