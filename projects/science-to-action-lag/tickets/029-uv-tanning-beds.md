# Ticket 029: UV Radiation / Tanning Beds → Skin Cancer

## Objective

Research the timeline from scientific evidence that UV radiation (particularly from artificial tanning devices) causes skin cancer to binding regulation restricting tanning bed use.

## What to research

1. **Early evidence date** — The UV-skin cancer link has been recognized since the early 20th century (outdoor workers, tropical populations). For ARTIFICIAL UV (tanning beds): commercial tanning beds became widespread in the 1970s-1980s. Epidemiological studies linking tanning bed use to melanoma risk accumulated through the 1990s-2000s. When did credible evidence specifically implicate artificial UV exposure?

2. **Consensus date** — IARC classified UV-emitting tanning devices as Group 1 carcinogens in 2009 (moved from Group 2A). WHO has recommended against tanning bed use. This is a clean consensus date.

3. **First OECD regulation** — Brazil banned commercial tanning beds entirely in 2009. Australia banned commercial tanning beds effective 2015 (state-level bans from 2014). Many OECD countries ban minors' access: France (1997), UK (2010), Germany (2012). What was the first BINDING restriction?

4. **US regulation** — No federal ban on tanning beds. FDA reclassified tanning devices and required warnings (2014). FCA (Affordable Care Act) imposed a 10% excise tax on tanning services (2010). Some states ban minors' access. California was the first state to ban minors (2011).

5. **Industry opposition** — The Indoor Tanning Association lobbied against restrictions. Moderate opposition — the tanning industry is much smaller than tobacco or fossil fuels.

6. **Exposure reduction** — Tanning bed usage rates among young people have declined significantly since the 2010s. CDC data tracks this.

## Where to look

1. IARC Monograph on UV radiation and tanning devices (2012, based on 2009 classification)
2. WHO position on artificial tanning
3. Brazil ANVISA regulation banning tanning beds (2009)
4. Australian state tanning bed bans (2014-2015)
5. FDA tanning device reclassification (2014)
6. CDC data on tanning bed usage trends among youth

## What "good data" looks like

- Separate the NATURAL UV-skin cancer science (early 20th century) from TANNING BED-specific evidence (1990s-2000s). This ticket is about tanning beds — the specific regulatory target.
- The IARC 2009 classification as a clean consensus date
- Range of regulatory responses: outright bans (Brazil, Australia), minors-only bans (Europe, some US states), taxation (US), nothing (many countries)

## Known pitfalls

- Natural UV radiation can't be "regulated" (you can't ban sunlight). The regulatory story is about ARTIFICIAL UV from tanning devices. Keep the focus narrow.
- The US has no tanning bed BAN — the 10% tax and FDA warnings are the strongest federal actions. For us_regulation_year, use the FDA warning/tax date or "null" if you judge these insufficient.
- France's 1997 ban on minors is early but narrow (under-18 only). Decide whether a minors-only ban counts as "meaningful regulation reducing population exposure."
- Category: substance-ban (banning a consumer service/product) or behavioral-mandate (restricting access by age).
