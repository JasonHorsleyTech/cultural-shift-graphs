# Home Inspector — Real Estate

The home inspector spends 2-3 hours crawling through your attic and under your house, then writes a detailed report. They charge $300-500. It's one of the most transparently priced services in real estate — one person, one house, one fee. No middlemen.

**Fundamental worker:** Home inspector
**Customer cost proxy:** Average home inspection fee. ASHI (American Society of Home Inspectors) may have data.

**Interesting angles:**
- This might be a "control case" for real estate — a service adjacent to home buying that DOESN'T have the commission distortion.
- Home inspection wasn't even standard practice until the 1970s-80s. The industry is relatively new.
- Has the inspection fee scaled with home prices? The work doesn't change much whether the house is worth $200K or $800K, but inspectors in expensive markets charge more.
- Some states now require inspectors to carry E&O insurance, be licensed, etc. Has that increased costs?
