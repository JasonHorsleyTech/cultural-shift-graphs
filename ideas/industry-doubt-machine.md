# The Industry Doubt Machine

## The question

For major hazards where industry privately knew the science was right, how long did they successfully delay public consensus? Measure the gap between internal industry acknowledgment and public scientific consensus.

## Why it's interesting

Known data points exist:
- **Tobacco:** Industry knew by 1950s, public consensus ~1964 (Surgeon General). ~10-14 year manufactured doubt gap.
- **Lead (Ethyl Corp):** Knew by 1920s, public consensus delayed until 1970s. ~40-50 year gap.
- **Sugar (SRF):** Knew by 1960s, public consensus ~2010s. ~40-50 year gap.
- **PFAS (3M/DuPont):** Knew by 1970s, public consensus ~2012 (C8 Science Panel). ~35-40 year gap.
- **Climate change (Exxon):** Internal research in 1970s-80s, funded counter-messaging through 2000s.

Internal documents have been made public for several of these (tobacco litigation, DuPont C8 lawsuit, Exxon climate research). The data is real and documented.

## What the graph would show

X-axis: calendar year. Each hazard gets a bar from "industry privately knew" to "public consensus formed." The bar IS the manufactured doubt period. Sort by gap length.

The devastating finding: is the doubt machine getting faster or more effective over time? Or has litigation/transparency (FOIA, whistleblowers, discovery) shortened the window?
