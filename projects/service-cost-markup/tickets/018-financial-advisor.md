# Financial Advisor — Wealth Management

The industry where the fee structure IS the story. A 1% AUM (assets under management) fee sounds small but compounds to enormous sums over a lifetime. The "fundamental worker" — the advisor — might spend 2 hours/year on your portfolio.

**Fundamental worker:** Financial advisor / wealth manager
**Customer cost proxy:** Total advisory fees paid by average client per year (AUM fee × average portfolio, or flat fee, or commission). This has shifted from commission-based to fee-based over time.

**Interesting angles:**
- A 1% AUM fee on a $500,000 portfolio = $5,000/year for maybe 5-10 hours of work. That's $500-1,000/hour effective rate. But the advisor might only keep 30-40% of that (the rest goes to the firm, compliance, platform fees).
- Robo-advisors (Betterment, Wealthfront) charge 0.25%. The ratio of "human effort to fee" is wildly different.
- The fiduciary rule fight — did the shift from commission to fee-based change who captures the money, or just relabel it?
