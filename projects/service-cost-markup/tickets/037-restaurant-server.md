# Restaurant Server / Waiter — Full-Service Dining

The server is the face of the restaurant experience. Their "compensation" is weird — they're paid $2.13/hour (federal tipped minimum) plus tips. The tip IS their compensation, and it comes directly from the customer. This might be one of the only industries where the fundamental worker's ratio is partially set by the customer in real time.

**Fundamental worker:** Server / waiter (full-service restaurant)
**Customer cost proxy:** Same denominator as the cook ticket — average meal cost at a full-service restaurant. But the server's "compensation" includes tips, which are not part of the restaurant's revenue.

**Interesting angles:**
- Tipping norms have shifted from 15% to 18-20% to "tip on the iPad for counter service." Has the server's TOTAL comp (wage + tips) as a fraction of the meal cost actually changed?
- The tipped minimum wage has been $2.13/hour since 1991. It hasn't changed in 30+ years. Meanwhile menu prices have doubled. The server's base wage as a fraction of the bill has collapsed, but tips might have compensated.
- Tip pooling, tip sharing with kitchen staff — the allocation is shifting. Does this show up in the data?
- Counter-service tipping (where there's no server at all) is pure overhead capture by the POS system / business.
