# Police Officer — Law Enforcement

Same structure as firefighters (municipal budget, property taxes) but a much more politically charged conversation. Police budgets have been a flashpoint. What does the ratio actually show?

**Fundamental worker:** Patrol officer / police officer (not detectives, not command staff — the street-level officer)
**Customer cost proxy:** Police department share of municipal budget, divided by residents or households served. Same methodology as firefighter ticket for comparability.

**Interesting angles:**
- Police overtime is a massive budget item — some officers earn 2x their base salary in overtime. Is the "fundamental worker compensation" the base salary or the total comp including overtime? (Use total comp — that's what the taxpayer is paying for.)
- Police department administrative overhead, internal affairs, training academies, legal settlements — all of this comes out of the police budget but doesn't go to patrol officers.
- "Defund" debate aside — has the patrol officer's share of the police budget actually changed over time, or has it been stable?
- Compare directly to firefighters: same tax base, different trajectory?
