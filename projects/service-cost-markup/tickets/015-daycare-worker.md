# Daycare / Childcare Worker — Early Childhood

One of the strangest paradoxes in the economy: daycare is simultaneously unaffordable for parents AND pays poverty wages to workers. Where is the money going? It's not going to shareholders — most daycare centers are small businesses barely breaking even.

**Fundamental worker:** Childcare worker / daycare teacher
**Customer cost proxy:** Average annual childcare cost per child (Child Care Aware of America tracks this).

**Interesting angles:**
- This might be the one industry where the ratio is ALREADY very high (workers are cheap, prices are high because of mandated ratios), but the worker is still poor because the volume is so low (you can only watch so many kids by law).
- Regulatory requirements (staff-to-child ratios, facility requirements, licensing) have increased over time. Has that changed the cost structure?
- This is a case where the "bloat" might not be institutional overhead but regulatory mandate. Different story than college.
