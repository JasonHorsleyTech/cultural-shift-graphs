# Movie Ticket — Quality Rubric

## What to research

Define what "going to the movies" meant in ~1955 and assess how the experience has changed. Build a quality rubric. The explore phase expects this to be a modest-gap case — the basic experience is similar but premium formats now exist.

## Research tasks

1. **1955 baseline:** What was the standard moviegoing experience? Single screen, one showing time or two, no reserved seating, possibly a newsreel or cartoon short before the feature. What kind of theater (neighborhood single-screen vs. downtown palace)?

2. **Quality attributes to score (minimum 4):**
   - Screen size and picture quality (film projection 1955 vs. digital 2025)
   - Sound quality (mono 1955 → Dolby Atmos 2025)
   - Seating (basic fixed seats 1955 vs. standard stadium seating 2025 vs. recliners)
   - Pre-show experience (newsreel + cartoon 1955 vs. 20 minutes of ads 2025)
   - Convenience (one showtime vs. multiple, online ticketing, reserved seats)
   - Concessions availability (modest 1955 vs. elaborate 2025, but concessions are separate cost)

3. **Is there a quality gap or improvement?** Arguments both ways:
   - **Improved:** Digital projection, surround sound, stadium seating, reserved seats, air conditioning (not universal in 1955).
   - **Degraded:** Pre-show ads, smaller screens (multiplex vs. single-screen), less communal experience.
   - Likely verdict: the standard experience improved in most measurable dimensions, but the premium experience (IMAX, Dolby Cinema, luxury recliners) costs significantly more.

4. **Modern tiers:**
   - Standard matinee: $9-12
   - Standard evening: $12-16
   - Premium format (IMAX, Dolby): $18-25
   - Luxury (dine-in, recliners): $20-30

## Data source hierarchy

1. NATO (National Association of Theatre Owners) — average ticket price data
2. MPAA/MPA annual theme reports — box office and pricing data
3. BLS CPI "admission to movies" component
4. Film history scholarship on theater exhibition practices

## Output format

Follow the **Type 2: Quality rubric** format from plan.md.

## What "good data" looks like

- Honest about the fact that the modern standard experience is arguably BETTER on most technical dimensions
- Identifies what the "quality-equivalent" is: either the standard ticket (if 1955 baseline was also basic) or premium format (if the 1955 experience was actually closer to today's premium — big screen, communal event)
