# Optometrist — Vision Care

You get your eyes checked by one person in a room with a fancy chair. Then you walk to the front and pay $400 for frames that cost $5 to manufacture (thanks, Luxottica). The optometrist's share of the total "vision visit" cost (exam + glasses) is probably very small.

**Fundamental worker:** Optometrist
**Customer cost proxy:** Total cost of a vision care visit including exam AND eyewear. Or just the exam fee alone — but the interesting story might be in the combined number since Luxottica's monopoly on frames is a huge part of what the customer pays.

**Interesting angles:**
- Luxottica (now EssilorLuxottica) controls ~80% of the eyewear market. They own LensCrafters, Pearle Vision, Sunglass Hut, Ray-Ban, Oakley, AND EyeMed insurance. The frame markup is ~1000%.
- Online retailers (Zenni, Warby Parker) disrupting the frame market — has that changed the optometrist's ratio at all?
