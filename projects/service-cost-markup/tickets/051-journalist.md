# Journalist — News Media

The advertising-funded model is dead. Subscriptions are back. But journalist salaries have been in freefall even as media companies charge $10-15/month for digital subscriptions. The "fundamental worker" — the person who writes the stories you're paying to read — is getting squeezed from both ends.

**Fundamental worker:** Journalist / reporter (newspaper or digital news)
**Customer cost proxy:** This is tricky. Pre-internet: newspaper subscription price + advertising revenue per subscriber. Post-internet: digital subscription price. Or total news industry revenue per journalist employed.

**Interesting angles:**
- Newsroom employment has been cut roughly in half since 2008. Revenue hasn't dropped by half. Where did the savings go?
- Local news vs. national news: local newsrooms have been gutted while national outlets (NYT, WaPo) have grown subscription revenue. Different ratio stories.
- The "journalist per subscriber" ratio has changed dramatically — fewer journalists serving more readers. Productivity went up. Did pay?
- Hedge fund ownership of newspapers (Alden Global Capital) — pure extraction. Buy paper, cut staff, extract profit until it dies.
