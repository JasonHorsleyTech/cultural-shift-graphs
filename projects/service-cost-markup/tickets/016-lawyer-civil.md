# Lawyer — Civil Litigation

The adversarial arms race profession. Two $500/hour lawyers arguing against each other produce roughly the same outcome as two $100/hour lawyers. The entire industry might be an antagonistic cost.

**Fundamental worker:** Lawyer (civil litigation — not corporate transactional, which is a different beast)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average total cost of a civil lawsuit to the plaintiff/defendant, or average hourly legal fees, or total legal services spending per capita.

**Interesting angles:**
- The lawyer is HIGHLY paid. The interesting question isn't "is the lawyer underpaid" but "how much of the total legal bill goes to actual lawyer time vs. associates, paralegals, discovery, expert witnesses, filing fees, court costs?"
- BigLaw associate billing rates have doubled since 2000 while associate salaries have grown more slowly. The spread is firm profit.
- Legal tech (e-discovery, AI contract review) was supposed to make law cheaper. Has it? Or did it just create a new cost layer?
- How much of the total cost of the legal system is borne by the taxpayer (courts, judges, public defenders)?
