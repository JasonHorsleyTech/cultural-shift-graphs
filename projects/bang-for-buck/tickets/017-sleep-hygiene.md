# Ticket 017: Sleep Hygiene Improvements — QALY Evidence and Cost

## Objective

Research the cost-effectiveness of sleep hygiene improvements and/or CBT for insomnia (CBT-I) for someone in Bob's profile. Poor sleep is correlated with low income and contributes to multiple health conditions.

## What to research

### QALY Evidence
1. **CBT-I cost-effectiveness** — this is the most evidence-based sleep intervention and has published QALY data. Search for CEA studies.
2. **Sleep hygiene education** — simpler than CBT-I. Any QALY data for basic sleep hygiene programs?
3. **QALY gains from improving sleep** — insomnia resolution improves productivity, mood, cardiovascular health, weight management. Multi-dimensional like walking.
4. **For Bob specifically** — shift workers have disrupted sleep. Does sleep hygiene work for shift workers?

### Cost Analysis
1. **Basic sleep hygiene (free tier):**
   - Consistent bedtime, dark room, no screens before bed, no caffeine after noon
   - Cost: $0-30 (blackout curtains, maybe a fan for white noise)
2. **CBT-I:**
   - Therapist-delivered (4-8 sessions): cost similar to CBT for depression
   - Digital CBT-I (e.g., Sleepstation, Sleepio): $0-50/month
   - Self-help workbook: $15-20
3. **Compared to sleep medication (as reference):**
   - OTC melatonin: $5-15/month
   - Prescription sleep aids: more expensive, habituating, not cost-effective long-term

### Access and Barriers
1. **Sleep environment** — Bob may live in a noisy, light-polluted, or overcrowded dwelling
2. **Shift work** — makes conventional sleep hygiene impossible; need shift-work-specific advice
3. **Screen use** — reducing screen time before bed is a lifestyle change, not a purchase

## Where to look

1. **Tufts CEA Registry** — "insomnia" or "CBT-I" or "sleep" cost-effectiveness
2. **NICE Guidelines** — insomnia management (CBT-I is first-line in UK)
3. **Cochrane Reviews** — "CBT for insomnia"
4. **Key studies:**
   - Espie et al. — digital CBT-I effectiveness
   - Botteman (2009) — economic burden of insomnia
5. **PubMed** — "sleep hygiene QALY" or "insomnia cost-effectiveness"

## Output format

Use the full intervention result format from plan.md. Decide whether to report "basic sleep hygiene" and "CBT-I" as one data point or two. If QALY data only exists for CBT-I, report that and note that basic sleep hygiene is less well-studied.

## What "good data" looks like

- CBT-I has stronger evidence than general sleep hygiene — lean on CBT-I data
- QALY estimates that capture the multi-dimensional benefits of better sleep
- Cost for the cheapest effective version (digital CBT-I or self-guided)
