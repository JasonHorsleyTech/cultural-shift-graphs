# American TV Show Exports and Broadcast Hours Abroad

American television has been exported globally since the 1950s. "I Love Lucy," "Dallas," "Baywatch," "Friends," and now prestige streaming shows have been fixtures of foreign television schedules for decades. Track the volume and share of American TV content broadcast on foreign networks.

**Metric:** US-produced content's share of television programming hours on foreign broadcast networks, by decade and region.

**Data proxy:** European Audiovisual Observatory, UNESCO communication statistics, Eurodata TV Worldwide, national broadcasting authority reports, academic studies on TV content flows.

**Interesting angles:**
- 1950s-60s: early American TV exports to Europe, Latin America, Japan. US shows were cheap to buy compared to local production costs.
- "Dallas" phenomenon (1978-91): watched in 90+ countries. One of the first truly global TV shows.
- "Baywatch": the most-watched TV show in history at its peak (1.1 billion viewers in 142 countries). Why did that specific show achieve global penetration?
- Format exports vs. direct exports: "Who Wants to Be a Millionaire," "American Idol," "The Office" — American formats adapted locally. Count both direct exports AND format licenses.
- EU Television Without Frontiers Directive (1989): required majority European content on European broadcasters. Was it effective?
- Telenovela counter-example: Latin American telenovelas are exported globally, including to the US. One of the few reverse flows.
- Streaming era: Netflix, Amazon, Disney+ distribute American content directly, bypassing broadcast quotas entirely.
