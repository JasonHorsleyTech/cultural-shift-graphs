# Ticket 002: Bob's Mental Health and Social Baseline

## Objective

Establish the mental health and social profile of an American at the 25th percentile of household income (~$30,000). Reference document for all intervention tickets.

## What to research

For adults in the bottom quartile of US household income:

1. **Depression** — prevalence of current depressive episode, major depressive disorder diagnosis rate
2. **Anxiety** — generalized anxiety disorder prevalence, self-reported anxiety/stress levels
3. **Substance use** — alcohol consumption patterns (heavy/binge drinking rates), drug use
4. **Social isolation** — average number of close friends/confidants, loneliness measures, relationship status distribution
5. **Mental health treatment** — % currently receiving any mental health treatment, % with unmet mental health needs
6. **Cognitive function** — self-reported cognitive difficulty, any data on executive function by income
7. **Sleep** — average sleep duration, insomnia prevalence, sleep quality measures
8. **Suicide risk** — ideation and attempt rates by income bracket

## Where to look

1. **CDC BRFSS** — depression screening (PHQ-8), mental health days, sleep data by income
2. **NHANES** — PHQ-9 depression screening by income, substance use data
3. **SAMHSA NSDUH** (National Survey on Drug Use and Health) — substance use and mental health by income
4. **Gallup-Sharecare Well-Being Index** — social well-being, loneliness by income
5. **Census CPS** — relationship status, household composition by income

## Output format

Use the baseline profile format from plan.md. Key statistics table with metric, value, and source citation.

## What "good data" looks like

- Statistics for bottom income quartile or closest bracket
- Recent data (2018-2024 preferred)
- Validated screening instruments (PHQ-9, GAD-7) preferred over self-report where available
- National-level data
