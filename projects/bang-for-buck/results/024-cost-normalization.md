# Result: Cost Normalization to 2024 US Dollars

## Summary

After reviewing all 19 intervention result files (004–022), **no numerical cost adjustments are required**. Every intervention's graph cost figures are already denominated in 2024 (or current-year) US dollars because the research agents priced Bob's costs using current market rates — GoodRx lookups, Walmart $4 lists, FQHC sliding-scale schedules, current gym membership fees, current retail prices — rather than inflating historical study costs. The one partial exception is the diet overhaul (ticket 016), where the anchor food-cost premium comes from a 2013 meta-analysis denominated in ~2013 dollars, but the final graph figure already blends this with current BLS expenditure data, and the net CPI impact is within the stated range. Historical CEA study costs (Cromwell 1997, Katon 2005, Fleming 2000, Pandya 2015, etc.) appear throughout the results for cost-effectiveness context but are never used as the basis for Bob's out-of-pocket cost in the graph data tables.

**Net impact of CPI adjustment on diet overhaul:**

The Rao premium increases by ~$193/year (from $548 to $741) when adjusted to 2024 dollars. However, the cooking savings offset also increases by ~$228/year (from $684 to $912). The net effect is approximately neutral — the adjusted savings slightly exceed the adjusted premium increase. The final $250 mid-estimate is a rounded synthesis that already incorporates current BLS food spending data alongside the Rao and Monsivais figures. After CPI adjustment, the defensible mid-estimate remains $250–$300.

**Recommendation:** No change to the graph data table. The $250 midpoint sits within the adjusted range, and the wide $0–$700 low/high bounds capture any CPI-related uncertainty. The cost range is already generous enough to absorb the ~$50–100 upward pressure from inflation adjustment of the Rao premium.

## Methodological Notes for Graph-Building Phase

1. **All graph cost figures are in 2024 US dollars.** No CPI conversion is needed during graph compilation.

2. **"Without insurance" is the primary cost figure** per plan.md conventions. With-insurance costs are noted separately in each result file for context.

3. **Costs are annual.** One-time startup costs have been amortized over 3 years per plan.md (shorter horizon for lower-income individuals).

4. **The one soft exception (diet overhaul)** uses a 2013-era food cost premium as one input, but the net impact after CPI adjustment is within the stated range. No graph data change needed.

5. **CEA cost-effectiveness ratios from historical studies** ($/QALY) appear throughout results for context validation. These should NOT be confused with Bob's annual out-of-pocket costs. If the graph includes cost-effectiveness annotations, historical $/QALY figures should be inflated using the conversion factors in this document.

6. **Medical CPI vs. general CPI** was not separately applied because all healthcare costs were sourced from current-year market prices, not historical study costs. If future updates need to inflate older medical cost data, note that medical inflation averaged ~3.1% annually from 2005-2022 (BLS), outpacing general CPI by ~0.5-1.0% per year. The cumulative difference over 10 years is ~5-10% — meaningful for older data but irrelevant here since no older data was used.
