# EMT / Paramedic — Emergency Medical Services

The $3,000 ambulance ride staffed by two people making $18/hour. This might be the single most extreme ratio on the entire graph.

**Fundamental worker:** EMT and/or paramedic
**Customer cost proxy:** Average ambulance bill (including what insurance pays). This is notoriously hard to pin down because of balance billing, but there should be aggregate data.

**Interesting angles:**
- Two EMTs × 45 minutes = ~$40 of labor. The bill is $2,000-$5,000. Where does the rest go?
- Many ambulance services are now run by private equity firms (KKR bought Air Methods, etc.). Did privatization change the ratio?
- Volunteer EMS vs. professional EMS — some rural areas still use volunteers. What does the "ratio" look like when the worker is free?
