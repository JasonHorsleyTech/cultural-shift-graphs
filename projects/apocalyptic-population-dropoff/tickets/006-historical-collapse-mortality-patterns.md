# Ticket 006: Historical Societal Collapse — Mortality Patterns and Timelines

## What to research

How quickly did populations decline in historical societal collapses? These are the closest real-world analogues for the curves we're trying to draw.

## Specific collapses to cover

For each, find: pre-collapse population, post-collapse population, timeline of decline, primary causes of death, and time to stabilization.

1. **Siege of Leningrad (1941-44)** — 3M pre-siege → ~1.5M deaths. Best-documented urban starvation event.
2. **Cambodian genocide / Khmer Rouge (1975-79)** — 8M → ~6M. Forced agrarianism + violence.
3. **Irish Great Famine (1845-52)** — 8.2M → 6.6M (continued to 4.4M by 1901). Long-tail decline.
4. **Black Death in Europe (1347-53)** — ~75M → ~50M (range: 30-60% mortality). Disease + social collapse.
5. **Post-Soviet Russia (1991-2003)** — Controlled decline with international aid. 3-7M excess deaths.
6. **Rwandan genocide (1994)** — 7M → rapid loss of 500-800K in 100 days. Violence-driven.
7. **Western Roman Empire collapse (5th century)** — Slow decline, population of Rome from ~1M to ~20K over centuries.
8. **Mayan Classic Period collapse (~800-1000 CE)** — Population estimates vary widely. Multi-century decline.
9. **Chinese Great Leap Forward famine (1959-61)** — 15-55M excess deaths (range is enormous). State-caused famine.
10. **Bronze Age Collapse (~1200 BCE)** — Multiple civilizations collapsed. Population data is archaeological.

## Where to look

1. **Tainter, Joseph — "The Collapse of Complex Societies" (1988)**
2. **Diamond, Jared — "Collapse" (2005)** — for Mayan, Easter Island, etc.
3. **Demographic studies for each specific collapse** (academic papers)
4. **Prentice, Thomson — WHO "Health, History, and Hard Choices" series**
5. **Historical demography databases**

## Result format

Key Data Points table with one row per collapse event:

| parameter | value | range_low | range_high | unit | timeframe | source | confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Parameters should include: `pre_collapse_population`, `post_collapse_population`, `mortality_pct`, `decline_duration_years`, `primary_cause`, `time_to_stabilization_years`.

Also include a **Mortality Timeline** table for the 2-3 best-documented collapses (Leningrad, Cambodia, Ireland) showing the shape of the decline curve.

## What good data looks like

- At least 6 of the 10 collapses have usable population numbers (not just "it was bad")
- The well-documented ones (Leningrad, Ireland, Cambodia) have year-by-year or month-by-month data
- Notes whether each collapse was sudden (months) or gradual (decades/centuries)
- Identifies which collapses are most analogous to a modern US grid-down scenario (Leningrad for urban starvation, Cuba for agricultural transition, etc.)
