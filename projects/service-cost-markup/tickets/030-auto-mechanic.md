# Auto Mechanic — Auto Repair

The mechanic buys his own tools. He works in a bay with a lift. The shop charges $100-150/hour for labor. The mechanic makes $20-30/hour. The spread is the shop's overhead + profit. Simple enough in theory — but the dealership vs. independent distinction makes this interesting.

**Fundamental worker:** Auto mechanic / automotive technician
**Customer cost proxy:** Average repair bill (AAA and BLS track repair costs). Or average labor rate per hour charged by the shop.

**Interesting angles:**
- Dealership labor rates are 2-3x independent shop rates. Dealership mechanics are often paid flat-rate (by the book hours, not actual hours). The ratio is probably very different between the two.
- Modern cars require more specialized (and expensive) diagnostic equipment. Has the capital cost of running a shop eaten into the mechanic's share?
- The mechanic provides his own tools ($30,000-100,000+ investment). Does that investment show up anywhere in the ratio, or is it silently borne by the worker?
