# HVAC Technician — Heating & Cooling

HVAC is interesting because it has both a service component (repairs, maintenance) and a product component (installing a new system). A new HVAC system costs $5,000-15,000. The installation labor is maybe $2,000-3,000 of that. The rest is equipment and markup.

**Fundamental worker:** HVAC technician
**Customer cost proxy:** Average cost of an HVAC service call (for maintenance/repair) AND/OR average cost of a new HVAC installation. Pick whichever has better longitudinal data, or do both.

**Interesting angles:**
- The equipment cost is a huge component — this isn't pure service. How do you separate the worker's share of the labor portion from the equipment markup?
- Home warranty companies (American Home Shield, etc.) are a middleman layer that pays HVAC techs rock-bottom rates. Has their growth changed the ratio?
- Refrigerant regulations (R-22 phaseout) created artificial scarcity and price spikes. Did the technician benefit, or just the supply chain?
