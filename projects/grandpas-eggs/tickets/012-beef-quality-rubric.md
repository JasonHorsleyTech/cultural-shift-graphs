# Ground Beef — Quality Rubric

## What to research

Define what a "pound of ground beef" meant in ~1955 and what the quality-equivalent product is today. Build a multi-attribute quality rubric.

## Why ground beef

Ground beef is the best cut for this project because: (1) it has the longest continuous price data (USDA/BLS), (2) it has clear modern quality tiers (conventional → all-natural → grass-fed → organic grass-fed), and (3) it's the most universally purchased beef product. Steak cuts have grading systems (USDA Choice, Prime) that add complexity without adding much to the story.

## Research tasks

1. **1955 baseline:** What was ground beef in 1955? How were cattle raised? (Mostly grass-fed on ranches, finished on grain for a shorter period than today, no growth hormones — DES was approved for cattle in 1954 but adoption was gradual.) What was the fat ratio? What was a typical farm/ranch size?

2. **Quality attributes to score (minimum 5):**
   - Cattle diet (grass-fed/finished vs. grain-finished vs. feedlot grain-only)
   - Hormone use (growth promotants — DES 1954-1979, then other hormones)
   - Antibiotic use (prophylactic/sub-therapeutic vs. therapeutic only)
   - Fat content/lean ratio (80/20, 85/15, 90/10 — has the default changed?)
   - Cattle breed and raising conditions (pasture vs. feedlot, CAFO size)
   - Processing and additives (pink slime/LFTB, ammonia treatment)

3. **Modern quality-equivalent:** Is it grass-fed, grass-finished ground beef? Organic? A specific USDA certification level? What matches 1955 cattle-raising practices?

4. **Divergence timeline:** When did the feedlot system become dominant? When did grass-fed re-emerge as a premium product? Key dates: growth of CAFOs (1960s-1970s), lean/extra-lean labeling (1990s), grass-fed premium market (2000s), LFTB/"pink slime" controversy (2012).

## Data source hierarchy

1. USDA historical livestock production reports
2. Academic studies on cattle industry consolidation and feedlot history
3. USDA organic and grass-fed certification standards
4. Industry sources (National Cattlemen's Beef Association)

## Output format

Follow the **Type 2: Quality rubric** format from plan.md.

## What "good data" looks like

- Honest about the fact that 1955 beef had food safety disadvantages (less consistent inspection, no HACCP)
- Clear timeline of the feedlot transition
- Distinguishes between grass-fed (may be grain-finished), grass-finished, and organic — they're not the same thing
