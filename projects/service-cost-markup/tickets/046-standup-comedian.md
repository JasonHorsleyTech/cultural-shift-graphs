# Stand-Up Comedian — Comedy

A comedian stands on a stage and talks. The overhead is a microphone and a stool. This should be one of the simplest ratio calculations — but the comedy club model is surprisingly extractive.

**Fundamental worker:** Stand-up comedian
**Customer cost proxy:** Average comedy show ticket price + minimum drink purchase (many clubs require a 2-drink minimum, which is really part of the ticket price).

**Interesting angles:**
- Club comedians (not headliners) are paid $50-150/set for a 15-minute spot. The club charges $25/ticket × 200 seats = $5,000 in door revenue per show, plus drink revenue. The comedian's share of the total spend is tiny.
- Headliners (touring acts) negotiate guarantees or revenue splits. The ratio is probably much better for headliners.
- Netflix specials changed the economics — a comedian gets $1M+ for a special, but it replaces a year of touring revenue. Different ratio entirely.
- Podcasting as a new revenue stream: the comedian IS the platform. The ratio should be nearly 100% (minus hosting costs and ads platform cut).
