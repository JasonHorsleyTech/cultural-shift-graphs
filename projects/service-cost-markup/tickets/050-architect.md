# Architect — Building Design

Architects typically charge 5-15% of total construction cost. On a $500,000 building, that's $25,000-75,000. The work might take 200-400 hours. That's decent pay — but the question is whether the architect's percentage has held steady, grown, or shrunk as building costs have exploded.

**Fundamental worker:** Architect (residential or commercial — pick whichever has better data)
**Customer cost proxy:** Total building/construction cost (same denominator as the GC ticket, but a different worker's share of it).

**Interesting angles:**
- The architect's fee as a percentage of construction cost has been DECLINING for decades. More complex buildings, more engineering consultants, more code compliance — but the architect's slice is shrinking.
- CAD and BIM software made drafting faster. Did that reduce the architect's hours, or did it get absorbed into more complex designs?
- "Design-build" firms (where architecture and construction are one company) vs. traditional architect-then-builder. Does the model affect the architect's share?
