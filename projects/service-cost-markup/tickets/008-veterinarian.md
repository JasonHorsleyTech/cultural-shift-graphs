# Veterinarian — Veterinary Care

Vet care is fascinating because it has tracked human healthcare inflation almost exactly, but WITHOUT the insurance distortion (pet insurance is still relatively rare). If the ratio is collapsing similarly to human healthcare, it suggests insurance isn't the primary driver of healthcare cost bloat.

**Fundamental worker:** Veterinarian
**Customer cost proxy:** Average annual vet spending per pet-owning household, or average cost of common procedures (exam, vaccination, spay/neuter).

**Interesting angles:**
- Corporate consolidation: Mars Inc. (yes, the candy company) owns ~30% of US vet practices now. Has that changed the ratio?
- Vet school debt has exploded similarly to medical school. Vets have among the worst debt-to-income ratios of any profession.
- Pet spending has gone from "practical" to "emotional" — are people spending more because vet services cost more, or because they're buying fancier services?
