# Tax Preparer — Tax Preparation

The service that should cost almost nothing and somehow doesn't. The IRS already has most of your tax information. In many countries, the government just sends you a bill. The US tax prep industry exists largely because Intuit and H&R Block lobby against free filing.

**Fundamental worker:** Tax preparer (H&R Block employee, CPA doing individual returns, enrolled agent)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average cost of tax preparation (individual return). IRS has some data on this. The National Society of Accountants does annual surveys.

**Interesting angles:**
- The tax prep industry actively lobbies to keep the tax code complex. The "overhead" is partially manufactured complexity.
- TurboTax charges $100+ for software that does what a human used to do. Is the fundamental worker now the software engineer who built TurboTax? Or has the profession been hollowed out?
- Compare the ratio when a CPA does your taxes vs. when software does. The labor cost dropped to nearly zero but the price didn't.
