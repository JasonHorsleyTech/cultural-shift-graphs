# Total Cost of Use Cases Over Time

Spin-off from grandpas-eggs (angle-02).

Instead of measuring the cost of a PRODUCT, measure the cost of an ACTIVITY:
- "What does it cost to watch TV at home?" (TV + subscriptions + internet)
- "What does it cost to get from A to B?" (car + insurance + gas + parking vs. transit)
- "What does it cost to educate yourself?" (tuition + books + access codes + laptop)

The insight: products got cheaper but the total cost of the use case often went UP because of subscription layers, accessories, and adjacent costs that didn't exist before.

Complementary to grandpas-eggs but fundamentally different metric — measures the envelope, not the item.
