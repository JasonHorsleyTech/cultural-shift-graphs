# Ticket 025: Engineered Pandemic — Modeled Scenarios and Parameters

## What to research

What does an engineered pathogen capable of civilizational disruption look like? What have exercises and studies modeled?

## Specific data needed

1. **Johns Hopkins Center for Health Security exercises:**
   - **Dark Winter (2001):** Smallpox release scenario. How many infected, how fast, containment failure
   - **Clade X (2018):** Novel engineered pathogen. Modeled 150M global deaths. What were the parameters?
   - **Event 201 (2019):** Coronavirus pandemic exercise (prescient). Less relevant to "civilizational collapse" but useful for infrastructure strain modeling

2. **DHS National Planning Scenarios (2006):**
   - Scenario 2: Biological attack (aerosol anthrax)
   - Scenario 3: Biological disease outbreak (pandemic influenza)
   - Casualty estimates and response modeling

3. **RAND (2023) and other think tank modeling:**
   - What pathogen characteristics produce civilizational collapse?
   - Minimum CFR × transmissibility combination that overwhelms response
   - Incubation period: longer = more spread before detection

4. **The "pandemic that breaks infrastructure" threshold:**
   - COVID-19 (CFR ~1%) strained but didn't break infrastructure
   - At what CFR does healthcare collapse completely? (Probably 5-10%)
   - At what CFR do essential workers (power, water, food) abandon posts? (Probably 20-30%)
   - At what CFR does society itself collapse? (Probably 30-50%)

5. **Parameters for our scenario (30-50% CFR engineered pathogen):**
   - Transmission: airborne, R0 of 5-10
   - Incubation: 7-14 days (long enough for global spread before detection)
   - CFR: 30-50% (high enough for civilizational disruption)
   - Duration: 6-12 months of active pandemic
   - What does the mortality curve look like? (S-shaped — slow start, exponential middle, saturation)

6. **Toby Ord probability estimate:**
   - "The Precipice" rates engineered pandemic as 1-in-30 per century
   - What's his reasoning?

## Where to look

1. **Johns Hopkins CHS** — Dark Winter, Clade X, Event 201 after-action reports
2. **DHS National Planning Scenarios (2006)** — declassified/summarized
3. **RAND Corporation** — bioterrorism and pandemic modeling
4. **Ord, Toby — "The Precipice" (2020)**
5. **Inglesby, Tom** — biodefense policy papers
6. **WHO — Disease Outbreak News** — for real-world pathogen parameters

## Result format

Key Data Points table:
- Parameters of the "canonical" scenario we're modeling (CFR, R0, incubation, duration)
- Modeled casualties from each exercise/study

**Mortality Timeline** for a 30-50% CFR engineered pandemic:

| time_hours | cumulative_deaths_pct | deaths_pct_low | deaths_pct_high | primary_cause | source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Timeline should cover month 1 through year 2, with the pandemic itself lasting ~6-12 months and secondary mortality from infrastructure collapse following.

## What good data looks like

- Clearly defines the "canonical scenario" parameters (not just "bad pandemic")
- Uses exercise data (Dark Winter, Clade X) as anchoring points
- Distinguishes between direct disease mortality and secondary infrastructure-collapse mortality
- The pandemic mortality curve should have a different shape from other scenarios: S-shaped (logistic growth) rather than step-function
