# Eggs — Quality Rubric

## What to research

Define what a "dozen eggs" meant in ~1955 and what the quality-equivalent product is today. Build a multi-attribute quality rubric comparing the 1955 baseline, the 2025 cheapest option, and the 2025 quality-equivalent.

## Research tasks

1. **1955 baseline:** What was the standard egg production system in 1955? How were hens raised? What was the typical farm size? Were antibiotics used? What did "a dozen eggs" mean to a consumer walking into a grocery store?

2. **Quality attributes to score (minimum 5):**
   - Hen housing/stocking density (caged vs. free-range vs. pasture)
   - Antibiotic/hormone use in production
   - Feed type (natural forage + grain vs. all-grain vs. fortified commercial feed)
   - Nutritional profile (omega-3 content, vitamin levels, yolk color as proxy for carotenoids)
   - Shell quality / freshness indicators
   - Breed diversity (heritage breeds vs. commercial hybrids like Hy-Line)

3. **Modern quality-equivalent:** What specific modern product matches the 1955 egg? Pasture-raised organic? Pasture-raised non-organic? A specific certification level? Name specific brands/certifications as examples.

4. **Divergence timeline:** When did the US egg market split into quality tiers? When did "cage-free," "free-range," "pasture-raised," and "organic" labels emerge? Key dates: the rise of battery cages (1950s-1960s), the organic movement (1990s), USDA organic certification (2002), cage-free pledges (2010s).

## Data source hierarchy

1. USDA historical reports on poultry production practices
2. Academic studies on egg production history and nutritional comparisons
3. USDA organic standards documentation
4. Industry sources (American Egg Board, United Egg Producers)
5. Consumer advocacy and food journalism for modern tier definitions

## Output format

Follow the **Type 2: Quality rubric** format from plan.md.

## What "good data" looks like

- At least 5 scored attributes with specific, measurable descriptions (not vibes)
- Honest assessment — if 2025 cheap eggs are genuinely better on some attributes (consistency, food safety, availability), say so
- Clear definition of the quality-equivalent product that a price researcher can go look up
- Divergence timeline with specific dates, not "sometime in the 1980s"
