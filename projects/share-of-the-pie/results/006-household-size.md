# Result: Average Household Size, 1947–2024

## Source
- **Primary source:** U.S. Census Bureau, Table HH-6, "Average Population Per Household and Family: 1940 to Present." Current Population Survey, March and Annual Social and Economic Supplements, 1947 to 2025. Downloaded from https://www.census.gov/data/tables/time-series/demo/families/households.html
- **Date accessed:** 2026-04-29

## Methodology
- **Definition:** Average number of persons per household (all ages), computed by the Census Bureau as total civilian noninstitutional population in households divided by number of households.
- **Universe:** All occupied housing units (households) in the United States. A household includes all persons who occupy a housing unit — family members and unrelated persons alike. Excludes persons in group quarters (barracks, dormitories, nursing homes, prisons).
- **Source survey:** Current Population Survey (CPS) Annual Social and Economic Supplement (ASEC), conducted each March. 1947 is the first year of CPS household data.
- **Revised estimates:** For years where the Census Bureau published revised estimates incorporating updated population controls from the most recent decennial census (marked "r" — 1993, 2011, 2021), the revised values are used. The original unrevised values are noted in Gaps and Caveats.
- **2014 note:** The 2014 CPS ASEC used a split-panel design with redesigned income and health insurance questions. The household size estimate uses the full 98,000-address sample and is not affected by the income redesign.

## Gaps and Caveats
- **No missing years.** The CPS provides annual data for every year from 1947 to 2024. No interpolation was needed (contrary to the ticket's expectation that pre-1960 data might require decennial interpolation).
- **Revised vs. original estimates:** Three years have both original and revised Census estimates. Revised values (used above) incorporate updated population controls from the subsequent decennial census. Original values for reference: 1993 original = 2.63 (revised 2.66); 2011 original = 2.58 (revised 2.56); 2021 original = 2.51 (revised 2.54).
- **1953 dip:** The 1953 value (3.28) is noticeably lower than surrounding years (1952: 3.32, 1954: 3.34). This appears in the official Census data and may reflect sampling variability in early CPS surveys. It is not a methodology change.
- **1947 age breakdown unavailable:** The Census Bureau marks the under-18 and 18+ breakdowns as "N" (not available) for 1947, but the all-ages average (3.56) is reported.
- **Trend summary:** Household size was roughly stable at 3.3–3.4 from 1947 through the mid-1960s, then declined sharply from ~3.3 (1966) to ~2.76 (1980), continued declining more slowly to ~2.62 by 1990, and has been roughly stable at 2.5–2.6 since the mid-2000s.
- **Confidence: High** across the entire series. This is a straightforward CPS tabulation published directly by the Census Bureau with no estimation or interpolation required.
