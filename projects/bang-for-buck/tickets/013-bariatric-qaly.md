# Ticket 013: Bariatric Surgery — QALY Evidence and Long-Term Outcomes

## Objective

Find the best QALY estimates for bariatric surgery (gastric bypass and/or gastric sleeve) with focus on long-term outcomes and populations matching Bob's profile.

## What to research

1. **Cost-effectiveness analyses**
   - Published $/QALY ratios for bariatric surgery
   - QALY gain over lifetime (typically reported as total lifetime QALYs — need to annualize)
   - Typical time horizon: 20-30 year follow-up studies

2. **QALY gain by procedure type**
   - Roux-en-Y gastric bypass (RYGB) — more effective but more complications
   - Sleeve gastrectomy — less invasive, fewer long-term risks
   - Are QALY differences large enough to report separately, or average them?

3. **Long-term outcomes**
   - Weight regain rates (20-30% regain significant weight by 5 years)
   - Resolution of comorbidities (type 2 diabetes remission, hypertension improvement)
   - Mortality reduction
   - How do these translate to sustained annual QALY gains?

4. **Annualized QALY calculation**
   - If total lifetime QALY gain = 2.0-2.5 over 20 years, annual = ~0.10-0.13
   - But benefits aren't evenly distributed — larger in early years, diminishing with weight regain
   - Report a defensible annualized range

5. **Risks and complications that reduce net QALYs**
   - Surgical mortality (0.1-0.5%)
   - Major complications (10-15%)
   - Nutritional deficiencies, dumping syndrome
   - Mental health effects (some studies show increased depression/substance abuse post-surgery)
   - Revision surgery rates

6. **Adherence**
   - You can't un-have surgery — "adherence" is essentially 100% for the anatomical change
   - But post-surgical dietary compliance is 60-70%
   - How does non-compliance affect QALY trajectory?

## Where to look

1. **Tufts CEA Registry** — "bariatric surgery" or "gastric bypass" or "sleeve gastrectomy"
2. **Cochrane Reviews** — "Surgery for obesity"
3. **NICE Technology Appraisals** — bariatric surgery guidelines
4. **Key studies:**
   - Swedish Obese Subjects (SOS) study — 20+ year follow-up, gold standard
   - Longitudinal Assessment of Bariatric Surgery (LABS) consortium
   - Arterburn et al. — long-term outcomes and cost-effectiveness
5. **PubMed** — "bariatric surgery QALY" or "bariatric surgery cost-effectiveness"

## Output format

Use the intervention result format from plan.md, focusing on QALY evidence. Cost data goes in ticket 014.

## What "good data" looks like

- Lifetime QALYs AND annualized estimates (we need annual for the graph)
- Net of surgical risks and complications
- Data from patients with BMI 35-45 (Bob's likely range if obese) not just BMI 50+
- Long follow-up (10+ years) — short-term studies overestimate benefits due to weight regain
