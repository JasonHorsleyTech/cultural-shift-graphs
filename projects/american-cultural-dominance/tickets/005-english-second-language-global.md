# English as Second Language — Global Rates by Region

How many people worldwide are studying or speaking English as a second language, and how has that grown? This is the bluntest measure of linguistic Americanization — when a country's education system decides every child must learn English, that's a national-level capitulation to Anglo-American cultural dominance.

**Metric:** Percentage of population studying English as a foreign/second language, by world region, per decade.

**Data proxy:** UNESCO education statistics, British Council reports ("The Future of English"), EF English Proficiency Index (2011-present), national education ministry data on mandatory English instruction.

**Interesting angles:**
- Track when countries made English mandatory in schools — many did so in the 1990s-2000s (China 2001, South Korea 1997, etc.).
- Europe: English overtook French and German as the most-studied foreign language. Eurostat tracks this precisely.
- Asia: massive growth in English education spending — South Korea's "English fever," China's gaokao English requirement.
- Middle East / North Africa: English increasingly displacing French as preferred second language in former French colonies.
- Scandinavia and Netherlands have near-universal English proficiency — when did this happen?
- Compare English-as-second-language rates to French-as-second-language rates over time — the crossover point in various regions is a key inflection.
- The British Council estimated 1.5 billion English learners worldwide in 2015, up from ~300 million in 1960.
