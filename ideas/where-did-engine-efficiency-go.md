# Where Did 50 Years of Engine Efficiency Go?

Spin-off from the efficiency-paradox project.

Car engines got dramatically more efficient since the 1970s. But fuel consumption per capita barely budged. Where did all that efficiency go?

A Sankey diagram showing the allocation of cumulative engine efficiency gains across:
- Fuel savings (the small slice)
- Vehicle weight increase (~25% heavier)
- Horsepower increase (100 HP → 250 HP average)
- Safety equipment (crash structures, airbags)
- Electronics and accessories (AC, infotainment, sensors)
- Aerodynamic losses from taller/boxier vehicle shapes (SUV shift)

The punchline: we didn't waste the efficiency — we *spent* it on making cars bigger, faster, safer, and more comfortable. The slice that went to actual fuel savings is shockingly thin.

Data: EPA Automotive Trends Report has historical fleet average weight, horsepower, footprint, and fuel economy going back to 1975.
