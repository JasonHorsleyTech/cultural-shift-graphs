# Ticket 015: Dioxins (TCDD) → Cancer / Developmental Effects

## Objective

Research the timeline from scientific evidence that dioxins (particularly 2,3,7,8-TCDD) cause cancer and other health effects to binding regulation restricting dioxin exposure.

## What to research

1. **Early evidence date** — Chloracne from dioxin-contaminated products was observed in the 1940s-1950s. The Seveso disaster (1976, Italy) was a major exposure event. Agent Orange (used in Vietnam, 1961-1971) contained TCDD as a contaminant. When did credible scientific evidence establish that TCDD causes cancer in humans?

2. **Consensus date** — IARC classified TCDD as a Group 1 carcinogen in 1997. EPA's dioxin reassessment was notoriously delayed (started 1991, draft 2000, finalized 2012). NTP listing? WHO TEQ (Toxic Equivalency) system?

3. **First OECD regulation** — Dioxin regulation is fragmented: emission standards for incinerators, food contamination limits, environmental cleanup standards. The Stockholm Convention listed dioxins (2001/2004). What was the FIRST binding regulation that meaningfully reduced general population exposure?

4. **US regulation** — EPA's approach has been primarily through emission standards under the Clean Air Act (incinerators, pulp mills) rather than a direct dioxin ban. The EPA dioxin reassessment took 20+ years to finalize. Confirm key regulatory milestones.

5. **Industry opposition** — The chemical industry (particularly manufacturers of herbicides like 2,4,5-T) and the paper/pulp industry (dioxins from chlorine bleaching) fought regulation. Agent Orange liability created massive legal stakes.

6. **Exposure reduction** — Dioxin levels in food, breast milk, and blood have declined substantially since the 1970s due to emission controls. Data on trends?

## Where to look

1. IARC Monograph on dioxins (TCDD)
2. EPA dioxin reassessment history
3. Stockholm Convention — dioxins/furans profile
4. Seveso disaster literature and subsequent EU Seveso Directives
5. PubMed — "dioxin TCDD regulation history" or "dioxin cancer evidence"

## What "good data" looks like

- Clear timeline from early observations (chloracne 1940s-50s) through Agent Orange concerns (1960s-70s) to formal cancer classification (1997)
- Regulation as emission standards rather than a product ban — this is an exposure-limit case
- The 20-year EPA reassessment delay is itself a data point about regulatory lag

## Known pitfalls

- Dioxins are contaminants/byproducts, not intentional products. You can't "ban" dioxins — you regulate the processes that create them (incineration, bleaching, herbicide manufacturing). This makes the regulatory endpoint fuzzier than a product ban.
- The Seveso Directives (EU, 1982/1996/2012) are about industrial accident prevention, not dioxin exposure reduction per se. Don't conflate accident-prevention regulation with health-based exposure standards.
- Category: exposure-limit or international-treaty (Stockholm Convention). Choose based on which came first as binding regulation.
