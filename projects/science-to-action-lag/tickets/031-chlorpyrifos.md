# Ticket 031: Chlorpyrifos → Developmental Neurotoxicity

## Objective

Research the timeline from scientific evidence that chlorpyrifos causes neurodevelopmental harm in children to binding regulation banning chlorpyrifos. This is a modern case showing regulatory whiplash — ban, reversal, re-ban.

## What to research

1. **Early evidence date** — Chlorpyrifos (Dursban/Lorsban, manufactured by Dow Chemical) was one of the most widely used pesticides in the US. Columbia University's Center for Children's Environmental Health published studies from the early 2000s linking prenatal chlorpyrifos exposure to lower birth weight, reduced IQ, and developmental delays. EPA began re-evaluating chlorpyrifos around 2000. When did credible epidemiological evidence establish developmental neurotoxicity?

2. **Consensus date** — EPA's own scientists recommended banning chlorpyrifos in 2015-2016, finding no safe level of dietary exposure for children. This was an internal consensus within the regulatory agency itself. Academic consensus from Columbia, Mount Sinai, UC Berkeley studies?

3. **First OECD regulation** — The EU did not renew chlorpyrifos's approval (effectively banning it) in January 2020. Were other OECD countries earlier?

4. **US regulation** — EPA proposed a ban under Obama (2015-2016). The Trump EPA reversed course and denied the petition to ban (2017, Scott Pruitt). The 9th Circuit Court ordered EPA to ban chlorpyrifos (2021). EPA revoked food residue tolerances (effective February 2022). This regulatory whiplash is the key US story.

5. **Industry opposition** — Dow Chemical (now Corteva) aggressively lobbied against the ban, contributed to political campaigns, and funded industry-friendly science. The Pruitt reversal is directly linked to industry lobbying. Strong opposition.

6. **Current status** — Banned for food uses in the US (2022). EU ban effective 2020. Still used in some countries for non-food applications.

## Where to look

1. EPA chlorpyrifos regulatory documents (risk assessments, proposed ban, reversal, final ban)
2. Columbia Center for Children's Environmental Health studies
3. 9th Circuit Court ruling (2021) ordering EPA to act
4. EU non-renewal decision (2020)
5. Dow/Corteva lobbying records and political contributions
6. PubMed — "chlorpyrifos neurodevelopment children"

## Known pitfalls

- The US chlorpyrifos story is as much about politics and lobbying as about science-to-action lag. The EPA's OWN SCIENTISTS recommended banning it — the delay was imposed by political appointees overriding career scientists. This is a distinct mechanism from "science hasn't convinced anyone."
- EPA banned chlorpyrifos for RESIDENTIAL use in 2000 (citing children's health risk). The remaining fight was about AGRICULTURAL use and food residues. Note this two-stage regulatory arc.
