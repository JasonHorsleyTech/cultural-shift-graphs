# Health Insurance Ate Your Raise

Spin-off from share-of-the-pie exploration (Pass 2, Stress Test 3).

## The question

What percentage of total worker compensation goes to employer-sponsored health insurance, over time? "What share of your raise went to your insurance company?"

## Why it's interesting

Employer health insurance costs went from ~$200/year in 1970 (~2.7% of compensation) to ~$16,500/year in 2024 (~27% of compensation). Workers feel like their wages stagnated, but total compensation grew — the growth just got eaten by health premiums they never see. This is a standalone graph that tells a visceral story.

## Data sources

- Kaiser Family Foundation annual employer health benefits survey
- BLS Employer Costs for Employee Compensation (ECEC)
- BEA compensation tables

## Relationship to other projects

The share-of-the-pie project will include a compensation-adjusted line that touches this, but this idea is focused specifically on the health insurance wedge as its own story — a single dramatic line going from 3% to 27%.
