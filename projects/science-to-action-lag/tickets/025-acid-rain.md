# Ticket 025: Acid Rain (SO2 / NOx) → Environmental / Health Damage

## Objective

Research the timeline from scientific evidence that sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxide emissions cause acid rain (damaging forests, lakes, and buildings) to binding regulation limiting these emissions.

## What to research

1. **Early evidence date** — Robert Angus Smith coined "acid rain" in 1872. Modern scientific concern emerged in the 1960s-1970s: Svante Odén's 1968 paper in a Swedish newspaper, Gene Likens' work at Hubbard Brook Experimental Forest (1963-present, published 1972). When did credible scientific evidence establish the causal chain from SO2/NOx emissions to environmental damage?

2. **Consensus date** — NAS reports on acid rain (1981, 1983). The National Acid Precipitation Assessment Program (NAPAP) was a 10-year study (1980-1990). When did a major body formally confirm the problem required action?

3. **First OECD regulation** — Convention on Long-range Transboundary Air Pollution (CLRTAP, 1979, European context). US Clean Air Act Amendments of 1990 (Title IV, acid rain cap-and-trade program). Which OECD country or treaty first addressed acid rain with binding regulation?

4. **US regulation** — Clean Air Act Amendments of 1990, Title IV: created a cap-and-trade system for SO2 emissions. Phase I (1995) and Phase II (2000). This is widely considered one of the most successful environmental regulations in US history.

5. **Success story** — US SO2 emissions dropped ~90% from 1990 levels. Acid rain largely solved in North America and Europe. This is a case where regulation WORKED decisively.

6. **Industry opposition** — Coal-fired power plants and utilities were the primary opponents. The Midwest-vs-Northeast political dynamic (Midwest plants emitted, Northeast received the acid rain) complicated US politics. Moderate opposition.

## Where to look

1. Likens et al. (1972) — "Acid Rain" in Environment
2. CLRTAP text and UNECE history
3. US Clean Air Act Amendments of 1990 — Title IV
4. NAPAP reports
5. EPA Acid Rain Program history and emissions data
6. NAS reports on acid precipitation (1981, 1983)

## What "good data" looks like

- The 1960s-70s evidence emergence, 1979 European treaty, 1990 US legislation timeline
- SO2 emission reduction data showing dramatic decline after regulation
- The cap-and-trade mechanism as an innovative regulatory approach — it achieved reductions at far lower cost than industry predicted

## Known pitfalls

- "Acid rain" is caused by both SO2 and NOx, but SO2 regulation was primary. Focus on SO2.
- CLRTAP (1979) is a framework convention — the actual binding emission reduction protocols came later (Helsinki Protocol 1985, Oslo Protocol 1994). Determine whether CLRTAP itself counts as "binding regulation" or whether a specific protocol does.
- Category: international-treaty (CLRTAP) or exposure-limit (US Clean Air Act cap). Use whichever came first chronologically with binding provisions.
