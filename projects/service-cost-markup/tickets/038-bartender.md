# Bartender — Bar / Nightlife

A bartender pours $0.50 of liquor into a glass and charges $12 for it. The markup on alcohol is 300-500%. The bartender's time per drink is maybe 60 seconds. Where does the $12 go?

**Fundamental worker:** Bartender
**Customer cost proxy:** Average cost of a cocktail or drink at a bar, or average bar tab per visit.

**Interesting angles:**
- The drink markup funds the entire experience (rent in a high-traffic location, ambiance, entertainment, liability insurance). The bartender's share of the drink price is probably very small, but their hourly comp (wage + tips) might be decent due to volume.
- Craft cocktail bars charge $15-18/drink. Speed bars in clubs charge $8-10. The bartender at both might make similar wages. The ratio is very different.
- Nightclub bottle service: a $50 bottle of vodka sold for $500-800. The server/bartender interaction is minimal. Pure venue premium.
