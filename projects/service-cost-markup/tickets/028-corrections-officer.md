# Prison Guard / Corrections Officer — Incarceration

The cost of incarceration is $30,000-60,000 per inmate per year depending on the state. The guard watching them makes $35,000-50,000. A guard watches maybe 50-100 inmates. The math should make the ratio obvious — but private prisons, healthcare costs for inmates, and legal fees might tell a more complicated story.

**Fundamental worker:** Corrections officer
**Customer cost proxy:** Average annual cost of incarceration per inmate (BJS and Vera Institute track this). The "customer" is the taxpayer.

**Interesting angles:**
- Private prisons (CoreCivic, GEO Group) vs. state-run facilities. Private prisons are supposed to be cheaper. Are they? And does the guard's ratio differ?
- Inmate healthcare is one of the fastest-growing components of prison budgets. It's not going to guards.
- The US incarcerates at 5x the rate of most other developed countries. Has the guard's ratio changed as the system scaled up? Economies of scale, or diseconomies?
