# Bicycle — Quality Rubric

## What to research

Define what "a bicycle" meant in ~1955 and build a quality rubric. The explore phase expects this to be a "progress wins" case �� the modern cheap bike is better than the 1955 standard. Verify this.

## Research tasks

1. **1955 baseline:** What was the standard adult bicycle? A heavy steel-frame cruiser or 3-speed (Schwinn, Raleigh, etc.). Single-speed or 3-speed internal hub. Coaster brake. Steel rims. Heavy (~40-50 lbs). No quick-release wheels. What did it cost?

2. **Quality attributes to score (minimum 5):**
   - Frame material and weight (heavy steel 1955 vs. light steel/aluminum 2025)
   - Gearing (single/3-speed vs. 7-21 speed)
   - Braking system (coaster brake vs. rim/disc brakes)
   - Tire technology (bias-ply heavy rubber vs. modern compounds)
   - Component quality and shifting precision
   - Durability / repairability (steel frames last forever; modern cheap bikes use proprietary parts)
   - Accessories included (fenders, chain guard, lights were standard in 1955)

3. **Is this really "progress wins"?** Arguments:
   - **Yes:** A $300 2025 bike from a bike shop has aluminum frame, 21 speeds, rim brakes, modern tires — objectively better than a 1955 Schwinn on most performance dimensions.
   - **Nuance:** A $150 Walmart bike (BSO — "bike-shaped object") may actually be WORSE than a 1955 Schwinn in durability and repairability. The cheapest modern bike is arguably lower quality than the cheapest 1955 bike.
   - **The $300 bike shop bike** is probably the fair comparison to the 1955 Schwinn — both were "the standard bike a normal person would buy."

4. **Divergence timeline:** When did bike quality tiers emerge? The 10-speed boom (1970s), mountain bike revolution (1980s-1990s), and the current era of carbon fiber high-end vs. department store BSOs.

## Data source hierarchy

1. Sears/Montgomery Ward catalogs — Bicycle models and prices
2. Bicycle industry history (National Bicycle Dealers Association, Bicycle Retailer)
3. Schwinn company history (well-documented)
4. Academic/enthusiast histories of American cycling

## Output format

Follow the **Type 2: Quality rubric** format from plan.md.

## What "good data" looks like

- Acknowledges that the cheapest modern bike (department store BSO) might actually be worse than 1955's cheapest
- Distinguishes between department-store bikes and bike-shop bikes as two different "cheapest" tiers
- Quality-equivalent clearly defined: probably a $300-500 bike-shop hybrid/commuter
