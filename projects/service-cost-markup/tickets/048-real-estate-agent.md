# Real Estate Agent — Home Sales

The 6% commission on a home sale has been one of the most durable pricing structures in any industry. On a $400,000 home, that's $24,000 — split between buyer's and seller's agents, then split with their brokerages. The agent might keep $6,000-8,000 for a transaction that took them 20-40 hours.

**Fundamental worker:** Real estate agent
**Customer cost proxy:** Total commission paid on a home sale (buyer + seller side). Or just the percentage. The interesting number is the agent's keep rate (how much of the gross commission they actually take home after brokerage splits).

**Interesting angles:**
- NAR settlement (2024) blew up the buyer's agent commission structure. Has it actually changed anything yet?
- Redfin, Zillow, and other tech platforms were supposed to reduce commissions. The percentage has barely budged in 50 years. Why is this industry so resistant to disruption?
- The agent's actual hourly rate varies wildly — a hot-market agent closing 20 deals/year vs. a new agent closing 3. The "average" hides a lot.
- Commission on a $100,000 house in 1980 vs. commission on a $400,000 house in 2024 — the percentage is the same but the dollar amount has 4x'd. Did the work 4x?
