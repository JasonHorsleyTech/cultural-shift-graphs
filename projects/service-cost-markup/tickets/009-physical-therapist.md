# Physical Therapist — Rehabilitation

PT is one of the most hands-on, time-intensive medical services. A PT session is literally one professional spending 30-60 minutes with one patient. The ratio should theoretically be high, but insurance reimbursement has been squeezing PT practices.

**Fundamental worker:** Physical therapist
**Customer cost proxy:** Average cost per PT session (total — patient + insurance). Or total PT/rehab spending per capita.

**Interesting angles:**
- PT clinics are increasingly being bought by private equity and hospital systems. Has consolidation changed the ratio?
- Insurance reimbursement per PT session has been declining in real terms while patient copays have risen — the total pie may be shrinking while the PT's slice also shrinks
- The rise of PT assistants (PTAs) — cheaper labor doing similar work. Does that change the ratio or just who the "fundamental worker" is?
