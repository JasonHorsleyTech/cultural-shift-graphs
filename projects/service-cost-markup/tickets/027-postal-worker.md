# Postal Worker — Mail Delivery

The postal service is fascinating because it's a government monopoly (first-class mail) competing with private companies (FedEx, UPS) for packages. The "customer cost" is the price of a stamp or a package shipment.

**Fundamental worker:** Mail carrier / postal worker (the person who delivers to your door)
**Customer cost proxy:** Price of a first-class stamp over time (very clean, easy data). Or average cost of sending a package via USPS.

**Interesting angles:**
- The price of a stamp has gone from $0.03 (1950s) to $0.73 (2024). Mail carrier wages have gone up but by how much relative to the stamp?
- USPS is legally required to pre-fund retiree health benefits 75 years in advance (unique among federal agencies). This mandate alone accounts for most of USPS's "losses." It's institutional overhead imposed by Congress.
- Email destroyed first-class mail volume. The same workforce delivers fewer letters but more packages. How does that change the ratio?
