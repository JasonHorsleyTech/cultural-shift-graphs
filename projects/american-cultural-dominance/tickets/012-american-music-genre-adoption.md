# American Music Genre Adoption Globally

American-origin music genres have sequentially conquered the world: jazz (1920s-40s), rock and roll (1950s-60s), disco/funk (1970s), hip-hop/rap (1980s-present), and EDM/pop hybrids (2000s-present). Each wave replaced or absorbed local music traditions. Track when each genre reached mainstream adoption in different regions.

**Metric:** Timeline of when American-origin music genres achieved mainstream chart/radio presence in different world regions.

**Data proxy:** Music industry histories, national radio programming data, first local artists in each genre by country, IFPI data on genre market shares by country.

**Interesting angles:**
- Jazz: brought to Europe by WW1 troops, exploded in 1920s Paris. African-American art form became global via American military and cultural export.
- Rock and roll: British Invasion (1960s) was itself a re-export of American music. Track when rock arrived in Japan, Latin America, Soviet bloc.
- Hip-hop: arguably the most successful American cultural export of the late 20th century. French rap, German rap, Japanese rap, Brazilian funk — all derivative. When did each scene emerge?
- Soviet bloc: American music was officially suppressed but hugely popular underground. The black market for American rock records is a measurable phenomenon.
- Track the lag time: how many years between a genre's US emergence and its mainstream adoption in different regions? Is the lag shrinking over time? (Jazz took decades; K-pop-style cross-pollination happens in months now.)
