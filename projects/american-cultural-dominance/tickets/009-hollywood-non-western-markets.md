# Hollywood Market Share in Non-Western Cinema Markets

The non-Western story is more complex than Europe. India (Bollywood), Japan, South Korea, and Nigeria (Nollywood) have maintained significant domestic film industries. China imposed import quotas. But most of Latin America, Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and Africa are overwhelmingly dominated by Hollywood.

**Metric:** US-produced films' share of cinema admissions/box office in major non-Western markets (Japan, South Korea, India, China, Brazil, Mexico, Nigeria), per decade.

**Data proxy:** National box office databases, MPAA international data, UNESCO film statistics, national film commission reports.

**Interesting angles:**
- India: Bollywood produces more films than Hollywood but in a fragmented domestic market. Hollywood's share has been growing since economic liberalization (1991). Track the trend.
- South Korea: Maintained screen quotas (73 days/year required domestic) until US trade pressure halved them (2006). Korean cinema share dropped immediately.
- China: Foreign film import quota (34 films/year as of 2012). What does US share look like within that constrained space vs. what it would be unconstrained?
- Japan: Once had a strong domestic film industry (Kurosawa era). Track the decline of Japanese films' domestic share.
- Latin America: Most countries have no significant domestic film industry anymore. When did Hollywood become dominant in Brazil, Mexico, Argentina?
- Africa: Outside Nigeria/Nollywood, domestic film production is minimal. Hollywood and Bollywood compete for viewership.
