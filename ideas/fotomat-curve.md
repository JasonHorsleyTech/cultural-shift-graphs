# The Fotomat curve

A focused graph of platform shifts in the digital era (2000-2015): Kodak, Blockbuster, Borders, taxi medallions, newspapers' classified revenue. All have clean BLS data, all are fast collapses, all happened in roughly the same 15-year window.

## Why it's interesting
These are all "platform shift" displacements — not gradual tool improvements, but wholesale replacement by a different paradigm. The collapse curves should look strikingly similar despite being in completely different industries. If they do, that's a powerful visual argument that platform shifts have a characteristic shape regardless of industry.

## Where it came from
Spin-off from displaced-professions exploration (Pass 2). The tool-vs-platform distinction was the key insight — platform shifts are fast and total, unlike gradual tool improvements. A standalone graph of just the digital-era platform shifts could be built quickly with clean modern data.
