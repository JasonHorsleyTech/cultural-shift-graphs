# Ticket 010: Generic SSRIs — Cost and Access for Uninsured/Underinsured

## Objective

Determine the realistic annual cost of generic SSRI treatment for Bob, including all associated costs (not just the pill price).

## What to research

1. **Medication cost**
   - Generic sertraline, fluoxetine, citalopram — cash price per month (GoodRx, Costco, Walmart $4 list)
   - Range across pharmacies
   - Annual medication cost: low / mid / high

2. **Prescriber costs**
   - Initial doctor visit to get prescription (without insurance: $100-250)
   - Follow-up visits (typically 2-4/year for medication management): cost per visit
   - Community health center / FQHC costs (sliding scale based on income)
   - Telehealth options and costs (lower barrier than in-person)

3. **Lab work**
   - Any required blood work for SSRI initiation or monitoring
   - Cost without insurance

4. **Total annual cost scenarios**
   - Best case: FQHC + Walmart $4 generics
   - Typical: Community clinic + GoodRx discount
   - Worst case: Uninsured, no discount programs
   - With Medicaid (relevant for some at this income level)

5. **Access barriers specific to Bob**
   - Stigma around mental health treatment in lower-income populations
   - Wait times at community health centers
   - Prescription refill logistics (monthly pharmacy trip)
   - Geographic availability of prescribers

6. **Time commitment**
   - Hours per year: initial appointment + follow-ups + pharmacy trips
   - Minimal daily effort (swallow a pill)

## Where to look

1. **GoodRx** — current cash prices for generic SSRIs
2. **HRSA / FQHC directory** — community health center pricing structure
3. **Walmart $4 Prescription List** — verify SSRIs are included
4. **MEPS** — out-of-pocket prescription spending by income
5. **SAMHSA** — mental health treatment access barriers by income
6. **Kaiser Family Foundation** — insurance status and mental health coverage by income

## Output format

Use the intervention result format from plan.md, focusing on cost and access sections. Reference ticket 009 for QALY data. Produce the complete "Data for Graph" table combining QALY estimates from 009 with costs from this ticket.

## What "good data" looks like

- Actual pharmacy prices, not list prices — GoodRx cash prices are closest to what Bob pays
- Full cost including prescriber visits, not just medication
- Realistic access scenario for the 25th percentile (not just "go to your doctor")
