# Sanitation Worker — Waste Collection

Nobody thinks about garbage collection until it stops. The person driving the truck and hauling the cans is doing a physically demanding, essential job. The "customer" pays through municipal taxes or a private hauler bill.

**Fundamental worker:** Sanitation worker / garbage collector
**Customer cost proxy:** Average monthly waste collection bill (for private haulers) or waste management share of municipal budget per household (for public services).

**Interesting angles:**
- Private vs. public waste collection: many cities contract out to Waste Management Inc. or Republic Services. Has privatization changed the worker's ratio?
- Recycling programs added cost and complexity. Did they grow the pie or just add overhead?
- Automation (robotic arm trucks) means one driver does what used to take three workers. Did the one remaining worker's compensation go up proportionally? (Probably not.)
