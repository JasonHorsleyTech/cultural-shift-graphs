# Ticket 039: Compound Climate Risks — Multiple Tipping Points and Cascading Failure

## What to research

The climate cascade scenario isn't any single risk — it's the compounding of multiple risks simultaneously: heat, drought, sea level, AMOC collapse, permafrost carbon feedback, ecosystem collapse. What does the compound picture look like?

## Specific data needed

1. **Lenton et al. (2019) — tipping point interactions:**
   - Which tipping points trigger which others?
   - Is there a "cascade" where crossing one threshold makes others inevitable?
   - Their "hothouse Earth" pathway: what temperature triggers irreversible cascading?

2. **The compound scenario for the US at 4°C+:**
   - Southwest: megadrought (already in one), water crisis, agricultural collapse
   - Southeast: extreme heat, hurricanes, sea level rise, possible Gulf Stream effects
   - Midwest: more extreme weather (floods, heat waves), but potentially more precipitation
   - Coasts: sea level rise displacing 10s of millions
   - West: wildfires, water scarcity, ecological regime shifts

3. **US population displacement:**
   - At 4°C, how many Americans need to relocate? (Sea level, heat, water scarcity)
   - Internal climate migration: from where to where?
   - Infrastructure cost of relocation vs. adaptation

4. **Water crisis:**
   - Colorado River: already over-allocated, projections at 4°C
   - Ogallala Aquifer: depletion timeline
   - Western US water wars: agricultural vs. urban use
   - How many Americans lose reliable water access?

5. **Economic collapse pathway:**
   - Unlike other scenarios, climate doesn't kill directly at these levels — it kills through economic/agricultural/social decline
   - GDP impact at 4°C: Nordhaus, Hsiang et al., Burke et al. estimates
   - At what economic decline level does institutional failure begin?
   - Could the US experience a "Venezuela-like" gradual collapse from climate pressure?

6. **Constructing the decades-long mortality curve:**
   - This is a unique curve shape: not a sudden drop but a slow, accelerating decline over 50-100 years
   - Compound annual excess mortality from heat, food scarcity, water scarcity, conflict, displacement
   - Where does it flatten? Does it flatten? (Possibly not — it could keep declining for a century)

## Where to look

1. **Lenton et al. (2019) — Nature** — tipping point cascades
2. **Steffen et al. (2018) — PNAS** — "Hothouse Earth" trajectory
3. **IPCC AR6 — WG2** — impacts and adaptation
4. **Hsiang et al. (2017) — Science** — US county-level climate impacts
5. **Burke et al. (2015) — Nature** — temperature and economic output
6. **USGCRP — National Climate Assessment** — US-specific projections

## Result format

**Mortality Timeline** on a decades scale (years 10, 20, 30, 50, 100):

| time_hours | cumulative_deaths_pct | deaths_pct_low | deaths_pct_high | primary_cause | source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Key Data Points:
- `us_population_displacement_millions_at_4c`
- `us_water_scarcity_population_pct`
- `compound_annual_excess_mortality_rate`
- `institutional_failure_threshold`

## What good data looks like

- Presents this as the most uncertain scenario — decades-long extrapolation has enormous error bars
- The range should honestly be very wide: maybe 10-80% mortality over 100 years
- Notes the key difference from other scenarios: this is gradual, allowing adaptation (but adaptation has limits)
- Should inform a recommendation about whether this scenario belongs on the same graph or in a separate panel
