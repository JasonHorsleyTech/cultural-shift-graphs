# Baumol's Cost Disease Visualized

Spin-off from grandpas-eggs (angle-02).

Services vs. goods cost trajectories, isolated from the quality question. Baumol's cost disease: sectors where productivity can't easily increase (education, healthcare, haircuts, live performance) see costs rise relative to sectors where it can (manufacturing, agriculture, tech).

Well-established economics, but never graphed in an accessible, xkcd-style way for a general audience. Could be very clean — just two lines (goods vs. services in labor-hours) with annotations at key inflection points.

Simpler and cleaner than grandpas-eggs. Could serve as a companion piece that explains WHY services got more expensive without the quality-equivalent framing.
