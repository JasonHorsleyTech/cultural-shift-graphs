# Coca-Cola Global Expansion Timeline

Coca-Cola is arguably the most recognized American brand on Earth. Its expansion timeline is one of the cleanest measures of American commercial-cultural penetration — when Coke arrives in a country, American consumer culture has arrived. The company has operated in nearly every country on Earth (notable exceptions: North Korea, Cuba until recently).

**Metric:** Cumulative number of countries where Coca-Cola operates, by decade. Secondary: per-capita consumption by country over time.

**Data proxy:** Coca-Cola Company annual reports and corporate history archives, business history studies, country-level bottling franchise records.

**Interesting angles:**
- First international bottling: Cuba and Panama (1906). Track the expansion curve country by country.
- WW2: Eisenhower ordered Coca-Cola available to every US soldier. The company built 64 bottling plants overseas to supply troops — and kept them open after the war. Military expansion as commercial expansion.
- Cold War: Coke became a symbol of American capitalism. Soviet bloc countries eventually allowed it (Hungary 1968, Soviet Union 1985). Each entry date is a data point.
- Anti-Coke movements: India expelled Coca-Cola (1977-1993) over trade secret disclosure laws. Track countries that banned and then re-admitted Coke.
- Per-capita consumption data shows depth of penetration, not just presence. Mexico has higher per-capita Coke consumption than the US.
