# Ticket 006: Strength Training / Gym Membership — QALY Evidence and Cost

## Objective

Research the cost-effectiveness of regular strength training (2-3 sessions/week) for someone in Bob's profile. This includes gym membership as the primary access method.

## What to research

### QALY Evidence
1. **All-cause mortality reduction** from resistance/strength training — meta-analyses (distinct from cardio benefits)
2. **Cost-effectiveness analyses** for strength training programs
3. **Specific benefits for Bob's profile:** muscle mass preservation with aging, metabolic improvements, bone density, depression reduction
4. **Marginal benefit over walking** — does adding strength training to cardio produce additional QALYs?

### Cost Analysis
1. **Gym membership costs** — Planet Fitness ($10-25/mo), community center, YMCA (sliding scale). What's realistic for Bob?
2. **Home equipment alternative** — resistance bands ($15-30), used dumbbells ($50-100). One-time cost.
3. **Startup costs** — initial gear, any training/orientation needed
4. **No-cost alternatives** — bodyweight exercises (push-ups, squats). Does the evidence apply to bodyweight-only?

### Access and Barriers
1. **Gym proximity** for low-income areas — how far is the nearest affordable gym?
2. **Time commitment** — 2-3 sessions × 45-60 min + travel time
3. **Intimidation/knowledge barrier** — knowing what to do without a personal trainer
4. **Injury risk** — especially for obese, sedentary beginners

## Where to look

1. **Tufts CEA Registry** — "resistance training" or "strength training" cost-effectiveness
2. **Cochrane Reviews** — "Progressive resistance strength training"
3. **Key meta-analyses:** Shailendra et al. (2022), Momma et al. (2022) — strength training and mortality
4. **YMCA / Planet Fitness** — published membership rates
5. **BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey** — recreation/fitness spending by income

## Output format

Use the full intervention result format from plan.md, including the "Data for Graph" table.

## What "good data" looks like

- Mortality/QALY data specific to resistance training (not just "exercise" broadly)
- Cost estimates realistic for Bob (Planet Fitness, not Equinox)
- Adherence rates for gym-based programs (these are notoriously low)
