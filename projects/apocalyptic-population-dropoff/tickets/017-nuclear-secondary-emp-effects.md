# Ticket 017: Nuclear War — Secondary EMP Effects

## What to research

A full nuclear exchange includes high-altitude detonations that produce electromagnetic pulse (EMP) across the continent. This is a SECONDARY effect of nuclear war (in addition to blast, fallout, and nuclear winter) that affects areas far from blast zones.

## Specific data needed

1. **EMP from nuclear war vs. dedicated EMP attack:**
   - In a full exchange, EMP is a byproduct — some warheads detonate at high altitude
   - How does EMP coverage in a full exchange compare to a dedicated EMP strike?
   - Are there areas of the US that escape both blast AND EMP?

2. **Grid damage from nuclear-war EMP:**
   - What fraction of the grid goes down from EMP vs. from blast destruction of power plants?
   - Is EMP damage incremental on top of direct infrastructure destruction, or is the grid already gone?
   - Which grid components survive blast zones but are vulnerable to EMP? (Transformers, SCADA systems, substations)

3. **Vehicle and transportation effects:**
   - What fraction of vehicles are disabled by EMP? (Much debated — EMP Commission testing showed mixed results)
   - Fuel supply chain: refineries, pipelines, gas stations — all grid-dependent
   - Without vehicles, how does evacuation/migration work?

4. **Communication systems:**
   - What survives EMP? (Some military-hardened systems, some amateur radio)
   - Loss of communication compounds every other problem (no coordination, no information)

5. **Relevance to the nuclear war curve:**
   - Does EMP significantly change the mortality curve beyond what blast + fallout + nuclear winter already cause?
   - Or is it redundant — the areas that escape blast are going to face grid-down conditions anyway?

## Where to look

1. **Congressional EMP Commission reports (2004, 2008, 2017)**
2. **DTRA / Sandia National Labs** — EMP effects testing
3. **Metatech Corporation reports** — EMP infrastructure vulnerability (contracted by EMP Commission)
4. **Oak Ridge National Laboratory** — grid vulnerability studies
5. **NRC (Nuclear Regulatory Commission)** — nuclear plant EMP vulnerability

## Result format

Key Data Points table. Focus on:
- `grid_survival_pct_post_emp` — what fraction of grid survives EMP (on top of blast destruction)
- `vehicle_disablement_pct` — range of estimates
- `additional_mortality_from_emp` — incremental deaths attributable to EMP beyond blast/fallout/famine

## What good data looks like

- Clearly states whether EMP is additive or redundant in the nuclear war scenario
- Uses EMP Commission data but notes its limitations (testimony, not peer-reviewed modeling)
- The key conclusion should be: "EMP adds/doesn't add significantly to the nuclear war mortality curve because ___"
- This ticket may conclude that EMP is already captured in the grid-down consequences of nuclear war and doesn't need a separate mortality component
