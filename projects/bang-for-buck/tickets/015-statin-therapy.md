# Ticket 015: Statin Therapy — QALY Evidence and Cost

## Objective

Research the cost-effectiveness of generic statin therapy (atorvastatin, simvastatin, rosuvastatin) for cardiovascular risk reduction in Bob's profile. Statins are among the most cost-effective medical interventions known — they're an important benchmark.

## What to research

### QALY Evidence
1. **Cost-effectiveness analyses** for primary prevention statins in moderate-risk adults
2. **QALY gain per year** — cardiovascular event prevention, mortality reduction
3. **Population match** — Bob's elevated cardiovascular risk (sedentary, elevated BMI, likely borderline lipids) makes him a candidate for primary prevention
4. **Side effects reducing net QALYs** — myalgia (muscle pain, 5-10%), new-onset diabetes (small but real risk), liver effects
5. **Long-term data** — statins have decades of follow-up data

### Cost Analysis
1. **Generic statin cost** — atorvastatin, simvastatin are on Walmart $4 list. Monthly cost with/without discount programs.
2. **Prescriber costs** — annual checkup + lipid panel. Cost at FQHC vs. private.
3. **Lab work** — lipid panel baseline + annual monitoring. Cost without insurance.
4. **Total annual cost** — medication + provider visits + labs

### Access and Barriers
1. **Prescriber access** — requires a doctor visit and blood work, but no specialist referral
2. **Adherence** — statin adherence rates (generally 50-70% at 1 year). Why do people stop?
3. **Activation energy** — moderate (need a doctor visit to start)
4. **Time commitment** — minimal (swallow a pill daily, 1-2 doctor visits/year)

## Where to look

1. **Tufts CEA Registry** — "statin" cost-effectiveness (extensive literature)
2. **NICE Technology Appraisals** — cardiovascular risk management guidelines
3. **Cochrane Reviews** — "Statins for primary prevention"
4. **CTT (Cholesterol Treatment Trialists') Collaboration** meta-analyses
5. **GoodRx** — current generic statin pricing
6. **USPSTF** — statin recommendation and supporting evidence review

## Output format

Use the full intervention result format from plan.md, including complete "Data for Graph" table.

## What "good data" looks like

- QALY estimates for primary prevention (not just post-heart-attack secondary prevention)
- Net of side effects
- Cost that reflects Bob's actual out-of-pocket (generic + FQHC visits + labs)
- This intervention should be very cheap with high evidence quality — it's the "boring but effective" benchmark
