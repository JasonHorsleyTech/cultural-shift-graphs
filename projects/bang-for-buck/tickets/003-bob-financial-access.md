# Ticket 003: Bob's Financial, Access, and Time-Use Baseline

## Objective

Establish the financial profile, healthcare access, and time availability of an American at the 25th percentile of household income (~$30,000). This determines which interventions Bob can realistically access and what they'd cost him.

## What to research

1. **Income and expenses**
   - Median household income at 25th percentile (latest Census ACS)
   - After-tax take-home pay estimate
   - Typical monthly budget breakdown (housing, food, transportation, healthcare as % of income)
   - Disposable income after essentials

2. **Health insurance status**
   - % uninsured in this bracket
   - % on Medicaid
   - % with employer-sponsored insurance
   - Typical deductible/copay for those with insurance
   - Out-of-pocket healthcare spending

3. **Transportation**
   - Car ownership rate
   - Average commute method
   - Access to public transit

4. **Time availability**
   - Average work hours per week (including multiple jobs)
   - BLS American Time Use Survey data for this bracket: leisure time, TV/screen time, exercise time
   - Shift work prevalence

5. **Geography**
   - Urban/suburban/rural distribution for this income bracket
   - Walkability (sidewalk availability, pedestrian safety statistics by income area)
   - Provider density (mental health providers, gyms, community health centers per capita in low-income areas)

## Where to look

1. **Census ACS** — income, insurance, transportation, housing costs
2. **BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey** — spending patterns by income quintile
3. **BLS American Time Use Survey** — time allocation by income
4. **Kaiser Family Foundation** — insurance coverage and costs by income
5. **MEPS** (Medical Expenditure Panel Survey) — out-of-pocket healthcare spending
6. **Smart Growth America / GHSA** — pedestrian safety and walkability data by income

## Output format

Use the baseline profile format from plan.md. Key statistics table with metric, value, and source citation.

## What "good data" looks like

- Statistics for the bottom quartile or $25-35k income bracket
- Recent data (2020-2024, but pre-pandemic baselines also useful for time use)
- Budget data should be monthly or annual, clearly labeled
