# Regulation vs. Market: Where Does Conservation Actually Come From?

Spin-off from the efficiency-paradox project.

The efficiency paradox research revealed that domains with low rebound (water, refrigeration, lighting) are all heavily regulated — mandatory fixture standards, appliance efficiency rules, bulb bans. Domains with high rebound (computing, communication, air travel) have no demand-side regulation.

**The question:** Across all major resource categories, what fraction of conservation gains came from regulation vs. market forces? Graph it as a stacked bar per resource.

This is a different question than "does Jevons exist." It's "does conservation ever happen without the government forcing it?"

Data sources: EPA, DOE appliance standards history, CAFE standards timeline, Clean Water Act mandates, EISA 2007 lighting rules. Cross-reference with actual consumption data from EIA/USGS.
