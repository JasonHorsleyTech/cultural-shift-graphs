# Ticket 011: CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) — QALY Evidence

## Objective

Find the best QALY estimates for a standard course of CBT (12-20 sessions) for depression and/or anxiety, in populations approximating Bob's profile.

## What to research

1. **Cost-effectiveness analyses for CBT**
   - $/QALY ratios for CBT vs. usual care
   - CBT vs. SSRIs (head-to-head comparisons)
   - CBT + SSRIs vs. SSRIs alone (combination therapy)
   - Incremental QALY gain per year

2. **Short-term vs. long-term QALY profile**
   - CBT's distinctive feature: skills persist after treatment ends, reducing relapse
   - What's the annualized QALY gain if benefits last 2-5 years beyond treatment?
   - Compare: SSRIs produce benefits only while taking them; CBT produces lasting change

3. **For which conditions is CBT most cost-effective?**
   - Depression (most studied)
   - Generalized anxiety disorder
   - Insomnia (CBT-I is the gold standard — strong QALY data)
   - Does CBT for insomnia have different QALY estimates than CBT for depression?

4. **Population match**
   - Are there CEA studies in primary care or low-income settings?
   - Does CBT efficacy vary by education level, health literacy, or cultural background?
   - Therapist availability by geography

5. **Adherence / completion rates**
   - What % of people who start CBT complete a full course (12+ sessions)?
   - Dropout patterns (front-loaded in sessions 1-4)

## Where to look

1. **Tufts CEA Registry** — "CBT" or "cognitive behavioral therapy" cost-effectiveness
2. **NICE Guidelines** — depression and anxiety treatment guidelines include CEA
3. **Cochrane Reviews** — "CBT for depression," "CBT for anxiety"
4. **Key studies:**
   - Cuijpers et al. (multiple meta-analyses on psychotherapy for depression)
   - Layard & Clark (2014) — cost-effectiveness of psychological therapy
   - Annals of Internal Medicine (2019) — comparative depression treatment effectiveness
5. **PubMed** — "CBT QALY" or "psychotherapy cost-effectiveness"

## Output format

Use the intervention result format from plan.md, focusing on QALY evidence sections. Cost data goes in ticket 012. Report QALY as annualized gain (even if studies report over a treatment episode — convert to annual).

## What "good data" looks like

- QALY estimates from RCTs comparing CBT to usual care (not waitlist — waitlist comparisons inflate effects)
- Annualized figures, not episode totals
- Clarity on whether the QALY estimate accounts for lasting post-treatment benefits or just the treatment period
