# Blocked from deploy

Date blocked: 2026-05-04
Reason: research feels conclusion-driven, not discovery-driven.

## Concrete concerns

- **Cost methodology is misleading.** "Annual out-of-pocket cost" silently excludes the most relevant number for some interventions. Smoking cessation shows $150/yr (NRT amortized over 3 years) — but ignores the ~$1,500–3,000/yr a smoker saves by not buying packs. Net cost is wildly negative. The methodology choice quietly puts a thumb on the scale by treating "money saved by quitting" as out of scope.
- **Loaded framing in research.** Smoking cessation entry described as "the single most cost-effective health intervention in modern medicine" and "Highest QALY on the chart" reads like the conclusion was decided before the analysis.
- **Multiple questions tangled together.** "Bang for buck per Bob" mixes addiction recovery, mental-health treatment, fitness maintenance, and preventive care under one cost-per-QALY axis. These are arguably different questions and don't compare cleanly.

## What needs to happen before deploy

Pick one:

1. **Honest cost axis** — switch to net annual cost (intervention cost minus avoided spending). This will reshape the chart: smoking goes deeply negative, fast food avoidance similar. Ranking may flip.
2. **Reframe the question** — split into separate, more honest questions (e.g., "what does it cost to undo each addiction?" vs. "what does it cost to maintain health?"). Each gets its own graph.
3. **Stay with current methodology but show the asymmetry** — keep current cost axis but add a second "net cost after avoided spending" view, or annotate every entry with what's excluded.

Until one of these is resolved, do not deploy.
