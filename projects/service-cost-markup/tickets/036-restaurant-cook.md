# Restaurant Cook — Full-Service Dining

The person who makes your food. You pay $25 for a meal. The ingredients cost $7 (food cost is typically 28-35%). The cook makes $15/hour and spent 15 minutes on your plate. That's $3.75 of cook labor on a $25 meal. Where's the rest?

**Fundamental worker:** Line cook / chef (full-service restaurant, not fast food)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average meal cost at a full-service restaurant (BLS has restaurant CPI; Census has average check data). Or use National Restaurant Association data.

**Interesting angles:**
- Restaurant margins are famously thin (3-5% net). If the cook's share is small AND the restaurant barely profits, where is the money going? Rent, probably. And labor — but not cook labor. Management, servers, hosts, bussers.
- Compare to the server (ticket 037) — same meal, different fundamental worker. Who gets more of the customer's dollar?
- Fast food vs. full-service: the cook's ratio is probably very different. A McDonald's worker makes $12/hour assembling a $10 meal in 2 minutes.
- Ghost kitchens and delivery apps (DoorDash, UberEats) take 15-30% of the order. New middleman. Has it changed the cook's share?
