# Locksmith — Emergency Services

The locksmith is a fascinating case study in emergency pricing and trust. You're locked out of your house at midnight, you Google "locksmith," and someone shows up and charges $200-400 to spend 5 minutes picking your lock or drilling the cylinder. The skill took 30 years to learn and 5 minutes to deploy.

**Fundamental worker:** Locksmith
**Customer cost proxy:** Average cost of a locksmith service call (emergency lockout, rekey, lock change).

**Interesting angles:**
- The locksmith industry has a massive scam problem — call centers that dispatch untrained workers with drills who destroy locks, then charge inflated prices. The "locksmith" who shows up might not be a locksmith at all.
- Emergency vs. scheduled work: the ratio is probably very different. A scheduled rekey at a hardware store vs. a midnight lockout.
- This industry has almost zero institutional overhead. It's one person, one van, some tools. The ratio should be high. If it's not, it's because of the dispatch/referral middlemen capturing the customer contact point.
- How far back does data go? Locksmithing is ancient but standardized pricing data is probably hard to find before the internet era.
