# Government as Middleman

Spin-off from share-of-the-pie exploration (Pass 2).

## The question

What percentage of GDP passes through government hands (taxes collected + transfers paid out) before reaching individuals? Has this share grown, and what does that mean for the "share of the pie" people experience?

## Why it's interesting

Government's intermediary role has grown from ~25% to ~35% of GDP. Before you even ask "what share does the median person get," there's a prior question: "what share does the government take and redistribute first?" The growing wedge between gross output and what reaches individuals as market income is partly a story about redistribution scale, not just inequality.

## Data sources

- BEA NIPA tables (government receipts and expenditures)
- CBO budget and economic data
- FRED fiscal data series
