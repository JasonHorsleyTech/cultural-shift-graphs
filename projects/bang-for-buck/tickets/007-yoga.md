# Ticket 007: Yoga — QALY Evidence and Cost

## Objective

Research the cost-effectiveness of regular yoga practice for someone in Bob's profile. Yoga combines physical activity, flexibility, and mindfulness — a distinct intervention category from pure cardio or strength training.

## What to research

### QALY Evidence
1. **Cost-effectiveness analyses** for yoga interventions — search CEA Registry and PubMed
2. **Health utility improvements** — any studies using EQ-5D, SF-6D for yoga participants
3. **Specific outcomes:** lower back pain reduction, depression/anxiety improvement, balance/fall prevention, blood pressure reduction
4. **All-cause mortality** — any evidence linking yoga to mortality reduction (likely limited)

### Cost Analysis
1. **Studio classes** — typical cost per class ($15-25) and monthly unlimited ($80-200). Likely out of Bob's range.
2. **Community/donation-based yoga** — availability and realistic cost ($0-10/class)
3. **YMCA/community center yoga** — included in membership or low additional cost
4. **Home practice (YouTube/app)** — free to ~$15/month. Mat cost ($15-30 one-time).
5. **Realistic annual cost range** for Bob

### Access and Barriers
1. **Cultural/gender barrier** — yoga participation skews female, white, higher-income. Is Bob likely to start yoga?
2. **Physical limitations** — can an obese, inflexible beginner do yoga safely?
3. **Availability** — yoga studios in low-income areas, community options

## Where to look

1. **Tufts CEA Registry** — "yoga" cost-effectiveness
2. **Cochrane Reviews** — "Yoga for chronic pain," "Yoga for depression"
3. **NICE evidence reviews** — yoga for specific conditions
4. **PubMed** — "yoga QALY" or "yoga cost-effectiveness"
5. **Yoga Alliance** — industry statistics on pricing and demographics

## Output format

Use the full intervention result format from plan.md. If QALY data is too thin for a reliable estimate, say so — this intervention may end up as Tier 3 evidence.

## What "good data" looks like

- Any published QALY estimate for yoga (these are rare — even one solid study is valuable)
- Cost data for the cheapest realistic access method (not premium studios)
- Honest assessment of evidence quality — yoga research has significant methodological limitations
