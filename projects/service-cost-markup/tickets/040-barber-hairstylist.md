# Barber / Hairstylist — Personal Grooming

The control case. Barbering has existed for thousands of years. The overhead is minimal: a chair, some scissors, a mirror. The service is one person spending 20-30 minutes with one customer. If any industry should have a healthy ratio, it's this one.

**Fundamental worker:** Barber or hairstylist
**Customer cost proxy:** Average cost of a haircut (BLS has CPI data for haircuts and personal care services).

**Interesting angles:**
- Booth rental vs. employed: many barbers/stylists rent a chair from a shop for $200-400/week and keep everything they make. Others are employees. The ratio is very different.
- The "barber revival" — hipster barbershops charging $40-50 for a cut. Did the barber's share go up, or did they just work in a more expensive building?
- This should be one of the healthiest ratios on the graph. If it's NOT, that's a very interesting finding.
- How far back can we go? Barbering has been tracked since at least the early 1900s.
