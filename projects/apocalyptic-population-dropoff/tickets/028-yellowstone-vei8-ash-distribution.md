# Ticket 028: Yellowstone VEI-8 — Ash Distribution and Immediate Casualties

## What to research

If Yellowstone's caldera erupts at VEI-8 scale (full "super-eruption"), what happens in the first days to weeks? How much ash, where does it go, and who dies immediately?

## Specific data needed

1. **Eruption parameters:**
   - Volume of ejecta: VEI-8 = >1000 km³ of material
   - Last Yellowstone VEI-8: ~640,000 years ago (Lava Creek eruption)
   - Comparison: Mt. St. Helens (1980) was VEI-5 — Yellowstone VEI-8 is ~1000x larger

2. **Ash distribution:**
   - USGS modeling of ash fall from a Yellowstone eruption
   - Meters of ash in immediate vicinity (WY, MT, ID — within 500 km)
   - Centimeters of ash across the Great Plains
   - Millimeters of ash across most of continental US
   - Which population centers get how much ash?

3. **Immediate casualties (days 1-7):**
   - Pyroclastic flows: lethal within ~100 km radius. Population in that zone?
   - Ash collapse of roofs: >10 cm of ash collapses many roofs. Population in that zone?
   - Respiratory failure: inhaling volcanic ash causes silicosis and asphyxiation. Lethal dose?
   - Transportation: all air travel stops, roads impassable under ash. Evacuation impossible in affected zones.

4. **Infrastructure damage:**
   - Ash shorts out electrical transformers and blocks air intakes
   - Water contamination from ash fall
   - Weight of ash on power lines
   - USGS estimates for infrastructure impact zones

5. **Population in affected zones:**
   - Within 100 km (pyroclastic flow zone): population count
   - Within 500 km (heavy ash): population count (includes Denver, SLC, parts of CO/UT/MT/WY)
   - Within 1000 km (moderate ash): population count (includes Midwest breadbasket)
   - Eastern US: light ash but atmospheric effects

## Where to look

1. **USGS Yellowstone Volcano Observatory** — eruption modeling and hazard assessments
2. **Mastin et al. (2014)** — USGS ash-fall modeling
3. **Self, Stephen** — supervolcano effects research
4. **WEF (2012)** — volcanic catastrophe risk assessment
5. **Geological Society of America** — Yellowstone eruption history
6. **FEMA** — volcanic hazard planning

## Result format

**Mortality Timeline** for first 7 days:

| time_hours | cumulative_deaths_pct | deaths_pct_low | deaths_pct_high | primary_cause | source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Key Data Points:
- `pyroclastic_zone_population` — people within lethal flow radius
- `heavy_ash_zone_population` — people in >10 cm ash zone
- `light_ash_zone_population` — continental US under ash
- `immediate_deaths_day7` — total deaths in first week

## What good data looks like

- USGS ash distribution models are the primary source — they've mapped this
- Immediate deaths are probably lower than nuclear war (Yellowstone area is sparsely populated) — maybe 500K-5M in the first week
- The real mortality comes later from volcanic winter and agricultural collapse (ticket 029)
- Notes that Yellowstone VEI-8 probability is very low (~1 in 730,000 per year per USGS)
