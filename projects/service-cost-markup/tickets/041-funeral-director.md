# Funeral Director — Funeral Services

The "monopoly of grief." You're buying a service while emotionally devastated, you have zero price sensitivity, you can't shop around because the body is already at the funeral home, and social pressure pushes you toward expensive options. Classic exploitation conditions.

**Fundamental worker:** Funeral director / mortician / embalmer
**Customer cost proxy:** Average funeral cost (NFDA does a biennial survey going back to the 1960s at least). Include burial AND cremation paths.

**Interesting angles:**
- The funeral industry has been quietly consolidated by a few companies (Service Corporation International owns 1,900+ funeral homes). Has consolidation changed the ratio?
- Cremation has gone from 5% in 1970 to 60%+ today. Cremation costs 1/3 of burial. Has the shift to cremation changed the funeral director's ratio, or just shrunk the pie?
- The FTC "Funeral Rule" (1984) required itemized pricing instead of bundled packages. Did that change anything, or do funeral homes just nudge you toward the expensive line items?
- Jessica Mitford's "The American Way of Death" (1963) exposed the industry. Did it produce any visible change in the ratio?
