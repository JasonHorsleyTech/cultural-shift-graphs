# Ticket 030: PFAS (Forever Chemicals) → Immune / Cancer Effects

## Objective

Research the timeline from scientific evidence that per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) cause health effects (cancer, immune system disruption, thyroid disease) to binding regulation restricting PFAS. This is a major IN-PROGRESS case.

## What to research

1. **Early evidence date** — 3M and DuPont had internal evidence of PFAS toxicity from the 1960s-1970s. Public scientific evidence emerged later: the C8 Science Panel (community health study near DuPont's Parkersburg, WV plant) ran from 2005-2013 and found probable links between PFOA and six diseases. Earlier: Giesy & Kannan (2001) published on global PFOS contamination in wildlife. When did credible PUBLIC scientific evidence emerge?

2. **Consensus date** — The C8 Science Panel published findings from 2012. IARC classified PFOA as a Group 1 carcinogen and PFOS as "possibly carcinogenic" (Group 2B) in 2023. National Academies report on PFAS health effects (2022). EFSA lowered tolerable intake for PFAS (2020). Which serves as consensus?

3. **First OECD regulation** — Stockholm Convention added PFOS (2009) and PFOA (2019). EU PFAS restriction proposal (2023, pending). Denmark banned PFAS in food packaging (2020). US EPA set enforceable drinking water standards for PFAS (2024). Which is the first binding regulation that meaningfully reduces exposure?

4. **US regulation** — EPA proposed PFAS drinking water limits (2023), finalized (2024). Earlier: some states enacted PFAS limits independently (e.g., Michigan, Maine, Vermont). The US is still in early regulatory stages.

5. **Industry opposition** — 3M and DuPont concealed PFAS health data for decades. This is one of the most egregious cases of corporate concealment (comparable to tobacco). The "Dark Waters" story (Rob Bilott's legal battle against DuPont). Strong, sustained opposition.

6. **Current status** — Document where regulation stands as of 2026. Many OECD countries are in active regulatory processes. The EU restriction proposal would be the most comprehensive action globally.

## Where to look

1. C8 Science Panel reports (2012-2013)
2. IARC Monographs on PFOA and PFOS (2023)
3. National Academies report on PFAS (2022)
4. EPA PFAS regulatory actions (drinking water standards, 2024)
5. Stockholm Convention PFOS listing (2009) and PFOA listing (2019)
6. EFSA PFAS risk assessment (2020)
7. Rob Bilott, "Exposure" — DuPont PFAS cover-up

## What "good data" looks like

- The gap between INTERNAL industry knowledge (1960s-70s) and PUBLIC scientific evidence (2000s-2010s) is itself a sub-lag worth documenting
- regulation_status: in-progress. Regulation is actively happening but far from complete.
- The 2024 EPA drinking water standard as a landmark US regulatory moment
- Stockholm Convention additions as the international regulatory framework

## Known pitfalls

- "PFAS" is a class of ~12,000+ chemicals, not a single substance. Regulation is often substance-specific (PFOA, PFOS) rather than class-wide. An EU proposal to restrict ALL PFAS as a class (2023) would be unprecedented.
- Internal industry knowledge (3M, DuPont) is distinct from PUBLIC scientific evidence. Use the public evidence date for early_evidence_year, but note the corporate concealment timeline in judgment calls.
- Category: This straddles substance-ban (Stockholm Convention), exposure-limit (drinking water standards), and international-treaty. Choose based on the first binding action.
- industry_opposition: strong.
