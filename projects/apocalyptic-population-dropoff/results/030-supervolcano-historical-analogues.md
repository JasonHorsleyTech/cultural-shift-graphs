# Result: Supervolcano — Historical Eruptions and Population Effects

## Summary

Historical volcanic eruptions provide a scaling ladder from VEI-6 (Pinatubo, Krakatoa) through VEI-7 (Tambora) to VEI-8 (Toba, Oruanui) for calibrating supervolcano mortality curves. The critical finding is that climate impact does NOT scale linearly with eruption magnitude — aerosol saturation effects mean a VEI-8 eruption produces proportionally less cooling per unit of ejected material than a VEI-7. Tambora (VEI-7) caused ~0.5-1.0°C global cooling and killed ~71,000-117,000 people; Toba (VEI-8) likely caused 2-4°C global cooling lasting 3-6 years, but the once-popular genetic bottleneck theory (population crash to ~10,000) is now largely disputed by archaeological and genomic evidence.

## Key Scaling Insights

**Volume scales ~10x per VEI level, but climate impact scales sub-linearly.** Pinatubo (VEI-6) injected ~20 Tg SO2 and caused ~0.5°C cooling. Tambora (VEI-7) injected ~60 Tg SO2 (3x more) but caused only ~0.7°C cooling (1.4x more). This sub-linearity becomes more extreme at VEI-8 scales due to aerosol saturation:

1. **Larger eruptions produce larger aerosol droplets.** Per unit mass, larger droplets are less effective at reflecting sunlight.
2. **Larger droplets settle out faster**, reducing atmospheric residence time from ~2 years (Pinatubo-scale) to possibly less, limiting cumulative forcing.
3. **Self-lofting and coagulation effects** in massive aerosol clouds cause clumping and faster fallout.

This means extrapolating linearly from Pinatubo (0.5°C per 20 Tg SO2) to a VEI-8 eruption would dramatically overestimate cooling. The revised Toba models (Timmreck et al. 2012) show ~3.5°C global cooling rather than the 10-15°C originally proposed by Rampino & Self (1992).

## Toba Bottleneck — Current State of the Debate

The Toba genetic bottleneck hypothesis (Ambrose 1998) proposed that the Toba eruption reduced the global human population to ~3,000-10,000 breeding pairs. The evidence has shifted significantly:

**Against the bottleneck:**
- Genetic analyses do not detect a bottleneck coinciding with Toba's timing (~74 kya). The observed bottleneck in non-African populations appears at ~40-60 kya, not 74 kya.
- Archaeological sites in India (Jwalapuram), South Africa (Pinnacle Point), and East Africa show continuous human occupation through the Toba eruption with no clear occupational hiatus.
- John Hawks (2021) summarizes: "The so-called Toba bottleneck simply didn't happen."

**In partial support:**
- The PNAS 2021 study (Yost et al.) showed Toba caused significant regional climate disruption, with "climate shelters" in some areas. This supports a population stress event without necessarily supporting near-extinction.
- Some populations (particularly in South/Southeast Asia closest to the eruption) likely experienced severe localized impacts.

**Current consensus:** Toba caused significant volcanic winter and regional ecological disruption, but it did not nearly wipe out humanity. The genetic bottleneck predates or postdates Toba and is likely related to the Out-of-Africa migration dynamics, not a volcanic catastrophe.

## Implications for Yellowstone VEI-8 Modeling

The scaling ladder suggests a Yellowstone VEI-8 eruption would cause:
- **Global cooling:** 2-4°C for 3-6 years (anchored to revised Toba models, not linear extrapolation)
- **Regional cooling (US/Canada):** Potentially 5-10°C in the first 1-2 years
- **Crop impacts:** Severe but not total — likely 30-50% reduction globally for 2-3 growing seasons, with near-total loss in heavily ash-affected US regions
- **Direct deaths from ash/pyroclastic flows:** Concentrated within ~100 km of caldera (covered in ticket 028)
- **The key mortality driver would be agricultural disruption**, not the eruption itself — similar to Tambora but at a much larger scale

The fact that the Toba bottleneck is now disputed is important for the graph: it means a VEI-8 eruption is a severe civilization-stressor but probably not a near-extinction event for a technologically advanced species with global food trade networks (though those networks would be severely disrupted).

## Data Gaps

- **Tambora indirect death toll varies enormously** (49,000 to 107,000+) depending on attribution methodology. The "Year Without Summer" deaths are particularly hard to attribute because multiple factors (Napoleonic War aftermath, pre-existing poverty) contributed to 1816 mortality.
- **Krakatoa crop impact is poorly quantified** at the global level. Regional reports exist but no systematic global agricultural assessment was conducted.
- **Toba SO2 emissions are unknown.** Climate models for Toba must assume SO2 injection, and estimates range from 200 Tg to 5000+ Tg, creating enormous uncertainty in the cooling estimate.
- **Oruanui population effects are unmeasurable** — no humans lived in New Zealand at the time. It is useful only for climate modeling comparison.
- **The aerosol saturation threshold is poorly constrained.** At what SO2 injection level does the sub-linear scaling become dominant? Models suggest somewhere between 50-200 Tg, but this has never been observed empirically.
- **No VEI-8 eruption has occurred in the era of modern agriculture.** All mortality estimates for agricultural impacts are extrapolations from VEI-7 (Tambora) scaled up, combined with crop modeling from nuclear winter studies.
