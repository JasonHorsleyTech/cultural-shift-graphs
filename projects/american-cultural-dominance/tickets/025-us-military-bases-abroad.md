# US Military Bases and Troop Presence Abroad

US military bases are cultural beachheads. Wherever American troops are stationed, American food, music, media, language, and consumer goods follow. Armed Forces Network (AFN) broadcasts American TV and radio to bases worldwide — and local populations tune in. Base towns develop American-style businesses. Troops marry locals. The cultural footprint far exceeds the military mission.

**Metric:** Number of US military bases/installations abroad and total troops stationed overseas, by decade (1940-present).

**Data proxy:** Department of Defense Base Structure Reports, DMDC (Defense Manpower Data Center) personnel statistics, David Vine's "Base Nation" research, SIPRI military expenditure data.

**Interesting angles:**
- Pre-WW2: minimal overseas presence (Philippines, Panama Canal Zone, a few others).
- WW2 peak: millions of troops in Europe, Pacific, North Africa. Many bases became permanent.
- Cold War: ~800 bases in 70+ countries at peak. Massive cultural footprint in Germany, Japan, South Korea, Italy, UK.
- Post-Cold War drawdown: base count declined but presence remained enormous by any other country's standards.
- Germany: tens of thousands of US troops continuously since 1945. Multiple generations of Germans grew up near American bases. Track the cultural impact.
- Japan/Okinawa: American bases remain a major political issue. The cultural influence (American food, music, fashion in base-adjacent communities) is well-documented.
- South Korea: USFK presence since 1950. "Itaewon" (the neighborhood near Yongsan base in Seoul) became synonymous with American culture in Korea.
- AFN broadcasting: American TV and radio available free in countries with US bases. Local viewership data if available.
