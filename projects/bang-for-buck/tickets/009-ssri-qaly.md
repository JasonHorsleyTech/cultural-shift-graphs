# Ticket 009: Generic SSRIs — QALY Evidence for Depression

## Objective

Find the best QALY estimates for generic SSRI treatment (sertraline, fluoxetine, citalopram) for depression, focusing on populations that approximate Bob's profile.

## What to research

1. **Cost-effectiveness analyses** for SSRIs vs. placebo or usual care
   - $/QALY ratios (these give us both cost and QALY gain)
   - Incremental QALY gain over no treatment
   - Net QALYs (accounting for side effects)

2. **QALY gain magnitude**
   - Typical incremental QALY per year of SSRI treatment for moderate depression
   - How does this vary by depression severity?
   - Long-term vs. short-term QALY profiles (do benefits persist after discontinuation?)

3. **Side effects that reduce net QALYs**
   - Sexual dysfunction (25-73% prevalence) — QALY impact
   - Weight gain — QALY impact (especially relevant for already-obese Bob)
   - Emotional blunting — harder to quantify but frequently reported
   - Withdrawal/discontinuation syndrome

4. **Population match**
   - Are there CEA studies specifically in lower-income or primary-care populations?
   - Does depression treatment efficacy differ by income bracket?

5. **Adherence context**
   - 6-month and 12-month adherence rates for SSRIs
   - Reasons for discontinuation (side effects vs. feeling better vs. cost)

## Where to look

1. **Tufts CEA Registry** — "SSRI" or "antidepressant" or "sertraline" cost-effectiveness
2. **NICE Technology Appraisals** — antidepressant guidelines include CEA data
3. **Cochrane Reviews** — "Antidepressants versus placebo"
4. **Key studies:**
   - Simon et al. (2006) — cost-effectiveness of depression treatment in primary care
   - STAR*D trial data — real-world SSRI effectiveness and costs
   - Annals of Internal Medicine (2019) — comparative effectiveness of depression treatments
5. **PubMed** — "SSRI QALY" or "antidepressant cost-effectiveness"

## Output format

Use the intervention result format from plan.md. Focus on QALY sections. Cost data goes in ticket 010. Fill in the "Data for Graph" table with QALY estimates only; leave cost fields for ticket 010 to complete.

## What "good data" looks like

- QALY estimates from RCTs or meta-analyses (pharma CEA literature is the strongest in all of medicine)
- Net QALYs accounting for side effects, not just symptom reduction
- Clear distinction between "QALY gain for clinically depressed patients" and "QALY gain for mildly depressed/dysthymic"
- Bob may or may not be clinically depressed — use the base rate from ticket 002
