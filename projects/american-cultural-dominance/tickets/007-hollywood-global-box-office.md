# Hollywood Global Box Office Share

The most obvious metric and one of the longest-running. Before WW1, French (Pathé, Gaumont), Danish (Nordisk), Italian, and German studios dominated global cinema. By the 1920s Hollywood had crushed all of them. That dominance has never been seriously challenged except in countries with deliberate protectionist policies (India, South Korea, China).

**Metric:** US-produced films' share of global box office revenue, per decade (or per 5-year period where data is richer).

**Data proxy:** MPAA annual reports (1920s-present), UNESCO Institute for Statistics film data, European Audiovisual Observatory, national box office databases (Rentrak/Comscore for modern era).

**Interesting angles:**
- Pre-WW1: European cinema dominated. WW1 destroyed European production capacity while Hollywood boomed — the war was the catalyst.
- 1920s-1930s: Hollywood established the studio system and dominated globally. Sound film (1927) gave English-language films an advantage.
- WW2: Hollywood films became propaganda tools and cultural exports to Allied nations.
- 1960s-70s: brief dip as New Wave movements (French, Japanese, Brazilian) gained international attention, but market share barely moved.
- 2000s-2010s: China's box office exploded but with heavy local content quotas — US films still dominate everywhere that doesn't actively restrict them.
- Track the countries where Hollywood does NOT dominate (India, South Korea, Japan partially) — what do they have in common?
