# Airline Pilot — Commercial Aviation

Pilots are highly skilled, highly trained, and responsible for hundreds of lives per flight. They used to be among the best-compensated workers in any industry. Then deregulation happened.

**Fundamental worker:** Commercial airline pilot (mainline carrier)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average domestic airfare (DOT has this going back to the 1970s, maybe further for CAB-era data).

**Interesting angles:**
- Pre-deregulation (before 1978): fares were high, pilots were very well paid. Post-deregulation: fares dropped but so did pilot pay. Did the ratio change, or did both sides shrink together?
- Regional airline pilots made $20,000-25,000/year as recently as 2010. They fly the same passengers who paid $300 for a ticket. The ratio for regional vs. mainline is a whole different story.
- Post-Colgan Air crash (2009), first officer minimums were raised to 1,500 hours. Did that improve pilot compensation or just create a barrier to entry?
- Pilot shortage post-COVID — wages have spiked. Is the ratio recovering?
