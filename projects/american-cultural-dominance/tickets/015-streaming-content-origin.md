# US Content Share on Global Streaming Platforms

Streaming platforms (Netflix, Amazon Prime, Disney+, Apple TV+) distribute content globally with no screen quotas, no broadcast regulations, and algorithmic recommendation. What percentage of the most-watched content on these platforms in non-US markets is American-produced?

**Metric:** US-produced content's share of top 10/most-watched lists on major streaming platforms in non-US markets, by year (2015-present).

**Data proxy:** Netflix weekly top 10 data (public since 2021), Parrot Analytics demand data, Ampere Analysis streaming reports, FlixPatrol global streaming charts.

**Interesting angles:**
- Netflix publishes weekly top 10 lists by country. Compare US content share across countries — is it higher in Latin America than in South Korea?
- Netflix has invested heavily in local content (Korean, Turkish, Indian, Spanish). Has this reduced US dominance on the platform, or does US content still dominate viewership?
- "Squid Game" moment: a Korean show became Netflix's #1 globally (2021). Is this a meaningful trend or an outlier?
- Disney+ is almost entirely American content (Marvel, Star Wars, Pixar). Track its market share growth in non-US markets as a pure measure of American content demand.
- Compare streaming content origin to broadcast TV content origin in the same countries — is streaming more or less American-dominated?
- EU AVMS Directive (2018) requires 30% European content on streaming platforms. Track compliance and whether it affects viewership patterns.
