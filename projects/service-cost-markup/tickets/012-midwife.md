# Midwife — Childbirth

Same service as an OB-GYN delivering in a hospital, radically different cost structure. A midwife-attended birth costs $2,000-5,000. A hospital birth costs $15,000-30,000. The "fundamental worker" (the person who catches the baby) is doing roughly the same job.

**Fundamental worker:** Certified nurse-midwife (CNM)
**Customer cost proxy:** Total cost of a midwife-attended birth (home or birth center). Compare against total cost of a hospital birth with the OB-GYN's salary as the fundamental worker share.

**Interesting angles:**
- This is one of the clearest natural experiments: same service, same outcome (for low-risk births), wildly different cost structures. The ratio difference tells you exactly how much hospital overhead adds.
- Midwifery was nearly eliminated in the US by the 1950s-60s (AMA lobbied against it). Its resurgence since the 1980s is a story about a low-overhead model re-entering a bloated market.
- How does the midwife's ratio compare to the OB-GYN's ratio? The midwife probably keeps a higher share of a smaller pie.
