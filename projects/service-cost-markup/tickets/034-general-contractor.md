# General Contractor — Home Construction

When you build a house, the general contractor is the "fundamental worker" in the sense that they manage the project — but they also hire all the subcontractors (plumbers, electricians, framers, roofers). The GC might take 10-20% of the total build cost as their fee, while coordinating hundreds of thousands of dollars of labor and materials.

**Fundamental worker:** General contractor (residential)
**Customer cost proxy:** Median new home construction cost (Census Bureau tracks this) or average cost per square foot for residential construction.

**Interesting angles:**
- The GC is a manager, not a tradesperson. Is the "fundamental worker" the GC, or the collection of tradespeople? This ticket should probably track BOTH — the GC's share of the total build cost, and the total labor share (all trades combined) vs. the home price.
- Housing costs have exploded. Is it land, materials, labor, or overhead? What share of a new home's cost is actual construction labor?
- Regulatory costs (permits, inspections, code compliance) have grown. How much of the home price is now regulatory overhead?
