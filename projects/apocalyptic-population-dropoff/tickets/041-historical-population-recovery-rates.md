# Ticket 041: Historical Population Recovery Rates Post-Catastrophe

## What to research

After a major population decline, how fast does recovery happen? This shapes the right end of every curve — does it flatten, or does it start climbing back?

## Specific recoveries to examine

1. **Post-Black Death Europe (1350-1500):**
   - Population dropped 30-60% by 1350
   - Recovery to pre-plague levels: ~1500 (150 years)
   - Rate: ~0.3-0.5% annual growth during recovery
   - Key factors: labor scarcity → higher wages → earlier marriage → higher fertility

2. **Post-WWII Germany and Japan:**
   - Germany lost ~8-10% of population; Japan lost ~3-4%
   - Infrastructure devastated
   - Recovery was rapid — population growth within 5-10 years
   - BUT: massive external aid (Marshall Plan), intact global economy
   - Not a good analogue for post-collapse (no external aid)

3. **Post-Khmer Rouge Cambodia (1979+):**
   - ~25% population loss
   - Baby boom in 1980s (population growth rate ~3% per year)
   - But persistent poverty and institutional damage for decades
   - Better analogue: less external aid than Germany/Japan

4. **Post-1918 flu:**
   - Relatively rapid recovery — the flu killed 0.5-1% of US population, society was intact
   - Baby boom in 1920s
   - Not analogous to civilizational collapse but useful for pure pandemic recovery

5. **Ireland post-famine (1851-present):**
   - Population NEVER recovered: 8.2M (1841) → 4.4M (1901) → 4.9M (2016)
   - Key lesson: emigration can prevent recovery even when conditions improve
   - But: post-collapse US has nowhere to emigrate TO

6. **Pre-industrial baseline growth rates:**
   - Pre-industrial population growth: ~0.1-0.5% per year
   - Constrained by mortality (especially infant/child mortality)
   - A post-collapse US would have pre-industrial mortality → pre-industrial growth rates
   - Time to recover from 50% loss at 0.3% growth: ~230 years. From 90% loss: ~770 years.

## Result format

Key Data Points for each recovery:

| event | population_loss_pct | recovery_rate_annual_pct | time_to_pre_crisis_level_years | external_aid | key_factor |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## What good data looks like

- Identifies which recoveries had external aid (Germany, Japan) vs. not (Black Death, Ireland) — post-collapse US would have no external aid at global scale
- The Ireland case is critical: population CAN fail to recover if structural conditions change
- Pre-industrial growth rates (~0.3%) provide the baseline for post-collapse recovery
- Notes that for our graph's 10-year timeframe, recovery is minimal — the curve stays flat, not climbing
