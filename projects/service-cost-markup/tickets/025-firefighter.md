# Firefighter — Fire Protection

Municipal service paid for by property taxes. Nobody argues firefighters are overpaid. Nobody argues fire protection is unnecessary. So if the ratio is changing, the story is purely about institutional growth — more administration, more equipment costs, more pension obligations.

**Fundamental worker:** Firefighter (career, not volunteer)
**Customer cost proxy:** Fire department share of municipal budget, divided by households served. Census of Governments tracks municipal spending categories. BLS has firefighter compensation data.

**Interesting angles:**
- Firefighter pension obligations are a growing share of fire department budgets in many cities. The pension isn't going to active firefighters — it's going to retired ones. Does that count as "fundamental worker compensation" or overhead?
- Fire calls have been declining for decades (better building codes, fewer smokers). But fire department budgets haven't declined. Where's the money going? Many departments have shifted to EMS calls — 70%+ of calls are now medical, not fire.
- Compare to police on the same basis (same municipality, same tax base). Which service has grown faster relative to the frontline worker?
