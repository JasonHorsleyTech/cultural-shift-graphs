# Taxi / Rideshare Driver — Ground Transportation

The most visible disruption story in modern labor. Uber and Lyft promised to cut out the middleman (medallion system, dispatch, taxi company overhead). Did they? Or did they just become the new middleman?

**Fundamental worker:** Taxi driver (pre-2010) / rideshare driver (post-2010)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average fare for a standardized trip (BLS has taxi fare CPI data). For rideshare era, average Uber/Lyft fare.

**Interesting angles:**
- Pre-Uber: taxi medallions in NYC cost $1M+. That's pure regulatory rent that the driver had to finance. What was the driver's share of the fare after medallion debt service?
- Post-Uber: no medallion, but Uber takes 25-40% of the fare + the driver covers gas, insurance, depreciation. Is the driver's effective share better or worse?
- This might be one of the few industries where you can see a clear before/after inflection point at a specific year (2012-2014).
