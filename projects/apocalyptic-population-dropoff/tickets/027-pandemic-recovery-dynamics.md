# Ticket 027: Pandemic — Recovery Dynamics and Stabilization

## What to research

After a catastrophic pandemic, the survivors have advantages that other scenarios don't: infrastructure is physically intact (just unstaffed), knowledge is preserved, and the cause of death is finite (the disease burns through and ends). How fast can society recover?

## Specific data needed

1. **Post-Black Death recovery:**
   - Europe lost 30-60% of population in 5 years
   - Recovery timeline: population returned to pre-plague levels by ~1500 (150 years)
   - BUT: economic conditions improved dramatically for survivors (labor scarcity → higher wages)
   - Social structure changes: feudalism weakened, urbanization increased

2. **Post-pandemic infrastructure restart:**
   - Unlike EMP/nuclear war, physical infrastructure survives. Power plants, water treatment, roads are intact
   - BUT they require skilled operators. How many survive? How quickly can they restart?
   - If 50% of skilled workers die, the survivors can restart systems designed for a full workforce
   - Estimated time to partial infrastructure restoration: weeks to months (vs. years for physical destruction)

3. **The "infrastructure advantage" of pandemic vs. other scenarios:**
   - Population drops to 50% but infrastructure capacity was designed for 100%
   - Survivors have excess capacity: housing, roads, factories, farmland
   - Food: if half the population dies but farmland is intact, per-capita food improves immediately
   - This is fundamentally different from nuclear war where infrastructure is physically destroyed

4. **Stabilization timeline:**
   - How quickly does mortality return to baseline after the pandemic ends?
   - Is there a secondary mortality spike? (Grief, PTSD, reduced social cohesion, reduced labor for agriculture)
   - When does the curve flatten?

5. **Post-pandemic population trajectory:**
   - Fertility changes: historical pandemics sometimes show a baby boom after (Black Death, 1918)
   - Immigration: would post-pandemic US attract immigration? (Unlike other scenarios, the US would still be habitable)
   - Long-term: when does population begin growing again?

## Where to look

1. **Benedictow — "The Black Death"** — post-plague recovery data
2. **Pamuk, Sevket** — economic effects of Black Death
3. **Alfani, Guido** — plague and inequality studies
4. **Historical demography journals** — post-pandemic fertility and population recovery
5. **FEMA / DHS** — infrastructure restart planning
6. **Engineering studies** — minimum staffing requirements for critical infrastructure

## Result format

Key Data Points:
- `infrastructure_restart_months` — time to partial restoration of power/water/communications
- `mortality_stabilization_months` — time until death rate returns to near-baseline
- `population_recovery_start_years` — when population begins growing (fertility > mortality)
- `time_to_pre_pandemic_population_years` — historical precedent for full recovery

## What good data looks like

- Clearly contrasts pandemic recovery with other scenarios (faster because infrastructure intact)
- Uses Black Death recovery as primary historical calibration
- Notes that modern pandemic recovery would likely be faster than Black Death (more knowledge preserved, infrastructure intact)
- The pandemic curve should flatten earlier than nuclear war or grid-down curves — probably by month 12-18 post-pandemic-end
