# English Replacing French as Diplomatic and Business Lingua Franca

French was THE language of international diplomacy for 300 years — the Treaty of Westphalia (1648) through the Treaty of Versailles (1919) were conducted in French. English's displacement of French as the world's lingua franca is one of the oldest measurable threads of Anglo-American cultural dominance.

**Metric:** Composite indicator tracking English vs. French as the language of international institutions, treaties, and business over time.

**Data proxy:** Language of international treaties (UN Treaty Series), official languages of international organizations, language requirements in multinational corporation job postings, international conference proceedings language.

**Interesting angles:**
- Treaty of Versailles (1919) was the first major treaty with an official English version alongside French — a symbolic inflection point.
- League of Nations used both; United Nations made English the working language from 1945.
- ICAO made English the mandatory language of international aviation (1951).
- International scientific publishing shifted from German and French to English post-WW2. Track % of scientific papers published in English per decade.
- Business lingua franca shift: when did "international business English" become a requirement? Track MBA program languages, corporate lingua franca policies.
- EU institutions: English became dominant working language despite Brexit. Percentage of EU documents originally drafted in English vs. French over time.
