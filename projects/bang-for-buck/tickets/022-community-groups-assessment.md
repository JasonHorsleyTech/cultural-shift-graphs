# Ticket 022: Community Group Participation / Social Prescribing — Evidence Assessment

## Objective

Determine whether there is sufficient evidence to include "joining a community group" or "social prescribing" as a data point. The UK NHS has invested in social prescribing and published some cost-effectiveness data — check if it's enough.

## What to research

1. **Social prescribing CEA data**
   - UK NHS social prescribing programs: any published QALY or $/QALY estimates?
   - These programs "prescribe" community activities: gardening groups, art classes, walking groups, volunteer networks
   - Search for evaluations of link worker / social prescriber programs

2. **Community participation and health outcomes**
   - Loneliness reduction programs: any QALY data?
   - Group-based activities and mortality (observational data — note confounding)
   - Social isolation as a health risk factor: what's the QALY burden of loneliness?

3. **Cost of community participation for Bob**
   - Many community groups are free or very low cost
   - Transportation is the main cost barrier
   - Time commitment: 1-4 hours/week typical

4. **Could we construct a defensible QALY estimate?**
   - If social prescribing evaluations report quality-of-life improvements (EQ-5D), those ARE QALYs
   - UK data would need adjustment for US context (different healthcare system, different community infrastructure)

## Decision criteria

**Include** if: at least one evaluation of social prescribing or community participation reports EQ-5D or QALY outcomes. Tier 2-3 evidence.

**Exclude** if: evidence is only observational with severe confounding (healthy people join groups, not: joining groups makes people healthy).

## Where to look

1. **NICE evidence base** — social prescribing
2. **NHS England** — social prescribing evaluations
3. **Tufts CEA Registry** — "social prescribing" or "community participation"
4. **PubMed** — "social prescribing QALY" or "social prescribing cost-effectiveness"
5. **What Works Centre for Wellbeing** (UK) — community programs evidence
6. **Holt-Lunstad et al.** — social isolation and mortality meta-analyses (for context, not for QALY estimates)

## Output format

If **include:** full intervention result format.
If **exclude:** evidence assessment format with verdict and rationale.
