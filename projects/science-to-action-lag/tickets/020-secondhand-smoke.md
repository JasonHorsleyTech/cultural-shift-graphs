# Ticket 020: Secondhand Smoke → Respiratory Disease / Cancer

## Objective

Research the timeline from scientific evidence that secondhand smoke (environmental tobacco smoke / ETS) causes disease in non-smokers to binding regulation restricting indoor smoking.

## What to research

1. **Early evidence date** — Hirayama (1981) published the first major epidemiological study linking secondhand smoke to lung cancer in non-smoking wives of smokers (Japan). Trichopoulos (1981) published similar findings from Greece. White & Froeb (1980) showed respiratory effects in non-smokers exposed to workplace ETS. What's the earliest credible evidence that ETS causes disease?

2. **Consensus date** — US Surgeon General's report on involuntary smoking (1986). EPA classification of ETS as a Group A (known human) carcinogen (1992). Which is the more appropriate consensus date?

3. **First OECD regulation** — The first comprehensive indoor smoking ban in any OECD jurisdiction? Ireland enacted a nationwide workplace smoking ban in 2004 (first country-wide ban). Earlier local/regional bans: California (1994 state law, 1998 bars). Norway and New Zealand also banned in 2004.

4. **US regulation** — No FEDERAL indoor smoking ban as of 2026. The regulatory story is at the STATE and LOCAL level. Pro-Children Act (1994) banned smoking in some federally funded buildings. Most action was state-level: California 1994-1998, New York City 2003, etc. How to handle the lack of federal action?

5. **Industry opposition** — The tobacco industry fought secondhand smoke science even harder than direct smoking science, because ETS evidence justified restrictions that affected ALL smokers (indoor bans), not just the individual smoker's choice. They funded junk studies, challenged the EPA classification in court, and lobbied extensively against indoor bans.

6. **50% exposure reduction** — CDC tracks cotinine levels (a biomarker for secondhand smoke exposure) in non-smokers. These have declined dramatically since the 1990s.

## Where to look

1. US Surgeon General's report on involuntary smoking (1986)
2. EPA risk assessment on ETS (1992)
3. Irish workplace smoking ban (Public Health (Tobacco) Acts, 2002-2004)
4. Hirayama (1981) — BMJ paper
5. CDC data on secondhand smoke exposure trends (cotinine levels)
6. Stanton Glantz, "The Cigarette Papers" — industry documents on ETS strategy
