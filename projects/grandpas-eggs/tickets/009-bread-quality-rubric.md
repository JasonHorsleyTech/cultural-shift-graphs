# Bread — Quality Rubric

## What to research

Define what a "loaf of bread" meant in ~1955 and what the quality-equivalent product is today. Build a multi-attribute quality rubric.

## Research tasks

1. **1955 baseline:** What was the standard loaf of bread? White bread was dominant — but what kind of white bread? What were the ingredients (flour, water, yeast, salt, sugar — vs. today's 20+ ingredient list)? How was it baked (local/regional bakeries vs. national brands)? What was the shelf life?

2. **Quality attributes to score (minimum 5):**
   - Ingredient count and composition (simple vs. preservatives, dough conditioners, high-fructose corn syrup)
   - Baking method (fresh-baked vs. industrial continuous-mix process)
   - Shelf life (shorter = fresher ingredients; longer = more preservatives)
   - Nutritional profile (fiber, protein, sugar content per slice)
   - Texture and crust quality (subjective but assessable via production method)
   - Source of flour (local mill vs. industrial commodity wheat)

3. **Modern quality-equivalent:** Is it artisanal bakery bread? A specific brand (Dave's Killer Bread, Pepperidge Farm)? Fresh-baked from a supermarket bakery section? What matches the 1955 experience of "a normal loaf of bread"?

4. **Divergence timeline:** When did industrial bread processing (Chorleywood process, continuous-mix) become dominant in the US? When did preservatives and dough conditioners become standard? The story of Wonder Bread and the industrialization of baking.

## Data source hierarchy

1. USDA/FDA historical regulations on bread composition (enrichment requirements, etc.)
2. Food history scholarship on bread industrialization
3. Industry sources (American Bakers Association)
4. Academic studies on bread ingredient evolution

## Output format

Follow the **Type 2: Quality rubric** format from plan.md.

## What "good data" looks like

- Acknowledges that 1955 bread had some genuine disadvantages (less consistent, shorter shelf life, less enrichment)
- Clearly identifies the industrialization timeline
- Distinguishes between "white bread" (what CPI tracks) and "quality-equivalent bread" (simple ingredients, bakery-style)
