# Ticket 011: Alternative Food Sources Post-Catastrophe

## What to research

Beyond conventional agriculture, what non-traditional food sources could supplement calories in a post-collapse or nuclear winter scenario? ALLFED (Alliance to Feed the Earth in Disasters) has done the most work here.

## Specific data needed

1. **Mushroom cultivation on dead trees/wood:**
   - Caloric potential if US forest biomass were partially converted to mushroom substrate
   - Timeframe to first harvest (weeks, not months)
   - Labor and knowledge requirements
   - ALLFED estimates for this pathway

2. **Seaweed / marine aquaculture:**
   - Current US seaweed production vs. potential
   - Caloric density of seaweed species
   - Scalability timeline
   - Viability under nuclear winter / impact winter conditions (reduced sunlight)

3. **Insects:**
   - Caloric efficiency of insect farming (crickets, mealworms)
   - Cultural barriers vs. actual nutritional value
   - Could this scale fast enough to matter in year 1?

4. **Wild game and fishing:**
   - Current US wild game populations (deer: ~30M, elk, wild boar, etc.)
   - How quickly would game populations collapse under pressure from 300M+ hungry people?
   - Fishing: freshwater and saltwater sustainable yield
   - Historical: how long did wild game last in previous frontier/survival situations?

5. **Leaf protein concentrate:**
   - ALLFED research on extracting protein from leaves/grass
   - Industrial requirements vs. improvised methods
   - Caloric contribution potential

6. **Stored non-food converted to food:**
   - Pet food (significant caloric reserve)
   - Animal feed (corn, soy in agricultural supply chain)
   - Cellulosic conversion (theoretical — wood to sugar)

7. **Total alternative food potential:**
   - Sum of all alternative sources: how many additional people could they feed?
   - In which scenarios do alternatives matter most? (Nuclear winter where conventional farming is impossible)

## Where to look

1. **ALLFED publications** — their entire research program is about this
2. **Denkenberger & Pearce — "Feeding Everyone No Matter What" (2015)**
3. **FAO** — wild game and fisheries data
4. **USDA** — animal feed stocks
5. **US Fish & Wildlife Service** — game population estimates
6. **Academic papers on alternative food in catastrophe scenarios**

## Result format

Key Data Points table. Each row is one alternative food source with:
- `caloric_potential_daily` — total calories per day if maximally exploited
- `people_feedable` — how many people that translates to at 1500 cal/day
- `ramp_up_time` — months until this source is producing at scale
- `viability_nuclear_winter` — yes/no/partial (works without sunlight?)

## What good data looks like

- ALLFED is the primary source — they've done the modeling
- Wild game depletion is realistic (30M deer ÷ 335M people = not many calories)
- Distinguishes between "theoretically possible" and "practically achievable in year 1 of a collapse"
- Total alternative food contribution is probably 10-30M people equivalent — not a replacement for agriculture, but a meaningful supplement
