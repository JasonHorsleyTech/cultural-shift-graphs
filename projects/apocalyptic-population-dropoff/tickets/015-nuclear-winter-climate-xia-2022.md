# Ticket 015: Nuclear Winter — Climate Modeling (Xia et al. 2022)

## What to research

The climate effects of a full-scale nuclear war: soot injection, temperature drop, precipitation changes, and duration. This is the foundational climate data that drives the famine modeling in ticket 016.

## Specific data needed

1. **Xia, Robock et al. (2022) — Nature Food:**
   - Soot injection scenario: 150 Tg (their largest scenario, representing US-Russia full exchange)
   - Global temperature change: year-by-year for 10+ years
   - US-specific temperature change (they model by latitude/region)
   - Precipitation changes
   - Solar radiation reduction (photosynthetically active radiation — PAR)
   - Duration: how many years until temperatures return to baseline?

2. **Comparison with other nuclear winter models:**
   - Robock et al. (2007) — earlier version of same group's work
   - Toon et al. (2007) — complementary modeling
   - Mills et al. (2014) — ozone depletion from nuclear war
   - How much variation exists between models? Do they converge?

3. **Reisner et al. (2018/2019) — Los Alamos (the counterpoint):**
   - Their fire modeling produces less soot reaching stratosphere
   - How much less? What temperature change do they predict?
   - Key methodological differences from Robock's group
   - (This feeds ticket 018 but basic comparison belongs here)

4. **UV radiation increase:**
   - Nuclear war depletes the ozone layer (Mills et al. 2014)
   - UV increase affects crop growth, human health, ecosystems
   - Quantify the UV increase and duration

5. **Ocean effects:**
   - Sea surface temperature changes
   - Marine ecosystem disruption timeline
   - Fish stock impact (directly relevant to food supply)

## Where to look

1. **Xia et al. (2022) — Nature Food** — primary source, including supplementary data
2. **Robock et al. (2007) — Journal of Geophysical Research**
3. **Toon et al. (2007) — Atmospheric Chemistry and Physics**
4. **Mills et al. (2014) — Earth's Future** — ozone and UV
5. **Reisner et al. (2018, 2019) — Journal of Geophysical Research**
6. **Coupe et al. (2019) — nuclear war ocean modeling**

## Result format

Key Data Points table with year-by-year climate parameters:

| parameter | value | range_low | range_high | unit | timeframe | source | confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Parameters: `temperature_change_c`, `par_reduction_pct`, `precipitation_change_pct`, `uv_increase_pct` — each for years 1, 2, 3, 5, 10.

## What good data looks like

- Xia et al. supplementary data provides the specific numbers (don't just cite "90% crop loss" — get the temperature/radiation curves that produce that estimate)
- Year-by-year data points, not just "nuclear winter lasts 5 years"
- Clear comparison between Xia/Robock (severe) and Reisner (moderate) predictions
- Notes the scientific debate status: is this a 50/50 split or does one side have stronger evidence?
