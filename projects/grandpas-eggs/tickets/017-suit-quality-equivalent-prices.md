# Men's Suit — Quality-Equivalent Prices 1950-2025

## What to research

Build a time series of the price for a **quality-equivalent men's suit** (matching the 1955 baseline as defined in results/015-suit-quality-rubric.md) from 1950 to 2025.

**Read results/015-suit-quality-rubric.md first.** If it doesn't exist yet, use "100% wool, half-canvas or full-canvas construction, made in a developed country" as the working definition.

## Target data points

Every 5-10 years from 1950 to 2025. This is harder data to find than the cheapest suit — quality-equivalent suit pricing requires matching specific construction/material specs across decades.

## Data source hierarchy

1. **Sears catalog archives** — For the 1950s-1970s, the Sears suit WAS the quality-equivalent. Look for the mid-range to upper-range suit listings with wool fabric and canvas construction. Track how that tier's price evolved.
2. **Brooks Brothers / traditional menswear retailers** — Consistent quality tier across decades. Brooks Brothers has been selling essentially the same product since the 1800s.
3. **Menswear industry publications and pricing surveys** — Post-2000 data on the "made well" tier.
4. **Current retail pricing** — Suitsupply, Brooks Brothers, J.Crew Ludlow, Hart Schaffner Marx for the modern equivalent. Confirm construction method and fabric to match the rubric.

## Important notes

- **Before ~1980, Line A and Line B may be close together.** When most suits were domestically made from wool, the cheapest suit and the quality-equivalent were not drastically different. The gap opened as cheap imports of fused poly-blend suits entered the market.
- **The suit story is about durability too.** If the rubric defines lifespan as an attribute, consider noting cost-per-year-of-wear as a supplementary metric (suit price / expected years of service). This doesn't change the primary data format but is useful context.
- **Brooks Brothers filed for bankruptcy in 2020** and was acquired. Their pricing and quality shifted. Use their historical pricing but note the discontinuity.

## Output format

Follow the **Type 3: Price data** format from plan.md. Unit = "suit (two-piece)".

## What "good data" looks like

- At least 8-10 data points spanning 1955-2025
- Product descriptions confirming the quality match (fabric, construction, origin)
- Clear documentation of when the quality-equivalent diverged from the cheapest
