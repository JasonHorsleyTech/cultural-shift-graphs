# Ticket 003: Asbestos → Mesothelioma / Lung Disease

## Objective

Research the timeline from scientific evidence that asbestos causes lung disease and mesothelioma to binding regulation banning or restricting asbestos use.

## What to research

1. **Early evidence date** — The Merewether & Price report to UK Parliament (1930) is often cited as the first definitive evidence. Earlier case reports exist (1900s-1920s). Find the earliest credible epidemiological evidence.

2. **Consensus date** — When did a major body formally state asbestos causes cancer? IARC classification, major government reports. The mesothelioma link (specifically to asbestos) was established by Wagner et al. in 1960.

3. **First OECD regulation** — The UK passed the first asbestos regulations in 1931 (Asbestos Industry Regulations), but these were workplace controls, not bans. First outright bans came much later: Iceland (1983), Norway (1984), Denmark (1986). Find the earliest MEANINGFUL regulation.

4. **US regulation** — EPA attempted a ban in 1989, overturned by the 5th Circuit Court in 1991. The US still has no comprehensive asbestos ban as of 2026 (the TSCA risk evaluation and restrictions came 2024). This is a key data point — one of the longest-lagging hazards.

5. **EU regulation** — EU-wide ban effective 2005. Individual member states earlier.

6. **Industry opposition** — The asbestos industry (Johns Manville, etc.) knew about health risks and covered them up for decades. This is one of the most well-documented cases of corporate concealment.

7. **50% exposure reduction** — Asbestos consumption peaked in the 1970s. When did US consumption drop to 50% of peak?

## Where to look

1. Merewether & Price report (1930) — UK National Archives
2. IARC Monographs on asbestos
3. Paul Brodeur, "Outrageous Misconduct" — detailed history of the asbestos industry cover-up
4. EPA regulatory history (1989 ban attempt, 1991 court reversal)
5. International Ban Asbestos Secretariat — tracks global regulatory status
6. Barry Castleman, "Asbestos: Medical and Legal Aspects" — comprehensive regulatory timeline

## What "good data" looks like

- Clear distinction between workplace safety regulations (1931 UK) and actual product bans (1980s-2000s)
- The US situation must be accurately characterized — no comprehensive ban, but increasing restrictions
- Industry opposition documented with specific companies and concealment strategies
- Both lung disease (asbestosis) and cancer (mesothelioma) evidence timelines — the cancer evidence came later than the fibrosis evidence

## Known pitfalls

- Asbestos regulation is a SPECTRUM from workplace dust limits (1931) to complete product bans (1983-2005). The choice of what counts as "first binding regulation" dramatically affects the lag calculation. Document the reasoning.
- The US is an extreme outlier — still no comprehensive ban 95+ years after the first evidence. This makes the "first OECD country" vs. "US" gap enormous.
- Different types of asbestos (chrysotile, amphibole) have different regulatory histories. Focus on the overall asbestos regulatory arc, not fiber-type specifics.
