# Movie Theater Worker — Cinema Exhibition

The theater is an interesting case because the "fundamental worker" is arguable. The projectionist used to be skilled labor. Now it's automated. The remaining workers are concession and ticket staff making minimum wage. But the customer's dollar goes to a complex chain: studio → distributor → exhibitor → staff.

**Fundamental worker:** Movie theater employee (ticket taker, concession worker, projectionist historically)
**Customer cost proxy:** Average movie ticket price (MPAA/NATO track this going back decades).

**Interesting angles:**
- Studios take 60-80% of ticket revenue in the first weeks. The theater makes almost nothing on the ticket itself — they survive on concession markup. This means the theater worker's ratio against ticket price is extremely low, but their ratio against concession revenue might be different.
- The projectionist used to be a union, skilled position. Automation eliminated it. Did ticket prices drop when the skilled worker was removed? (No.)
- Premium formats (IMAX, Dolby, recliner seats) charge 2-3x standard price. The workers are the same. Pure overhead/capital capture.
